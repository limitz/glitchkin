# Critique — Cycle 13
**Critic:** Nkechi Adeyemi — Target Audience and Emotional Resonance Specialist
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Subject:** *Luma & the Glitchkin* — Pitch Package Emotional Readiness Review
**Context:** Response to C12 B- ("not yet emotionally pitch-ready")

---

## Pitch Brief — Emotional Premise

**Score: 72 / B+** (was C, C12)

- The addition of "the kid who notices what no one else sees; Glitchkin need her to see them" is the specific interior sentence the C12 brief was missing. This now names Luma's wound and its structural answer in a single clause.
- The phrase "technically imaginary" in the tone section is doing real emotional work — it names the thing children feel about companions no one else can verify. This is the emotional engine of the show. It should be foregrounded, not tucked into the tone paragraph.
- "Sees the world differently than everyone around them" still reads as generic children's media boilerplate alongside the new specific language. It dilutes what is now a sharp brief. Cut it or replace it.

**Bottom line:** The brief has found its emotional premise; now it must trust it and cut the residual vagueness.

## Luma Expression Sheet — v007

**Score: 68 / C+** (was B-, C12)

- Six expressions remain: CURIOUS, DETERMINED, SURPRISED, WORRIED, DELIGHTED, FRUSTRATED. These are correct but generic. C12 asked for one expression specific to Luma — her quality of attention, a face that says *she notices what others miss*. That expression does not exist in v007. The proportion fix was correct and necessary; it did not address the emotional gap.
- WORRIED remains arms-crossed generic concern. FRUSTRATED remains the adult's idea of child frustration (arms folded, backward lean). Neither has aged up to 12. Neither has the social layer that C12 flagged.
- CURIOUS has the reaching arm, which is Luma's best expression. The forward lean and off-screen point are correct. But the spec still shows both brows raised symmetrically — pure surprise, not Luma's specific alertness.

**Bottom line:** Proportion is fixed; interiority is still absent — v007 is technically stronger but emotionally identical to v006.

## Byte Expression Sheet — v004

**Score: 78 / B+** (was A-, C12)

- RELUCTANT JOY asymmetric arm fix (arm_l_dy=-2 resisting the joy impulse) is the right direction and it is working.
- STORM/CRACKED arm asymmetry (arm_l_dy=6, arm_r_dy=22 — 16-unit differential) reads convincingly as physical damage, not dramatic pose. Children will understand this.
- **C12's unresolved request:** The unguarded warmth state — a moment where Byte trusts without fighting it — is still not present. The nine expressions run: NEUTRAL, GRUMPY, SEARCHING, ALARMED, RELUCTANT JOY, CONFUSED, POWERED DOWN, RESIGNED, STORM/CRACKED. Every positive emotional state is still defensive or reluctant. A child needs to know what Byte looks like when the fight is over and the guard is down. Without this, the friendship's emotional reward remains invisible.
- RESIGNED vs POWERED DOWN distinction is good. The design knows the difference between giving up and shutting down.

**Bottom line:** Strong technical emotional design, but the one missing state from C12 is still missing — Byte's warmth ceiling is "reluctant."

## Grandma Miri Expression Sheet — v003

**Score: 84 / A-** (maintained from C12)

- KNOWING STILLNESS is still the single most narratively sophisticated expression choice in the package. Nothing has changed there.
- The suppressed smile concern from C12 stands: a fully readable suppressed smile is a different thing from one that only reveals itself over time. If this expression is legible on first viewing, it undercuts the secret it is designed to hold. The team should ask: can a child on first screening name what Miri knows, or do they only feel that something is there?
- The WARM/WELCOMING to WISE/KNOWING posture arc provides a genuinely full supporting character emotional range. This is the most complete character documentation in the package.

**Bottom line:** KNOWING STILLNESS remains the benchmark for the whole package; be careful not to over-signal the secret.

## Glitch Expression Sheet — v003

**Score: 74 / B** (was C+, C12)

This is a significant improvement and I want to be direct about what has changed.

- YEARNING is the most important addition: body at rest, UV_PURPLE eyes, no confetti, no mouth. The stillness reads as interior. A child watching YEARNING will register something they may not be able to name — the specific longing of wanting something that belongs to someone else. This is the first time Glitch has felt like a character with a private life. The spec note "watching Luma have something Glitch cannot name — and wanting it" is exactly right.
- HOLLOW is brave. The deflated body, the empty-stare single white pixel in both eyes, the absence of confetti — this codes as the aftermath of desire, not as dramatic suffering. Children understand the hollow that comes after wanting something and not getting it. This is emotionally honest.
- COVETOUS is the weakest of the three new additions. The bilateral ACID_GREEN target-lock slit eyes read as predatory scheming rather than interior desire. It looks like a villain expression, not an interior state. The distinction between wanting and coveting is real, but COVETOUS as designed communicates "I will take this" rather than "I want this." That is a performance state, which is what C12 said Glitch did not need more of.
- The bilateral-eye innovation for these three states (YEARNING, COVETOUS, HOLLOW) is the right instinct — the design comment that "bilateral eyes = genuine feeling tell (breaks the destabilized-right-eye signature)" is sound reasoning. A child won't articulate the eye symmetry rule, but they will feel that these expressions are different in kind, not just degree.

**Bottom line:** YEARNING and HOLLOW have genuinely opened Glitch's interior life; COVETOUS slides back toward performance — revise it toward want, not threat.

## Style Frame 01 — Discovery (v004)

**Score: 72 / B** (was B, C12)

- The procedural pass (wobble polygons, variable stroke, add_face_lighting()) has improved visual warmth. The warm lamp from upper-left is correct — it reads as the analogue world Luma belongs to before the digital world intrudes.
- The face is still open-mouthed with full smile, blush active. C12 asked for uncertainty before the smile — the pause of a 12-year-old processing something impossible. The procedural pass has not addressed this. The face is still showing the resolution of the emotion (delight), not its beginning (stunned recognition).
- Ghost Byte alpha calibration at 90 is correct. The ghost figure still communicates "unusual screen artefact" more than "someone looking back." At commission-pitch scale, this matters.

**Bottom line:** Visual quality has improved; the face's emotional beat is still one step ahead of where discovery actually happens.

## Style Frame 02 — Glitch Storm (v005)

**Score: 69 / C+** (was B-, C12)

- Environment communicates danger effectively. The scale relationship (char_h = 18% of frame height) is correct — Luma is child-sized in a situation that is not child-sized.
- C12's central concern is unresolved: Luma's face in sprint still has no internal read. The hair stream, the CORRUPT_AMBER outline, the storm skin tint — all environmental. None of it is internal. A child watching this sprint cannot tell whether Luma is scared or decided. Those are different emotional experiences.
- The dutch angle (4°) is doing structural work for tension. The cold confetti dominance (DATA_BLUE 70%) is correct.
- This frame is the weakest in the package for emotional communication. It is the strongest for spectacle. For a pitch to a children's commissioning panel, that gap will be noticed.

**Bottom line:** The storm is impressive; Luma's interiority during it is absent — this remains the package's emotional gap.

## Style Frame 03 — The Other Side (v005)

**Score: 81 / B+** (was B+, C12)

- The UV_PURPLE_DARK saturation fix (31% → 72% sat) is confirmed correct. Deep void zones now read as digital void, not grey-purple ambiguity.
- The warm/cold collision — Luma's hoodie orange as a thermal anomaly in a cold world — continues to be the most conceptually sophisticated piece of visual storytelling in the package. A child will feel this without being able to name it.
- C12 asked for a focal event within the wonder — something Byte does in this environment that demonstrates belonging. Still absent. The frame is an impressive landscape with two figures standing in it. It is not yet a moment.
- Byte's teal body is visible and readable. Byte being seen in their own world is the minimum requirement; it is met.

**Bottom line:** Atmospheric and conceptually strong; it needs one gesture or action beat from Byte that converts landscape into moment.

## Style Frame 04 — Luma + Byte (v003)

**Score: 71 / B-** (was B, C12)

- The gaze divergence (Luma looking off-screen right, Byte looking up at Luma's face) is still the correct compositional instinct and it is the most emotionally legible relationship beat in the package.
- C12 asked for one gesture communicating *care, not just proximity*. The source generators are stubs — the original file is missing. I cannot verify whether the proximity-vs-relationship gap has been addressed. The SoW documents note this as a known risk.
- The dual lighting split (warm amber from window, cool monitor cyan from right) landing on both characters is the right visual metaphor. It places Luma at the intersection of both worlds. This is working.
- Byte's teal luminance at 60–70% of canonical is noted as an intentional dual-lighting decision. I accept this. What matters is whether Byte reads as a character at shoulder scale. From the spec, this remains the unresolved question.

**Bottom line:** The relationship composition is correct; without the original source, I cannot confirm whether the care-vs-proximity gap from C12 has been closed.

## Overall Assessment — Critique 13

**Overall emotional readiness: B / Approaching pitch-ready**

Progress since C12 is real and in the right places:
- The pitch brief now has its interior sentence (Luma's wound + its answer)
- Glitch has interior states for the first time (YEARNING, HOLLOW)
- The bilateral-eye innovation for Glitch's desire states is a genuine design insight

What remains unresolved from C12:
1. **Luma's expression sheet lacks her specific expression** — the one that shows a child who notices what others walk past. This is still the package's largest single gap.
2. **Byte's unguarded warmth state is still missing.** RELUCTANT JOY is the ceiling of Byte's openness. A child needs to know what it looks like when Byte stops fighting.
3. **SF02 shows Luma's body in action without Luma's face in feeling.** Sprint without interiority.
4. **SF04 source generators are lost** — the care-vs-proximity question cannot be answered without them.

The package is no longer the adventure show masquerading as an emotional show that it was in C12. Glitch and the brief have been genuinely improved. But the protagonist — Luma — still does not have her own expression on her own expression sheet. Until she does, this cannot be pitched as the show about the kid who sees the world differently. It can only be pitched as the show that talks about such a kid.
