# ZOE PARK — Audience Critique | Cycle 15
**Reviewer:** Zoe Park, age 11, Millbrook Elementary (5th grade)
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Subject:** Luma & the Glitchkin — full pitch review

---

## OVERALL SCORE: 78 / 100

---

## WHAT I LOOKED AT
Characters, the colored pictures, the storyboard panels, and the story summary.

---

## ISSUES

- **Luma looks kind of plain.** Her hoodie is cool (the pixel pattern is actually sick) but her face is the same in almost every picture — the expressions feel like the same face with eyebrows moved. I want to see her be chaotic. The story says she's reckless and fast but the drawings look like she's just... standing there being calm.

- **Byte is the best thing in this whole pitch and he's SIX INCHES TALL.** He's grumpy and has a cracked eye that glitches when he's pretending not to care — that's literally my favorite character concept I've ever heard. Every expression on his sheet is good. He is doing more work than Luma right now, which is a problem.

- **Cosmo is fine but he feels like a side character from a different show.** His thing is "anxious guy with a notebook" which I've seen before. The arms raised in panic face is funny though. He needs one weird thing that makes him actually HIS.

- **Grandma Miri is genuinely great.** The "knowing stillness" face where she KNOWS what's happening and just watches — that's actually scary in a good way. She feels like a real person not a grandma from a cartoon.

- **Glitch (the villain?) is way too small to be scary.** It's a tiny diamond shape with sparkles. Even with the "hollow" and "calculating" expressions it just looks like a forgotten Tamagotchi. The story says the Other Side is more frightening because it's TOO ORGANIZED — but the villain design isn't giving that. It's not giving anything. I don't feel threatened.

- **The "Other Side" style frame is ACTUALLY COOL.** The dark purple with floating rectangles everywhere — that looks like a level from a video game I'd actually want to play. That made me lean forward. More of that energy please.

- **The glitch storm style frame is confusing.** Two stick figures running in the dark. I didn't know who was who or what was happening without reading the notes. If I have to read the notes it already lost me.

- **The discovery scene (Luma and Byte at the CRT) is the best single image.** The big teal eye on the screen, the warm yellow room, Luma reaching — I actually got excited. That's the one that works.

- **The Miri/Miri thing (grandma and the glitch elder having the same name) is a genuinely good mystery.** I noticed it while reading and immediately thought "wait that's on purpose." That's exactly the kind of thing that makes you want episode 2.

- **The "Byte came through the CRT and now something is leaking" plot is fun.** It's clear, it moves fast, it makes sense. I didn't have to re-read anything. That's good.

---

## WOULD I WATCH IT?
Yes. Episode 1 for sure. I'd probably text my friend Maya about Byte before it was even over.

## WOULD I RECOMMEND IT TO MY GROUP CHAT?
Yes but only after Byte does something that makes me actually laugh. I need one moment. Give me one moment.

---

**Bottom line:** The story and Byte are carrying this pitch — the visuals need to catch up, especially Luma's energy and Glitch's threat level, because right now the show sounds better than it looks.
