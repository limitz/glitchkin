## Story / Pilot Episode Outline

Score: 82

- The cold open sequence actually hooks me. Like the part where Luma is asleep surrounded by junk food wrappers and her hair is eating a pillow? That is a real person. I know girls like that. That is not an adult writing "quirky teen" — that is just how people are when nobody is watching.
- "The flesh dimension." ok that was actually funny. Byte saying that like he is personally offended by having a body is the kind of joke that gets funnier every time you think about it. Fire line.
- The thing where Cosmo calls Byte "the entity" and Byte is immediately mad about it? That is genuinely hilarious. Their whole dynamic of not being able to talk to each other directly is SO good. That is the kind of bit you quote in the group chat.
- Luma reaching into the TV is cool but the "doubt-in-certainty beat" description reads like something a teacher wrote. In the actual show, if they hold that pause too long it is going to feel slow. Kids will check their phones. Keep it to like 2 seconds max of her hesitating.
- Grandma Miri saying "I thought that might be you" to Byte is SUCH a good twist. I literally went "wait WHAT" when I read that. That is how you make someone want episode 2.
- The social cost beat with Dev texting is lowkey the most real thing in the whole script. Not because it is dramatic but because it ISN'T. She just doesn't notice. That is how it actually works and most shows get that wrong.
- The stinger with Byte alone behind the TV saying "I should have mentioned this earlier" is perfect. Do NOT explain what he means. Ever. For as long as possible.

Bottom line: The story actually makes me want to watch this, which is rare because I can usually tell where a pilot is going by minute 3 and this one I could not.

## P18 — Notebook Doodles (NEW)

Score: 78

- Ok so the IDEA of this panel is really good. Luma was doodling Byte's face before she even knew what he was? That is the kind of detail that makes a character feel real. I do that. I draw stuff I see in my head before I know what it is.
- "not random. it LOOKED at me." and "what if a computer had feelings???" are exactly what someone would write in their notebook. The handwriting energy is correct.
- The hero doodle of the CRT with the face in the static is a nice touch — she drew it before she knew what it was. That lands.
- "grumpy. definitely grumpy" at the bottom with the little arrow is funny.
- The panel works as a story beat but it is very production-document-looking. When this is actually animated I need to FEEL like I am looking at a real kid's notebook, not a storyboard planning document. Right now it reads more like instructions for an artist than an actual frame of TV.

Bottom line: Good story beat, needs to feel messier and more personal when it is final art — a real kid's notebook is chaos, not organized.

## P17 — Beat of Stillness / Chip Falls

Score: 77

- The single pixel chip falling between them while everything else is still is a good visual gag. It is the kind of tiny joke that rewards paying attention.
- I like that they are at a distance from each other and neither one is moving closer. That feels real — two strangers deciding if they trust each other.
- Luma's "assessing" face looks a little too serious for her? She should look more curious than concerned. She is not worried about Byte. She is FASCINATED.
- "Negative space (their distance)" — I get what this is trying to do but in the actual show this needs to be a SHORT beat. Like 1.5 seconds. Kids do not sit in negative space.

Bottom line: Good emotional beat but keep it SHORT or it will lose the audience between the comedy of P16 and the comedy of P19.

## Character Designs — Lineup

Score: 74

- Luma looks cool. The hoodie with the pixel pattern is something I would actually want to wear. Her hair cloud is distinctive — I could draw her from memory after seeing her once, which is important.
- Byte looks like he has OPINIONS and I respect that. The cracked eye is a good character detail. He is cute but in an angry way which is the best kind of character design.
- Cosmo... is fine. He looks like a kid who makes lists. Which is accurate. But he does not look like someone I would pick as my favorite character. He needs one thing that is visually unexpected. The striped shirt and big glasses are giving "mom dressed me" which maybe is the point but it is a little boring.
- Grandma Miri reads as warm and solid which is correct. Her design says "I know things" without being mysterious about it.
- The Glitchkin on the right side of the lineup is cool — the diamond shape with the angry little eyes is a good villain design. It looks like if an error message had feelings.
- The lineup staging itself is small and hard to read at this size. The characters are tiny.

Bottom line: Luma and Byte carry the design language. Cosmo needs one visual hook that is not just "anxious kid in glasses."

## Character Expression Sheets

Score: 76

- Luma's expression range is good. CURIOUS and DETERMINED are her strongest — those feel like her. FRUSTRATED is also right. She looks like someone who is mad at the problem, not at herself.
- Byte's expressions are fire actually. GRUMPY vs RELUCTANT JOY vs UNGUARDED WARMTH tells you his whole arc in three faces. CONFUSED is funny — he looks personally attacked by not understanding something.
- Cosmo's expressions are solid but SKEPTICAL and WORRIED look too similar. I need to be able to tell the difference in half a second from across the room.
- Grandma Miri's KNOWING STILLNESS expression is exactly right. That is the face of someone who is waiting for you to figure it out.

Bottom line: Byte's expression sheet is the standout — every face tells a micro-story.

## Storyboard Sequence P03-P24 (Overall Flow)

Score: 79

- The escalation from P03 (single pixel) through P06 (Byte's face) to P08 (full reveal) is well-paced. It builds tension without being boring.
- P10 (Byte looking at sleeping Luma) with the sight line and warm/cool split is a lowkey beautiful frame. Even as a rough storyboard I can feel the mood.
- P11 (eyes closed, then SNAP open) is tense in a good way. The brow twitch before the snap is a detail that makes it feel not like a cartoon but like a real moment.
- P14 (bookshelf ricochet) is chaos in the best way. The multi-exposure trail showing Byte's trajectory is visually cool and reads as comedy immediately.
- P23 and P24 (Luma and Byte facing the chaos together) are cool hero shots but they are small and hard to read.
- The sequence from P14 to P19 is the strongest run. The comedy-tension-comedy rhythm actually works. P14 (chaos) → P15 (floor) → P16 (one eye, "WHAT") → P17 (stillness) → P18 (notebook) → P19 (first words). That is professional pacing.
- Missing panels (P20-P22) are a gap I can feel. Right now the sequence goes from their first conversation to suddenly ALL the monitors going crazy and I need to see that escalation happen step by step or it will feel like the show skipped a chapter.

Bottom line: The cold open is strong and the P14-P19 run is the best stretch in the whole pitch so far.

## Overall Pitch Assessment

Score: 78

- **Would I watch episode 2?** Yes. Genuinely yes. The Grandma Miri twist and Byte's stinger are two hooks and I only need one. I want to know what he should have mentioned. I want to know how Grandma Miri knows him. I want to see Cosmo finally see Byte. That is three reasons to come back and most shows give me zero.
- **Would I recommend this to my group chat?** I would send them P16 (the eye on the floor), the "flesh dimension" line, and the Cosmo/Byte "entity" exchange. Those are shareable moments. The show needs more of those per episode — at least 3-4 moments that are screenshot/quote material.
- **Is Luma actually cool?** Yes. She is cool because she is not trying to be cool. She is just paying attention and that makes her different. She feels like a person, not a character. I would not want to BE Luma (she is too chaotic) but I would want to be her friend, which is actually better for a protagonist.
- **Does anything feel like it is trying too hard?** The "doubt-in-certainty beat" description sounds like an essay question. In the actual show, that moment needs to be SHORT and visual, not a philosophical pause. Also some of the production doc language is very "adults explaining the emotional significance of things" which is fine for behind-the-scenes docs but if ANY of that tone leaks into the actual dialogue I will clock it immediately.
- **Is anything cringe?** The show's self-awareness about its own emotional architecture is actually its biggest risk. It knows EXACTLY what it is doing at every moment and that is powerful but also dangerous — if the characters ever feel like they are performing the theme instead of living it, the whole thing collapses. Right now the script walks that line well. Keep walking it carefully.
- **What is missing?** I need to see the Glitch Layer. All the cool stuff about impossible geometry and pixel-art plants and code waterfalls? I have read about it. I have not SEEN it. The pitch is all Real World right now. Show me the other side. That is where the show becomes something I cannot get anywhere else.

Bottom line: This is a show with a real heart, a funny voice, and characters I actually care about — which puts it ahead of 90% of the pilots I have seen. The missing piece is the Glitch Layer visuals. Show me the weird stuff. That is your hook for kids like me who have seen everything.
