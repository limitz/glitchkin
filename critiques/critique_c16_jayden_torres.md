All four style frames, character lineup, Luma and Glitch expression sheets, cold open storyboard contact sheet, Act 2 contact sheet, story bible, and the Glitch appearance guide.

## ASSET REVIEWS

---

### SF01 — "The Discovery" (LTG_COLOR_styleframe_discovery.png)
```
Score: 78
- Luma reaching toward the screen actually works — the open palm toward Byte
  sells the want without being corny. The brow asymmetry (wonder + doubt at once)
  is the best face she has anywhere in this pitch.
- The warm room vs cold CRT glow divide is doing real work here. This image
  has a mood. I stayed with it. That's rare.
Bottom line: This is the one image in the pitch that feels like a real show — everything else should be gunning for this.
```

---

### SF02 — "Glitch Storm" (LTG_COLOR_styleframe_glitch_storm.png)
```
Score: 51
- I can barely tell what's happening. Luma, Byte, Cosmo are all running
  in a storm of dark blue confetti and I don't know which one I'm supposed
  to care about. The Dutch angle is supposed to add chaos but it mostly adds
  confusion. No read on who's scared vs who's determined.
- The damaged storefront window is a nice detail but it's in the wrong corner
  for me to find it unless I'm zoomed in. At thumbnail scale this is just dark
  rectangles and blue dots. The QA warm/cool separation is only 8.5 — it shows.
Bottom line: The scene sounds exciting but the image isn't — three characters running sideways in the dark tells me nothing about stakes.
```

---

### SF03 — "The Other Side" (LTG_COLOR_styleframe_otherside.png)
```
Score: 82
- This image is actually sick. The floating geometry, the deep void, the purple
  atmosphere with cyan accents — it reads exactly like the "ordered precision is
  scarier than chaos" concept from the story bible. I didn't need to read the
  notes to feel that something was wrong in a bad way.
- Characters are tiny. I understand why (sense of scale) but I couldn't see
  Luma or Byte's faces at all. The image works as a world statement but not
  as a character moment. Those should be the same thing in a pilot pitch.
Bottom line: Best world-building image in the pitch; loses points because I can't see the characters I'm supposed to be worried about.
```

---

### SF04 — "The Dynamic" (LTG_COLOR_styleframe_luma_byte.png)
```
Score: 63
- The concept is right — I can see what they're going for. Luma and Byte
  together, the size difference, the dynamic between them. But the execution
  is flat. QA flagged warm/cool separation at 1.1 (FAIL) and it shows —
  the image has no atmosphere. It's just two characters on a background.
- Byte looks like he wants to be somewhere else, which tracks, but the
  image doesn't have any tension or energy. It's the "dynamic" between them
  and I don't feel the dynamic. Show me them mid-argument or mid-something.
  Two characters standing next to each other isn't a relationship.
Bottom line: The idea of this frame is good but the execution is a neutral greeting card — I need to feel the friction or the warmth between them, not just see them coexist.
```

---

### Character Lineup (LTG_CHAR_lineup.png)
```
Score: 70
- Glitch being the same size as everyone else in the lineup kills its menace
  before the show even starts. If it's the villain, it should be staged to feel
  wrong — hovering off the ground line, slightly too far from the group,
  something that makes you feel the difference before you know what it is.
- Luma's hoodie pixel pattern is genuinely cool design work and reads at this
  scale. Byte's size relative to everyone else is correct and funny without
  trying to be funny. That proportion gag works.
Bottom line: Solid functional lineup but Glitch should be visually separated from the rest — right now it just looks like a teammate, not a threat.
```

---

### Luma Expression Sheet (LTG_CHAR_luma_expressions.png)
```
Score: 54
- RPD silhouette test shows 9 FAILs and 9 WARNs — the QA tool is telling
  the same thing I'm seeing: too many expressions look like the same pose
  with a different face. A character who is supposedly reckless and kinetic
  should have BODY expressions, not just brow changes.
- THE NOTICING is actually a distinct expression — one eyebrow raised, squint
  on the other side, closed mouth. That one reads. I can tell something just
  changed for her. The other five feel like variations on "standing there being
  a 12-year-old." SURPRISED and FRUSTRATED in particular look nearly identical
  from across a room.
Bottom line: The face work is improving but the body poses are still way too similar — Luma should look like a different PERSON in each expression, not the same person making a different face.
```

---

### Glitch Expression Sheet (LTG_CHAR_glitch_expression_sheet.png)
```
Score: 79
- RPD silhouette test PASSED at 55.5% worst pair — Glitch's expressions are
  actually distinct in body language even before you see the face. That's
  exactly right for a character communicating through geometry.
- CALCULATING is genuinely unsettling in a good way. COVETOUS (bilateral acid
  slit eyes) is the creepiest thing in this whole pitch. That expression should
  be in a style frame, not buried on a character sheet.
- YEARNING and REACHING OUT are unexpected and interesting — the idea that a
  villain has interior desire is what separates a real antagonist from a plot
  obstacle. I notice it and it makes me curious.
Bottom line: Glitch's expression sheet is the most coherent character design in this pitch — the COVETOUS expression needs to be in front of people's faces as early as possible.
```

Score: 58

### Story — Premise, Tone, Character Arcs
```
Score: 88
- "The kid nobody sees" as protagonist premise is actually specific and true.
  Not "the chosen one." Not "the loser who gets powers." The kid who notices
  everything and nobody cares. That's real. I've been that kid.
- Byte's characterization is exactly right: grumpy, tiny, competent, cracked
  eye that glitches when he's pretending not to care. That is a real character.
  Specifically: the cracked eye as an involuntary honesty tell is one of the
  best character design ideas I've seen.
- Glitch communicating through geometry instead of speech is extremely cool.
  "It was already there" as a staging direction is how you write a villain that
  doesn't embarrass the audience for caring. That said — Episodes 1-4 showing
  only Corruption's ambient signature before Glitch appears is a long time to
  tease something. It better pay off with exactly the image I'm imagining.
- The Miri/Miri dual identity plant is the kind of thing that makes you trust
  a show's writing staff. They know what they're building toward.
Bottom line: The writing is doing more work than the visuals right now — which means when the visuals catch up, this is going to hit hard.
```

## OVERALL SCORE: 71 / 100

---

## WOULD I WATCH IT?

Yeah. Episode 1 for sure. The Glitch appearance in Episode 5 — the "it was already there" reveal — I genuinely want to see that done right and I think they can do it. Byte is the main reason.

## WOULD I RECOMMEND IT TO MY GROUP CHAT?

Not yet. I'd wait until I had Byte doing the cracked-eye glitch moment on video to show people. I need a clip, not a pitch deck.

---

## TOP PRIORITIES FROM A 13-YEAR-OLD WHO DOESN'T CARE ABOUT YOUR PRODUCTION SCHEDULE

1. **SF02 needs a reread.** I should be able to tell who's running and who's scared from across the room. Right now I can't.
2. **Luma's expression sheet bodies need work.** The story says she's kinetic. The drawings say she just found out class was canceled.
3. **Get COVETOUS into a style frame.** That expression is the most interesting villain visual in the whole pitch.
4. **SF04 needs atmosphere or stakes.** Two characters standing together is not a dynamic.
5. **The Glitch Layer in the storyboards should match the Glitch Layer in SF03.** Right now there are two different visual registers and it breaks the promise.

---

**Bottom line:** The writing is a full letter grade ahead of the visuals — Byte and Glitch's character design is genuinely strong, but the style frames except SF01 and SF03 are not landing, and Luma still doesn't move like the reckless 12-year-old the story says she is.
