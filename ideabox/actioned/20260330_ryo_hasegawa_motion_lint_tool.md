**Idea:** Build a `LTG_TOOL_motion_spec_lint_v001.py` that validates motion spec sheet PNGs against the beat-count and secondary-motion standards I've established. The tool would: (1) check that a motion sheet PNG has exactly the expected number of panels (3 or 4 depending on character); (2) sample annotation text zones for presence of beat-count numbers and timing labels (using simple pixel sampling to detect text vs blank regions); (3) verify the sheet is ≤1280px in both dimensions; (4) report PASS/WARN/FAIL. This is cheap (no Claude vision needed — just pixel occupancy checks) and would let QA confirm that motion spec sheets are populated, not blank, before they go to the critics.
**Benefits:** Prevents empty or malformed motion sheets slipping through to critique. Saves critic time (no need to visually confirm basic completeness). Can be integrated into `LTG_TOOL_precritique_qa_v001.py` as a new Section 8. Low build cost — follows same pattern as `render_qa_v001` and `expression_silhouette_v001`. All motion-spec producers benefit.
