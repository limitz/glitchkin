**Idea:** Build a small CLI tool (`LTG_TOOL_thumbnail_preview_v001.py`) that takes any PNG and outputs two side-by-side crops: (1) a 1280×720 full view, and (2) a 120×68 thumbnail simulation at 8x zoom. This would help us quickly verify whether subtle story-plant elements (like the diamond crystal in the living room) are visible at full size but invisible at thumbnail — without having to eyeball it manually or send the image to Claude. The tool could also mark the bounding box of a specified pixel region with a colored outline for easy reference.
**Benefits:** Any team member adding subtle details (Easter eggs, visual plants, background jokes) could instantly validate their work. Would reduce iteration cycles for this type of detail work. Also useful for critics checking whether flagged issues are visible at pitch-deck thumbnail scale.
