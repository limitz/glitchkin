**Idea:** When placing a GL-palette contamination artifact in a Real World scene (as in SF04's CORRUPT_AMBER CRT fringe), the contamination reads most convincingly at a chromatic transition zone — the edge where a cool or neutral fill meets a dark region. The alien warmth is felt as a wrongness because there's nothing in the environment that should be producing it. This "transition zone contamination" rule could be documented in image-rules.md or a GL-contamination spec: place contamination colors at zone boundaries, not inside zones. It distinguishes intentional narrative contamination from accidental palette drift.
**Benefits:** Helps Jordan, Rin, and Lee place future Glitch-residue details in a way that reads correctly to viewers even without explanation. Also gives Morgan Walsh and the CI tools a rule to verify: if a GL color appears INSIDE a Real World zone rather than at its edge, flag for review.
