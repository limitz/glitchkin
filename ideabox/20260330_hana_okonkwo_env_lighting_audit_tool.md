**Idea:** Build a lightweight environment lighting audit tool — `LTG_TOOL_env_lighting_audit.py` — that takes any ENV PNG and reports: (1) estimated dominant light direction (using per-quadrant brightness gradient, similar to sight_line_diagnostic's approach but for scenes rather than characters), (2) whether warm-light zones are in the upper half and cool-light zones in the lower half (the QA metric), (3) a per-zone value heatmap showing 3×3 grid of median values. This would help catch lighting setup errors before running full render_qa and would give a quick pass/fail preview on new environment generators before committing the structural code. Currently, diagnosing a warm/cool FAIL requires guessing which rows to adjust and re-running QA in a loop — the audit tool would shortcut that to a single inspection pass.
**Benefits:** All environment artists (currently just me, but future hires). Speeds up new environment generator development by roughly 30% based on my C41 experience (5 QA iterations on living room vs 2 on classroom with explicit zone planning). Also useful for critics reviewing environmental lighting — they could see a lighting summary alongside the PNG rather than inferring from visual inspection alone.
