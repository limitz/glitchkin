**Idea:** Establish a standard annotation format for SOUND CUE moments in storyboard panels. Currently sound cues are freeform text (e.g. "soft tick" in P17). With more panels containing timing-critical audio moments — the chip falling, the Glitch energy buzzing, Byte's first words — a consistent annotation style would help the director and sound team read the board faster. Proposed standard: yellow text box in-panel labelled [SFX: "soft tick"] or [DLG: "...WHAT."] with a small speaker or balloon glyph so the reader's eye finds it immediately. Could be added to the caption retrofit tool as an optional Tier 3b row for panels flagged as audio-critical.
**Benefits:** Speeds up pitch presentation — sound team and director both read audio cues from the board without verbal walkthrough. Also reduces the chance of misreading a dialogue annotation as a staging note.
