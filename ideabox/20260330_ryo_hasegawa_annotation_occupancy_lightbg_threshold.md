**Idea:** The `annotation_occupancy` check in `LTG_TOOL_motion_spec_lint.py` uses a fixed 15% brightness threshold to detect annotation content. This works for dark-background sheets (Byte) and image-heavy panels, but consistently WARNs on light-background sheets where annotations are rendered as small text on warm cream (248,244,238). The Cosmo motion sheet (all light-bg panels) and light Luma panels both hit this issue. Proposed fix: add a per-family `annotation_bg_color` field to `sheet_geometry_config.json` and use contrast-against-bg rather than absolute brightness. Alternatively, use a lower threshold for families with light backgrounds (e.g., detect avg panel brightness > 200 and apply a 5% threshold instead of 15%). Either approach would eliminate the false WARN pattern for text-only annotation panels on light backgrounds.
**Benefits:** Removes a persistent WARN from motion spec sheets for any light-background character (Cosmo, any future Real-World characters). Keeps annotation_occupancy check meaningful on dark-bg sheets (Byte) without polluting WARN counts for light-bg sheets. Resolves the asymmetry between Byte (dark panel, genuine detection challenge) and Cosmo/Luma (light panel, false positive pattern).
