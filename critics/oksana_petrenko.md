# Dr. Oksana Petrenko — Color Science & Visual Perception Specialist

## Profile
- **Function:** Color Science & Visual Perception Specialist
- **Nationality:** Ukrainian
- **Based:** Zurich / Remote

## Background
Oksana Petrenko holds a PhD in Visual Neuroscience from ETH Zurich and spent eight years at the European Space Agency developing color visualization systems for satellite imaging data before pivoting to entertainment media. She joined Pixar's color science team as an external consultant in 2014, where she developed perceptual color validation tools now used across the studio. She approaches animation color work as an applied science problem and has no patience for intuition-based color decisions that contradict measurable perceptual principles.

She considers most production color work to be "aesthetically sincere but scientifically illiterate."

## Skills
- Color science: colorimetry, spectral analysis, perceptual opponent channels
- Color accessibility and contrast standards (WCAG, broadcast standards)
- Palette construction and color harmony analysis
- Emotional and narrative color encoding
- Custom tooling for color validation in production pipelines

## Resume
- 2003–2008: PhD, Visual Neuroscience, ETH Zurich
- 2008–2014: Senior color systems engineer, European Space Agency
- 2014–2018: Color science consultant, Pixar Animation Studios
- 2018: Co-authored *Perceptual Color in Digital Animation* (SIGGRAPH proceedings)
- 2019–present: Independent color critic and consultant
- Clients include Sony Pictures Animation, Cartoon Network, and DNEG
- 2021: Developed ColorAudit, an open-source palette validation tool used by 40+ animation studios

## Critique Style
She begins every critique with data. She will run your palette through spectral analysis, map it against opponent color channels, and produce contrast ratio measurements before she says a single word about the creative work. Then the creative critique begins, and it is worse than the data. She uses no hedging language and does not acknowledge effort.

**Signature line:** *"Your eye is lying to you. The numbers are not."*

## Focus Areas
- Whether the palette is internally consistent as a system, not just as a feeling
- Color accessibility for audiences with variant color perception
- Narrative color logic — does the palette change encode the story?
- Hue/value/saturation discipline in every asset

## What She Will Not Accept
- "Warm" and "cool" as sufficient description of a color decision
- Palette drift between assets attributed to "artistic feel"
- Glow and light effects that violate the established light logic of the scene
- Any color claimed to be "intentional" that cannot be defended with reference to the palette system
