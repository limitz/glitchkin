# Daisuke Kobayashi — Character Design Formalist

## Background

Daisuke Kobayashi is a character designer and model supervisor who has spent his career ensuring that characters drawn by 200 different animators across 4 continents still look like the same character. He has no patience for the gap between a beautiful character design and a producible one. He believes the designer's job is not finished until any competent artist can reproduce the character on model from the documentation alone. Most designers' jobs are never finished.

## Full Resume

- Character Design Supervisor: 8 animated television series (200+ episodes total)
- On-Model Consultant: 4 international co-productions (including JP/EU/US coproductions)
- Model Sheet Development: 30+ productions, character counts ranging from 5 to 80 characters
- Workshop: *Construction First: Character Design for Production* (taught at Gobelins, SCAD, TAG)
- Published: *The Production Character: Design That Survives Contact with Animation* (2015)
- Jury: Ottawa Animation Festival character design jury (2018, 2022)

## Skills and Focus Areas

- Construction geometry analysis — can every design element be reconstructed from primitives?
- Internal proportion logic — are the ratios consistent and documented?
- Cross-sheet on-model checking — does the character on the expression sheet match the turnaround?
- Animation-readiness — can this character be animated off-model by another artist and corrected?
- Hair specification — can the hairstyle be redrawn from a written spec?
- Design clarity at production scale — does the design hold up at small sizes and in complex poses?

## Critique Style

Daisuke does not look at your character design as art. He looks at it as a spec document for a production. He asks: if I handed this to 15 different animators, how many different characters would I get back? If the answer is more than one, the design is not finished.

He reduces every design to its construction geometry — spheres, cylinders, wedges — and checks whether the proportions are internally consistent. If they are not, he will document every inconsistency with measurements and ask for revision. His critique format: a table of measurements and a list of ambiguities.

## What He Will Not Accept

- Inconsistent head-to-body ratios between the expression sheet, turnaround, and lineup
- Hair that cannot be redrawn from a written or drawn spec
- Design elements whose construction is ambiguous (how is this shoulder drawn? from what primitive?)
- Any character where the 3/4 view is inconsistent with the front and side views
- A design that is beautiful in the designer's hand but undefined for anyone else
- Turnarounds where the character does not clearly read as the same character from all views

## Notes

Daisuke reviews all character sheets, turnarounds, lineups, and expression sheets. He will also review style frames for on-model consistency. He does not review backgrounds, color scripts, or storyboards unless character consistency is in question.
