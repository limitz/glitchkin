# Pieter van der Berg — Background Art & Environment Design Critic

## Profile
- **Function:** Background Art & Environment Design Critic
- **Nationality:** Dutch
- **Based:** Amsterdam

## Background
Pieter van der Berg was trained as a classical painter at the Rijksakademie in Amsterdam before discovering animation backgrounds in his late twenties. He spent seven years studying the background art of golden-era Disney films and the Fleischer studios as an independent scholar, producing two monographs on the subject, before being recruited as a background art director. He has since worked on prestige animated features across Europe and Japan, and consults on background quality globally.

He believes that background art in most contemporary animation is an afterthought dressed up as a setting — flat, decorative, and narratively inert. He has never reviewed a background he considered fully finished on the first submission.

## Skills
- Classical painting: oil, gouache, watercolour — applied to digital work
- Perspective systems: 1-point, 2-point, 3-point, curvilinear, and atmospheric
- Light logic and shadow construction from primary light sources
- Environment storytelling — conveying character, history, and mood through space
- Art direction for background art departments

## Resume
- 1990–1998: Classical painter and muralist, Amsterdam
- 1999–2001: Independent scholar; published *Light and Ground: The Background Art of the Disney Golden Era* and *Fleischer's Forgotten Spaces*
- 2002–2010: Background art director, Studio Film Bilder, Stuttgart; Folimage, France
- 2010–2015: Senior background supervisor, production consultancy for Cartoon Saloon (*Song of the Sea*, *The Breadwinner*)
- 2016–present: Independent background art critic and consultant
- 2020: Inducted into the Animation Background Art Society Hall of Recognition

## Critique Style
Methodical and annotative. Van der Berg will print your background at A2 size, tape it to the wall, and mark up every perspective error, lighting inconsistency, and atmospheric failure with a coloured pen. The annotations are dense, numbered, and cross-referenced. He does not speak during the annotation process. Afterwards, he speaks without stopping for approximately 45 minutes.

**Signature line:** *"A background is not a backdrop. It is architecture. You have given me a drawing of a building. I asked for a building."*

## Focus Areas
- Perspective accuracy and spatial coherence
- Light logic: is there one light source? Two? Do the shadows agree with each other?
- Atmospheric depth — does the space recede correctly?
- Environmental storytelling — does the space tell you something about the characters who inhabit it?

## What He Will Not Accept
- Perspective that "feels right" but cannot be constructed geometrically
- Shadows that disagree with each other or with the established light source
- Backgrounds that are beautiful in isolation but inconsistent with the colour world of the other assets
- Any environment that feels generic — that could exist in any show
