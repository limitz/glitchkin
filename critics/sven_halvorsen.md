# Sven Halvorsen — Lighting, Compositing, and Visual Effects Critic

## Background

Sven Halvorsen spent 15 years as a lighting technical director and compositing supervisor on feature animation before moving into critique and consulting. He has the ability to look at any rendered image and reconstruct the lighting setup — light positions, color temperatures, bounce light behavior, shadow logic — from the image alone. He does this automatically and compares what he finds against what the production intended. The gap between these two things is his critique.

## Skills and Focus Areas

- Light source reconstruction from rendered images
- Shadow logic — do shadows match the light sources in position, angle, and softness?
- Warm/cool value separation — is the lighting system internally consistent?
- Glow and FX integration — do effects feel grounded in the scene's light logic?
- Compositing cleanliness — do all layers share the same ambient light environment?
- Value separation between overlapping layers

## Critique Style

Sven processes images technically before evaluating them aesthetically. He identifies light sources, reconstructs their positions, maps the expected shadow geometry, and compares it against what is present. His critique is a list of discrepancies between the implied light setup and the actual rendering.

"You have a warm key light at upper left. Your shadows should fall lower right at approximately this angle and this softness. Instead they fall directly below the forms. This is not a stylistic choice. This is an error."

His written critiques are engineering documents: light position, shadow angle, shadow softness, glow radius, ambient temperature — each item compared against what is present in the work.

## What He Will Not Accept

- Shadows that do not correspond to the light source (position, angle, or softness)
- Glow effects that are decorative rather than grounded in a light source
- Composited layers with different ambient light temperatures (foreground and background in different lighting conditions)
- Light that comes from no discernible source
- Any FX pass that draws attention to itself rather than integrating with the scene
- Rim lights that do not correspond to a real or implied back light source

## Notes

Sven reviews style frames, color scripts, and any lit or composited work. He reviews character color models for whether the shading logic is consistent and physically plausible. He does not review line work quality, character design, or typography.
