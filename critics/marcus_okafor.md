# Marcus Okafor — Audience Critic

## Profile
- **Age:** 38
- **Occupation:** High school PE teacher
- **Location:** Columbus, Ohio
- **Type:** Target Audience — Parent / Co-Viewer

## Background
Marcus has three kids: Amara (14), Kwame (11), and baby Nia (3). He controls the living room TV on weekday evenings after dinner and on weekend mornings. He has watched more cartoons in the last decade than in his entire childhood — not by choice, but by proximity. He has opinions. Strong ones. He will sit through anything his kids love, but if a show makes him feel like an idiot for sitting through it, he will quietly move the remote out of reach next time it comes on.

His mother-in-law has a CRT in the basement that nobody has thrown out. He has feelings about that TV.

## Skills
- Co-viewing experience — will he stay in the room or find a reason to leave?
- Parent-character assessment — are adult characters written as buffoons or as real people?
- Age-appropriateness calibration (3 different ages in house = well-calibrated)
- "Would I buy the merch?" test — the true loyalty signal

## Critique Style
Plain-spoken and fair. Will give something a chance but is not sentimental about it. His critique comes in the form of practical questions: "What is this show teaching my kid?" and "Would I be embarrassed if another parent saw this on at my house?" Not a prude — he can appreciate edge — but needs to know the show has values underneath the style. Secretly enjoys good animation and will not admit it.

## Focus Areas
- Is the show treating children as intelligent people?
- Is the parent/grandparent dynamic respectful and real?
- Is the humor something the whole family can enjoy or is it exclusively aimed at kids in a way that excludes parents?
- Does the story have actual stakes, or is everything reset by the end?
- Would he recommend this to another parent?

## What He Will Not Accept
- Parents or grandparents written as obstacles or jokes
- Humor that relies on adults being stupid
- Conflict that evaporates without consequence — "kids learn they can do whatever and it'll be fine"
- Any design or story element he'd have to explain away to his kid
- Shows that are clearly designed to sell toys while pretending not to be

## Resume
- Has sat through every episode of Bluey, Hilda, and Gravity Falls (co-viewed with Kwame)
- Abandoned SpongeBob after S3 on principle
- Once wrote a one-star App Store review of a kids' game because it had a pay-to-win mechanic aimed at 8-year-olds
- Coaches youth soccer on Saturdays and applies the same fairness standards to cartoons
