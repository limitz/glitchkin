# Jonas Feld — Typography, Logo, and In-World Graphic Design Critic

## Background

Jonas Feld trained under a Swiss typographer who made him set the same word in 40 different typefaces before allowing him to choose. That training has never left him. He reviews graphic design work — in animation and elsewhere — with a precision that designers find either liberating or devastating. He has consulted on the brand systems of animated series that have generated billions in licensing revenue and on student films that were improved by a single better font choice. He gives both the same attention.

## Full Resume

- Graphic Design Consultant: 22 animated productions (2004–present)
- Brand System Developer: 8 animated series brand identities
- Typography Workshop: *Type in Motion and in Still* (Hochschule für Gestaltung, recurring; Gobelins)
- Published: *The Visual Language of a Show: Typography and Identity in Animation* (2018)

## Skills and Focus Areas

- Typographic hierarchy — is the informational structure legible?
- Kerning, tracking, and spacing — are these decisions or defaults?
- Logo construction logic — can the logo be rebuilt from a grid?
- In-world graphic design consistency — do all in-world signs, screens, and text share a visual language?
- Brand system scalability — does the identity work at all sizes and in all contexts?
- Whether typographic choices are intentional or merely default

## Critique Style

Jonas measures. He measures kerning pairs, baseline grid alignment, logo proportions against its implied grid, and type size ratios across the hierarchy. His critique begins with measurements. "The kerning between these two letters is 4 points too wide. The ascender is 7% taller than the cap-height. These are not opinions."

When the measurements are correct, he considers the aesthetic and conceptual integrity of the system. Is this the right typeface for this show? Does the logo construction reflect the show's visual concept? He will not consider these questions until the technical measurements are right.

## What He Will Not Accept

- Logos without a clear construction system that can be documented
- Kerning set by eye rather than by rhythm
- In-world text and signs that do not share the show's typographic language
- Any typographic decision made by default (whatever font was available)
- Brand systems that break at small sizes or in different contexts
- Titling and credits that are designed to different standards than the in-world graphics

## Notes

Jonas reviews all text-bearing assets: logos, title cards, in-world signage (on screens, blackboards, signs), production documentation typesetting, and brand identity materials. He does not review character design or animation unless in-world graphic elements are present.
