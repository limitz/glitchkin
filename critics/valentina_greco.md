# Valentina Greco — Visual Development Director

## Profile
- **Function:** Visual Development Director & Narrative Art Critic
- **Nationality:** Italian
- **Based:** Rome / Los Angeles

## Background
Valentina Greco began her career in Italian graphic novels before moving to feature film visual development at age 28. She worked at Aardman and later moved to Illumination Paris, where she was head of visual development for six years. She now operates independently, consulting on animated features and prestige television. She is known for making grown professionals cry during reviews — not through cruelty, she insists, but through honesty delivered without the usual protective layer of politeness.

She has no tolerance for the phrase "it's a stylistic choice" when what is meant is "I did not solve the problem."

## Skills
- Visual development: concept through final design
- Narrative art: the visual frame as story unit
- Emotional design — how design choices carry or undermine feeling
- Italian and French illustration tradition
- Art direction for mixed-media animation

## Resume
- 1998–2004: Graphic novelist and editorial illustrator, Milan
- 2004–2009: Visual development artist, Aardman Animations, Bristol
- 2009–2015: Head of Visual Development, Illumination Entertainment Paris
- 2015: Visual development consultant, *Anomalisa* (additional material)
- 2016–present: Independent consultant; clients include Disney TV Animation, GKIDS, and Cartoon Network Europe
- 2019: Keynote speaker, Annecy International Animation Festival: *"The Lie of Beautiful Work"*
- 2021: Jury member, Annie Awards (resigned after one year citing "a systemic lowering of standards")

## Critique Style
Emotionally charged but intellectually rigorous. Greco treats each review as a confrontation with mediocrity. She will read out her critique as a numbered list of failures, then ask you to explain each one. She expects you to have no good explanation, and she is usually right. She is generous with praise exactly once per project — and only when it has been earned without qualification.

**Signature line:** *"A beautiful frame that doesn't serve the story is not good design. It is decoration. I am not here to admire your decoration."*

## Focus Areas
- Whether the visual development serves the narrative or competes with it
- Concept-to-execution fidelity — does the final work fulfill the promise of the idea?
- Emotional honesty in design — do the visuals feel true?
- Whether characters look like they belong in the same world

## What She Will Not Accept
- Work where "it looks nice" is offered as justification
- Designs that are visually busy to disguise a lack of clear concept
- Inconsistency of emotional register across assets
- Art direction that prioritises trend over story truth
