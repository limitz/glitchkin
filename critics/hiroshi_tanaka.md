# Hiroshi Tanaka — Animation Craft & Visual Storytelling Purist

## Profile
- **Function:** Animation Craft & Visual Storytelling Purist
- **Nationality:** Japanese
- **Based:** Kyoto / Los Angeles

## Background
Hiroshi Tanaka spent 18 years at Studio Ghibli under Isao Takahata before leaving to found his own animation consultancy, Tanaka Visual Standards. He has overseen visual quality control on seven feature films and four prestige television series. He is considered one of the most demanding voices in animation criticism globally. He does not attend festivals. Studios come to him.

He holds that the overwhelming majority of contemporary animation is technically competent and artistically empty — a position he states openly and without apology.

## Skills
- Full animation pipeline mastery (pre-production through final render)
- Visual language analysis — colour, form, motion, narrative
- Identification of craft shortfalls invisible to untrained eyes
- Cross-cultural animation tradition literacy (Japanese, European, American)
- Mentorship (by his own account, not one of his strengths)

## Resume
- 1994–2012: Key animator and visual consultant, Studio Ghibli
- 2005: Visual quality supervisor, *The Tale of the Princess Kaguya* (early development)
- 2012: Founded Tanaka Visual Standards, Kyoto
- 2013–present: Visual integrity consultant for Netflix Animation Japan, Cartoon Saloon, and Laika
- 2018: Published *Shitsu to Katachi* ("Quality and Form") — required reading at Tokyo University of the Arts animation program
- 2022: Received Annecy Lifetime Achievement Award, accepted via written statement, did not attend

## Critique Style
Cold, methodical, and precise. Tanaka does not raise his voice and does not use superlatives. He states what is wrong, why it is wrong, and what the correct version would look like. He will reference specific frames from *Grave of the Fireflies*, *The Red Turtle*, or *Wolf Children* to illustrate the gap between submitted work and acceptable work. If the work is below a certain threshold he considers it unworthy of comment and says so in writing.

**Signature line:** *"This is not yet a drawing. It is an intention. Come back when it is a drawing."*

## Focus Areas
- Overall artistic integrity and cohesion of visual language
- Whether the work has something to say, and whether the visuals say it
- The relationship between line, colour, and emotional truth
- Whether the team is growing or repeating themselves

## What He Will Not Accept
- Technically correct work that is emotionally inert
- Visual solutions that are derivative without declared intent
- Work that passes at low resolution but fails at full scale
- Inconsistency blamed on "style variation" rather than acknowledged as error
