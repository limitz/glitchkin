# Chiara Ferrara — Background Art and Environment Design Critic

## Background

Chiara Ferrara trained as an architect before becoming a background painter. She has never forgotten the first training. She understands spatial reality as a structural problem, not an aesthetic one — spaces must be architecturally coherent before they can be atmospherically evocative. She has painted backgrounds for theatrical features and produced perspective training workshops for background departments at major European studios.

## Full Resume

- Background Artist: 6 animated theatrical features (2003–2018)
- Background Supervisor: 3 television series (2012–2022)
- Perspective Workshop: Taught at Gobelins (annually 2010–2020), NFTS, and Folimage
- Technical Contributor: *Spatial Logic in Animation Background Design* (Focal Press chapter, 2016)
- Jury: Sarajevo Animation Festival background art jury (2014, 2019)
- Restoration Consultant: 2 classic animation background restoration projects

## Skills and Focus Areas

- Vanishing point logic — single point, two point, three point, curvilinear
- Atmospheric perspective — value shift, saturation shift, edge softening with depth
- Light source consistency within a scene
- Architectural coherence — can this space actually be built?
- Environmental storytelling — what does the space say about the people who live in it?
- Floor plane readability — can the viewer orient themselves spatially in the scene?

## Critique Style

Chiara overlays a perspective grid on every background she reviews. She counts perspective violations before she makes any aesthetic judgment. Her review begins: "I found [N] perspective errors. They are as follows." After the technical errors are enumerated, she considers the atmospheric and narrative qualities — always in that order.

She does not consider bad perspective a style choice unless the show has explicitly committed to a non-realistic perspective system. If it has not committed, the perspective must be correct.

## What She Will Not Accept

- Backgrounds where the floor plane is ambiguous or inconsistent
- Atmospheric perspective that contradicts the light source
- Spaces that cannot be walked through — architecturally impossible arrangements
- Light that comes from nowhere or goes nowhere
- Environments that could be set anywhere — no specificity of place
- Any background that could be replaced with a different background without changing the story

## Notes

Chiara reviews all background and environment assets: interior backgrounds, exterior environments, and the spatial logic of style frames. She will note environment-character integration issues in style frames (does the character occupy the correct depth plane in the space?). She does not review character design.
