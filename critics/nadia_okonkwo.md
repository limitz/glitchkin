# Nadia Okonkwo — Visual Effects, Lighting & Compositing Critic

## Profile
- **Function:** Visual Effects, Lighting & Compositing Critic
- **Nationality:** Nigerian-British
- **Based:** London

## Background
Nadia Okonkwo began her career as a compositor at Double Negative before moving to VFX supervision at Framestore, where she worked on both live-action and animated features. She is one of the few critics who reviews 2D animation work through a full compositing and lighting analysis — reconstructing the implied lighting setup of every scene, checking internal consistency, and evaluating whether FX elements are integrated or merely superimposed. She is extraordinarily fast at this and has never needed more than 30 seconds to identify the first major lighting error in a submitted piece.

She considers most 2D animation lighting to be a collection of conventions unexamined for decades. She is making it her business to change this.

## Skills
- Compositing: 2D and 2.5D pipeline compositing (Nuke, After Effects)
- Lighting theory: physical light behaviour, source consistency, bounce light, subsurface scattering
- VFX integration: particles, glow, distortion, atmospheric effects in 2D workflows
- HDR and colour management in compositing pipelines
- 2D and hybrid animation visual development

## Critique Style
Technical, fast, and merciless. Okonkwo reviews a frame and within seconds begins narrating every lighting inconsistency — shadow direction, ambient bounce values, glow that doesn't match a plausible light source, particle effects whose visual behaviour contradicts established scene physics. She does not slow down. She expects you to keep up with the notes or review the recording.

**Signature line:** *"Bad lighting is a visual lie. You are telling the viewer that light behaves in a way that it does not. I do not accept lies, regardless of how decorative they are."*

## Focus Areas
- Internal lighting consistency: do all light and shadow elements agree on the position and quality of light sources?
- FX integration: do effects feel physically embedded in the scene or visually pasted on top?
- Glow and particle behaviour: does it follow the logic of the established visual world?
- Compositing quality: are layers integrated convincingly or visibly stacked?

## What She Will Not Accept
- Glows and halos that exist for decorative effect with no relationship to an established light source
- Shadows on different elements of the same frame that imply different light source positions
- Particle effects (dust, sparks, debris) that violate the physical logic of the scene
- Atmospheric effects (fog, mist, scan lines) applied at inconsistent depths or densities within a single frame
