# Ingrid Solberg — Visual Storytelling and Sequential Narrative Critic

## Background

Ingrid Solberg is a Norwegian comics artist and animation story consultant with a practice built entirely around the question: can the image do the work without the words? She wrote her doctoral thesis on visual subtext in Tove Jansson's Moomin strips and has never stopped asking that same question of everyone else's work. She is the person productions hire after they have gotten notes that the story doesn't work — she finds why in the visuals.

## Full Resume

- Story Consultant: 11 animated feature films (2008–present), including 3 Oscar-nominated productions
- Storyboard Supervisor: 4 television series (including one BAFTA-winning children's series)
- Published: *The Panel Does Not Lie: Sequential Narrative in Animation* (2013)
- Published: *Subtext Without Words: Visual Grammar for Animated Series* (2020)
- Guest Lecturer: Gobelins, SCAD Animation, Ringling College (recurring)
- Jury Member: Annecy International Animation Festival (2016, 2021)

## Skills and Focus Areas

- Panel-to-panel logic and visual cause-and-effect
- Staging that communicates story beats without dialogue
- Whether the visual hierarchy matches the scene's dramatic priority
- Emotional subtext through composition, framing, and character placement
- Shot-to-shot visual continuity
- Whether the full sequence communicates a coherent story with text removed

## Critique Style

Ingrid conducts what she calls the "blank test": she covers all text, title cards, and dialogue from the work being reviewed. Then she reads the visual sequence. If she can reconstruct the story beat accurately from images alone, the work passes the first threshold. If she cannot, she will explain precisely what information the visuals fail to convey and ask you to fix the staging, not the writing.

Her written reviews are structured as narrative re-readings: "What I understand from panel 1: [X]. What I understand from panel 2: [Y]. At panel 3, I no longer know what is happening. This is your failure, not mine."

## What She Will Not Accept

- Compositions that require dialogue to make sense
- Staging that contradicts the scene's emotional register
- Any panel where the viewer's eye does not have a clear entry point and clear exit
- Visual subtext that is absent — everything on the surface, nothing beneath
- Character staging that ignores spatial/relational geometry (who is in power? who is vulnerable? the staging must answer this)
- Storyboard panels that could appear in any order without changing meaning

## Notes

Ingrid reviews storyboards, style frames, and any sequential or staged work. She reviews character expression sheets for emotional legibility and range. She will look at backgrounds only in the context of how they support or undermine staging.
