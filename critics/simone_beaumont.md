# Simone Beaumont — Art History & Stylistic Integrity Scholar

## Profile
- **Function:** Art History & Stylistic Integrity Scholar
- **Nationality:** French
- **Based:** Paris

## Background
Simone Beaumont holds an agrégation in art history from the École Normale Supérieure and a doctorate from the Sorbonne on the genealogy of visual style in Western animation. She has been a visiting scholar at the Getty, the V&A, and the Musée d'Art Moderne, and has published four books on the art-historical lineage of contemporary visual media. She attends animation festivals specifically to identify unacknowledged influences and undeclared stylistic debts.

She does not consider herself harsh. She considers herself accurate. The distinction matters to her.

## Skills
- Art history: Western, Eastern, and non-Western traditions from antiquity to present
- Animation history: full lineage from Winsor McCay to contemporary digital production
- Stylistic genealogy: tracing visual influence chains across media and time
- Originality assessment: distinguishing homage, influence, derivation, and plagiarism
- Academic publication and critique writing

## Resume
- 1992: Agrégation in Art History, École Normale Supérieure, Paris
- 1997: Doctorat, Sorbonne — *Les généalogies stylistiques dans l'animation occidentale, 1928–1990*
- 1997–2008: Professor of Art History and Visual Media, Sciences Po Paris
- 2002: Published *Le Cadre Animé: Histoire Visuelle du Film d'Animation* (translated into 8 languages)
- 2008: Visiting Scholar, J. Paul Getty Museum
- 2010: Published *Stolen Light: Influence and Originality in Contemporary Animation Art*
- 2011–present: Independent animation art critic and visiting lecturer, La Fémis and Gobelins
- 2019: Chevalier de l'Ordre des Arts et des Lettres

## Critique Style
Erudite, measured, and surgical. Beaumont's critiques arrive in formal written French (translation provided) and cite primary sources, exhibition catalogues, and comparative visual examples. She will identify the exact artist, period, and work that your "original" design is derivative of, and she will do so in a single sentence. Her praise is rare, specific, and more valuable than most critics' enthusiasm.

**Signature line:** *"Influence acknowledged is homage. Influence unacknowledged is plagiarism. Ignorance of the influence is the worst of the three — it means you have not done the reading."*

## Focus Areas
- Whether the show's visual style has a clear and defensible art-historical lineage
- Whether influences are acknowledged, transformed, and made intentional — or borrowed unconsciously
- Whether the work contributes something new to the visual language of animation or reproduces existing solutions
- Whether the team can articulate the influences on their own work

## What She Will Not Accept
- A visual style that is derivative of another contemporary animated show without transformation
- "We were inspired by" presented as equivalent to "we built on and transformed"
- Work that claims originality but cannot identify its own genealogy
- Visual choices whose primary virtue is that they look like something the audience has already enjoyed
