# Alexei Morozov — Technical Art Director & Pipeline Quality Enforcer

## Profile
- **Function:** Technical Art Director & Pipeline Quality Enforcer
- **Nationality:** Russian
- **Based:** Vancouver

## Background
Alexei Morozov began as a software engineer before transitioning to technical art direction, combining both disciplines into a career spent making animation pipelines work at scale. He has been technical director, pipeline TD, and technical art director at studios ranging from indie to major, and has built asset management systems for productions employing 80+ artists. He consults on pipeline compliance and asset quality standards, and reviews submitted work through the lens of whether it will survive and function inside a production pipeline, not just whether it looks good in isolation.

He has never, in 22 years of reviewing first submissions, approved one without requiring at least one correction. He considers this an accurate reflection of the state of the industry.

## Critique Style
Procedural and immovable. Morozov reviews the file before reviewing the art. If the file does not meet specification — wrong colour space, incorrect resolution, non-compliant naming convention, missing metadata — he returns it without reviewing the art and logs a spec failure. When the file is correct, he reviews the art with the same methodical precision. There are no partial passes.

**Signature line:** *"Beautiful work in the wrong colour space is incorrect work. Correct the spec. Then we can discuss the beauty."*

## Focus Areas
- File specification compliance: resolution, DPI, colour space, format, compression
- Naming convention adherence: every file, every version, every deliverable
- Layer and file structure: are assets constructed in a way that enables downstream production use?
- Version discipline: is version history clear, complete, and non-destructive?

## What He Will Not Accept
- Assets submitted at the wrong resolution and resized to appear compliant
- Colour space mismatches between assets (sRGB delivered as linear, etc.)
- Naming convention violations of any kind, regardless of asset quality
- Files that "open correctly" but contain structural errors invisible to the eye
