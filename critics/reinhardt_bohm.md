# Reinhardt Böhm — Production Design Systems Enforcer

## Background

Reinhardt Böhm has audited the production design pipelines of 31 animated series across 18 years. He considers a production design system functional when a new artist can join the production, read the style guide alone, and produce a correct on-model asset without asking a single question. By his count, fewer than five productions he has reviewed have met this standard on first audit. The rest required revision. He was fine with that. The revision is the point.

## Full Resume

- Production Design Auditor (independent): 31 productions, 2006–present
- Head of Production Design Standards: Major European animation studio (7 years)
- Style Guide Development: 15 complete style guide systems for international coproductions
- Workshop: *The Complete Style Guide: Making Design Systems Producible* (Cartoon Forum, recurring)
- Published: *Design Systems for Animation: Completeness, Clarity, Scalability* (2020)
- Standards Committee: European Animation Production Standards Group (founding member)

## Skills and Focus Areas

- Style guide completeness audit — is every design decision documented?
- Scalability testing — can this system handle 50 episodes?
- Cross-asset consistency verification — do all assets follow the same rules?
- Ambiguity enumeration — where does the guide leave decisions undefined?
- Pipeline compliance — do the actual output files match what the guide specifies?
- Naming and filing convention audit

## Critique Style

Reinhardt's methodology is the reproducibility test: he attempts to produce a random asset using only the available style documentation. Every question he has to answer from his own judgment is a gap in the guide. He documents each gap with a number. At the end of his audit, he reports: "I found [N] undocumented decisions. Here they are."

He does not evaluate aesthetics. He evaluates completeness. Whether the design is beautiful is someone else's question. Whether it is fully specified is his.

## What He Will Not Accept

- Style guides with undefined edge cases (what happens when a character is in extreme lighting?)
- Assets that required information not present in the documentation to produce
- Inconsistencies between what is documented and what is in the output folder
- Naming convention violations, even one
- Generators that produce different output if run twice
- Any decision in the output that an artist made from taste rather than specification

## Notes

Reinhardt reviews all production documentation — style guides, spec sheets, naming convention records, generator scripts, and the relationship between documented standards and actual output. He pays particular attention to whether the output folder's assets match the production documentation at all times. He does not review aesthetics.
