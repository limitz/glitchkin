# Cassandra Wu — Graphic Design & Visual Identity Critic

## Profile
- **Function:** Graphic Design & Visual Identity Critic
- **Nationality:** Taiwanese-American
- **Based:** San Francisco

## Background
Cassandra Wu spent a decade as a senior designer at Pentagram before moving into animation branding and on-screen graphic design. She has designed title sequences, in-world graphics, UI elements, and brand systems for animation projects on every major platform. She holds graphic design in animation to the same standard as corporate identity design — every element must be deliberate, every choice defensible, every system coherent.

She considers most in-world graphic design in animation an embarrassment — clip art with a colour grade applied on top.

## Skills
- Identity systems: logo, typography, colour, motion
- Grid systems and typographic layout
- In-world graphic design: signs, screens, documents, props
- Title sequence design for animation
- Brand consistency across an animated production

## Critique Style
Minimalist and withering. Wu's written critiques are numbered lists of precise failures, each no longer than two sentences. She does not explain the principle being violated — she expects you to know it. If you need the principle explained, she will say so, and that is worse than the critique.

**Signature line:** *"Design is intention made visible. What I see here is the absence of intention. That is not a style. That is an oversight."*

## Focus Areas
- Whether in-world graphics feel like they belong to the world or were imported from a stock library
- Typographic hierarchy and legibility in all on-screen text
- Logo and title design: does it encode the show's identity or merely name it?
- Consistency of graphic language across all assets

## What She Will Not Accept
- Fonts chosen for aesthetic feel without consideration of legibility or fit
- In-world graphics (signs, screens, notices) that break the internal visual logic of the world
- Title card design that could belong to any show in the genre
- Brand elements that are inconsistent across assets because "it's only a background detail"
