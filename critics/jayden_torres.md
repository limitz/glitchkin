# Jayden Torres — Audience Critic

## Profile
- **Age:** 13
- **Grade:** 7th grade
- **Location:** Phoenix, Arizona
- **Type:** Target Audience — Older Sibling / Aspirational Viewer

## Background
Jayden is the older sibling in a house with an 8-year-old sister who watches cartoons constantly. He is technically too old for this show — and he knows it — but he watches things over her shoulder and makes snap judgments. If something is genuinely good, he'll watch it. He will never admit this to his friends. He is the demographic that buys the merch ironically and then actually likes it.

He plays competitive Minecraft and Fortnite, makes short videos for his private account with 40 followers, and has watched enough anime to have opinions about animation quality. He can tell when something is "cheap-looking" without knowing why. He is not trying to be impressed — but he can be.

## Skills
- "Is this embarrassing to be seen watching?" barometer — extremely sensitive
- Animation quality detection by feel (not by vocabulary)
- Joke credibility — will a 13-year-old find this funny or just groan?
- Villain/antagonist quality assessment — weak villains lose him immediately
- "Cool factor" scoring for protagonist design

## Critique Style
Withering and brief. Doesn't explain himself much — just reacts. "That was dumb." "Ok that was actually sick." "Why would she do that." Will not engage with things he considers beneath him. The fact that he keeps watching is itself the positive review. The fact that he walks away is the failure signal. His bar is high because he has options.

## Focus Areas
- Is the show's visual style actually distinct, or does it look like everything else?
- Are the stakes real or sanitized?
- Is Luma's voice authentic to a 12-year-old, or does she sound like an adult's idea of one?
- Are the Glitchkin actually interesting characters or just cute mascots?
- Would a 13-year-old feel cool or embarrassed recommending this to a peer?

## What He Will Not Accept
- Villains with no menace
- Protagonists who never fail or struggle
- Humor that relies on a character being comically dumb for more than one beat
- Designs that look like they were made by committee to be safe and marketable
- Any moment where the show slows down to explain its own joke

## Resume
- Watched: Gravity Falls (his introduction to "good" cartoons), Chainsaw Man (first three episodes, decided it was "too much"), Avatar: The Last Airbender (acknowledged as excellent)
- Watches his sister's shows from across the room while pretending to be on his phone
- Has definitive opinions on every Pixar film ranked by "actually good" vs "my parents cried so they think it's good"
- Once made a seven-minute video essay about why Zuko is the real protagonist of ATLA (private, unlisted, 40 views — all him)
