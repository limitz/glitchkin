## 1. CHARACTER OVERVIEW

Miriam "Miri" Okonkwo-Santos is the warm, sharp, former-software-engineer grandmother who raised Luma after Luma's parents left. She is the emotional center of Luma's home life and, by extension, one of the show's primary sources of grounding and warmth. She does not appear in every episode, but when she does, the audience exhales slightly — because Miri is the feeling of being safe.

She is in her mid-70s. She was precise and technical for most of her career. She built systems for a living — large, complex, reliable systems — and the habits of that work (meticulous attention to detail, patience with complex problems, the refusal to panic) are still visible in her. But she has relaxed into the life she's living now. She's done proving things. She's in the "doing things because I want to" phase, and it suits her.

**Her core design truth:** She looks like she belongs in her den. The den is an extension of her — layered, warm, organized in a way that only makes sense when you understand the logic behind it. She is the kind of person whose house tells you everything about who she is before she says a word.

**Her relationship to Luma:** She is everything Luma will eventually be — audacious and warm and completely herself — and everything Luma is trying to learn from — patient, rooted, knowing when to act and when to wait. The design must communicate their connection (visual rhymes) and their difference (the contrast between energy and stillness).

## 2. SHAPE LANGUAGE

### Primary Shapes
- **Head:** A rounded circle — but warmer and slightly more compressed vertically than Luma's. Where Luma's circle is 95% circular (pure energy), Miri's head is **88% circular** — gently compressed, like a circle that has settled comfortably. She has the roundness of someone whose face has relaxed into itself over decades. The jaw is soft and clearly present — not pointed, not wide, just a gentle curved jaw that communicates someone who has decided who she is and is fine with it.
- **Torso:** A **weathered rectangle** — upright, solid, but with rounded corners that are more generous than Cosmo's precise angles. Think of a well-worn wooden block that's been handled so long the sharp edges have gone soft. Her torso is wider than Cosmo's and shorter — she is not gangly. She is planted. The torso reads as a foundation, not a tower.
- **Arms:** Soft cylinders — not tapered aggressively (she is not kinetic like Luma), not rigidly straight (she is not contained like Cosmo). They have a natural comfortable bend at the elbow in default pose — the arms of someone whose hands are always slightly ready to make something or hold something.
- **Legs:** Short and solid. Similar proportions to Luma's legs — the family resemblance. But where Luma's legs are toy-figure columns (identical width top to bottom), Miri's legs have a slight, gentle tapering toward the ankle. She has walked a long way on these legs. They know what they're doing.
- **Feet:** Slightly smaller relative to body than Luma's — not oversized, just solid flat ovals. She plants her feet. She does not duck-walk; she stands with feet parallel or slightly outward, but not exaggeratedly so.
- **Hands:** The most expressive element after her face. Miri's hands have the graphic suggestion of knuckle detail — an extra curved line across each hand indicating the knuckle row. Just one line, but it communicates years of use, craft, and gentle age. Not wrinkled dramatically — graphically suggested. These are working hands.

### Rationale
Circles communicate safety and warmth — they are Luma's dominant shape too, and this is the visual family connection. But Miri's circles have been modified by life: slightly more compressed, slightly less perfectly spherical. She is the origin point of the family's circularity.

The weathered rectangle torso is the key contrast with the pure circle: she has structure, history, solidity. Where Luma's torso is a pillow (all rounded), Miri's is a beam. Not harsh — the corners are generous — but she has a fundamental orderliness to her that she and Cosmo share, though hers came from lived engineering rather than anxiety.

Circles (warmth, safety, welcome) + weathered rectangles (history, precision, groundedness) = someone who was once more angular and has softened into the form of their best self.

## 3. PROPORTIONS (Head Units)

**Total height: 3.2 heads**

She is shorter than everyone — shorter than Luma (3.5 heads), much shorter than Cosmo (4.0 heads). She is compact and present. Her shortness reads not as diminishment but as density: she contains a great deal in a small space.

| Body Section | Measurement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Head | 1.0 head | Baseline — slightly wider than Luma's relative to the body |
| Neck | 0.08 heads | Short neck, head sits close to the body. She does not have the slight extension Cosmo has. |
| Torso | 1.05 heads | Slightly wider than her head (1.2x head width). Solid and present. |
| Upper legs | 0.55 heads | Shorter than Luma's proportionally — she is not built for running |
| Lower legs + feet | 0.52 heads | Stable footing |
| **Total** | **3.2 heads** | |

**Shoulder width:** 1.1x head width — slightly wider than her head. This is unusual for the cast; Luma and Cosmo are both shoulder-width equal to or narrower than head. Miri's slightly wider shoulders give her a settled, broad-based feeling of stability. She is not wide — she is rooted.

**Eye size:** Each eye is approximately 0.16x head width. Smaller than Luma's (0.22x) — she does not have the large, wondering eyes of youth. But they are lively: the eyes contain significant expressive information and are placed with intention in the lower half of her face.

**Hair volume adds approximately 0.25 heads** above the skull — she has natural gray hair worn in a soft updo that adds modest height. Not Luma's explosive cloud, but not Cosmo's flat cap either.

## 4. FACIAL CONSTRUCTION

### Head Shape
A gently compressed circle. The jaw is present and soft — slightly wider than Luma's minimal-jaw construction, but not pronounced. Miri has a face that has settled. The most important facial feature is the distribution of her expressions: where Luma's expressions travel from brows to mouth and back in rapid cycles, Miri's expressions are calmer — the changes are often in the eyes first, then spread outward. She is a reader; her eyes show information before her face announces it.

**Crow's feet:** Two small curved lines at the outer corner of each eye — fine, single-weight lines at 50% of the silhouette line weight. They are the only explicit aging mark on her face. They read as laugh lines, not as tiredness. They are always present.

**Smile lines:** A very subtle pair of curved lines running from the outer edge of her nose to the outer corner of her mouth — present in her resting face, deepening in smiles. Drawn at 40% line weight. They are the record of every smile she has ever given.

### Eyes
- **Shape:** Rounded almond shapes — slightly wider and more horizontal than Luma's rounded rectangles. They are the eyes of someone who looks steadily at things before speaking.
- **Iris:** **Deep Warm Amber (#8B5E3C)** — darker and richer than Luma's Warm Amber (#C87D3E). The same amber family, but deeper. This is where Luma's eye color comes from.
- **Pupil:** **Near-Black Espresso (#1A0F0A)** — same as Luma's hair, deep and warm.
- **Highlight dot:** Static White (#F0F0F0), upper-left (same position as Luma's). The shared highlight position is a subtle visual DNA marker.
- **Eye white:** **Warm Cream (#FAF0DC)** — consistent with the show.
- **Eyelid:** The upper eyelid has a slight heaviness — not drooping, but weighted. The arc is lower and flatter than Luma's, reflecting an eyelid that has been open for decades. It reads as "calm" rather than "tired." The lower lid has more curve than Luma's — the slight "smile crinkle" lower lid.

### Eyebrows
- Softer and thinner than Luma's thick graphic brows. They are not precise and "drawn" like Cosmo's — they have a natural, slightly irregular quality. In neutral they form a very gentle arch with a slight inward slope — the brow of someone perpetually slightly amused.
- **Color: Warm Gray (#8A7A70)** — the gray of hair that was once dark brown. The warm undertone prevents them from reading as cold or severe.
- Width: 0.22x head width. Narrower than Luma's 0.3x — less graphic weight in the brow, more in the eye itself.

### Nose
More defined than Luma's apostrophe. A soft button nose with a clear bridge line in 3/4 view. The nostrils are slightly indicated — two small flared curves at the base. This gives her face a slight maturity without being imposing. Front view: a rounded arc with two small curves at the base. This nose is the most detailed nose in the main cast.

### Mouth
- In neutral, a gentle upward curve — slightly fuller and wider than Luma's neutral. She defaults to "about to say something kind" rather than "about to smile." There is a distinction: Luma's default reads as kinetic (about to move), Miri's reads as warm (about to engage).
- Her closed smile is her most frequent expression: a wide, closed-mouth upward arc that fully presses the cheek forms. It reads as "I have seen a lot of things and most of them were fine, and some were wonderful, and you are in the second category."
- She shows teeth less than Luma — her big laugh expression does show them, but her default emotional range operates in the closed-mouth or slightly parted register.
- The corners of her mouth have a natural slight upturn even at complete neutral — not a Mona Lisa, but a baseline warmth.

### Cheeks
- Miri's cheeks have a permanent, very slight blush — not the situational blush of Luma, but a constant warm tone on the cheeks as if she is always slightly flushed with pleasure or mild exertion. **#D4956B** — slightly deeper and more saturated than her base skin, applied at 25% opacity feel. It is always present.
- In genuine joy, the blush deepens and spreads upward, pressing the eyes into crinkles.

## 5. HAIR

### Description
Miri's hair is predominantly silver-white with occasional warm gray undertones — her hair in youth was the same deep dark brown as Luma's hair, and traces of the warmth survive in the shadow tones. She wears her hair in a soft updo — a textured bun on the back-upper head, not rigidly pinned, with several strands that have escaped and frame her face (she stopped fighting these years ago). Two or three curls hang at the side of her face, especially near her ears. This is the echo of Luma's escaping ringlets — same tendency, different scale, different phase of life.

### Structure
- **Main mass:** A soft rounded bun sitting at the upper-back of her head. It adds 0.25 heads of height and does not extend significantly wider than the head.
- **Loose wisps:** Three or four fine strands or small curls at the temples and ears. These are soft, not flyaway-wild like Luma's — they are the relaxed version of Luma's chaotic hair energy. They always escape. She no longer minds.
- **Part:** A soft center-right part, visible where the hair pulls back from the forehead.

### Den Cardigan (Primary Garment)
Miri's signature garment is a large, chunky-knit cardigan that she wears in her den. It is the visual anchor of her home presence — the garment so associated with her that it functions the way Luma's hoodie does: as a wearable identity.

| Area | Color Name | Hex | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base | Warm Terracotta Rust | #B85C38 | A deep, muted terracotta — burnt and rich. Not orange-bright like Luma's hoodie; this is the mature, settled version of that warm spectrum. It reads as the color of a well-loved thing. |
| Shadow | Deep Rust | #8A3C1C | In the folds and deep cable-knit grooves. |
| Highlight | Dusty Apricot | #D4825A | On the raised cable-knit ridges and the shoulders. |

**Cardigan construction:** The cardigan is oversized — it falls to mid-thigh and the shoulders drop slightly. It has textured cable-knit indicated by vertical pairs of slightly-offset curved lines on the body of the garment. Not heavy detail — three or four cable lines per body panel, suggesting the texture without overworking it. The cardigan always has a slight V-neck visible at the collar, showing the top of whatever she's wearing underneath (typically a soft high-necked undershirt in **Warm Cream #FAF0DC**).

**Color relationship to Luma:** The terracotta rust of Miri's cardigan is a deep, settled version of Luma's warm orange hoodie. They are in the same warm family — both running hot, both expressive, both too large for strict propriety — but Luma's orange is morning-bright and Miri's rust is afternoon-golden. When they are in frame together, these two garments create an immediate warm family unit against the cooler world.

**Why slippers:** Miri is almost exclusively seen in her den or in the home. The slippers communicate "this is her domain" — she is relaxed and at home in a way that other characters are not. Outside scenes (rare) would show her in low, sturdy shoes in **Deep Rust (#8A3C1C)** — matching her cardigan.

### Line Work
- **Silhouette lines:** **Deep Cocoa (#3B2820)** — the canonical show-wide line color, matching Luma and Cosmo. This is the locked specification confirmed against the style guide in Cycle 3. Previous drafts referenced Warm Dark Brown (#4A2810) — that value is SUPERSEDED. All character lineup and model sheet documents should use #3B2820 for Miri's silhouette lines. The slightly warmer perception of her edges is achieved through her warm fill colors and slightly reduced line weight, not through a different line color.
- **Internal lines (knit texture, crow's feet, smile lines):** Deep Cocoa (#3B2820) at 50% weight.
- **Hand knuckle lines:** Single curved line at 40% weight — subtle.

### Dual-Blush Rule — Miri and Luma in the Same Frame

When Miri appears in the same frame as Luma in an excited state, the following disambiguation rule applies:

**The "Pride Override":**
Miri's permanent cheek blush fades to **30% opacity** in any frame where Luma's excited-state blush is active. This is a character tell — Miri is suppressing her own blush to look composed in the face of Luma's enthusiasm. She is proud. She is not going to show it quite yet.

- Miri's permanent blush at full presence: **#D4956B at 25% opacity feel** (as specified in Section 4 and Section 6)
- Miri's blush during Pride Override: **#D4956B at 30% of that 25% = approximately 7.5% opacity feel** (a barely-visible warmth, almost fully suppressed)
- Production note: The Pride Override does NOT change Miri's expression or posture. It is only the cheek blush that fades. Her face otherwise reads the same. The suppression is subtle — the audience should sense she's pleased before they identify why she looks slightly different.
- The Pride Override activates on the SAME FRAME as Luma's excited blush appears. No delay.
- The Pride Override deactivates (blush returns to normal) 8 frames after Luma's excited blush is no longer active.
- **Why it matters compositionally:** Without this rule, a frame containing Miri + excited Luma has two active blush systems of different intensities competing. The Pride Override ensures Miri's permanent blush does not flatten against Luma's situational blush, and ensures each blush system communicates a different emotional layer: Luma's blush = action/excitement; Miri's (suppressed) blush = private pride.

## 7. CLOTHING CONSTRUCTION — DETAILS

### The Den Cardigan
The dominant garment. Oversized, textured, deeply worn-in.

**Cable-knit indication:** Two to four vertical cable lines per front panel — pairs of slightly-raised curved lines that suggest the texture. In medium shots, visible and characteristic. In wide shots, they simplify to a slightly rough silhouette edge on the cardigan rather than individual detail lines.

**Fold behavior:** Miri's cardigan folds generously where Luma's hoodie has minimal, fresh folds. The oversized knit fabric creates 3-4 deep, rounded folds at the elbow when her arm is bent, and a soft horizontal roll at the waist where the cardigan has settled into its shape after years of wear. These folds are not chaotic — they are patterned, repeating, predictable. She knows exactly where her cardigan folds.

**Pocket use:** She keeps things in her pockets. The two front pockets are large enough to hold tools, remote controls, a small notebook, anything. The pockets hang slightly weighted — they are not empty in most scenes. What's in them is a character detail left to individual episodes.

### The Undershirt
A simple, clean high-necked shirt in **Warm Cream (#FAF0DC)** — barely visible at the collar of the cardigan. In close-up, a small simple collar edge is visible. The shirt is never the focus. It is a canvas layer under the cardigan.

## 8. THE DEN — Visual Context

Miri IS her den. The design should communicate this connection. When drawing Miri in her environment, note:

**The den color palette echoes her:** warm terracotta, warm tan, warm cream, deep warm brown, sage green. These are her colors. The den is her body language writ large in the environment.

**Objects that belong to her:**
- **A large, low-tech desktop computer:** from a previous generation. Miri's engineering background, present. She keeps it running. She has modified it in ways that would confuse any current technician.
- **Bookshelves organized by category but labeled in a personal shorthand:** Not alphabetical, not by color. By some system that makes complete sense when you know her.
- **A mug, always present, always the same mug** — Deep Terracotta (#8A3C1C) base with a small Warm Cream (#FAF0DC) handle. The mug is not named but is always in her hand or on a surface nearby.
- **Circuit board fragments and old tech components:** Not displayed as trophies — kept in ceramic dishes like other people keep coins or keys. They are materials to her. They have use.
- **Plants:** Low-maintenance, doing well. She chose the right plants for the space. She is not surprised that they thrive.

**Silhouette relationship to the den:** In a wide shot where Miri is seen in her den, she and the den create a unified warm composition. She does not stand out from the den against a contrasting background — she is slightly integrated into it. This is intentional and should be preserved. She belongs there.

## 9. EXPRESSION SHEET — 6 KEY EXPRESSIONS

### Expression 1: WARM ATTENTION (The Default Expression)
**Emotional state:** She is watching. She sees you. She has noticed something you haven't about yourself.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: open at 80% aperture — not wide with surprise, not heavy with fatigue. Alert and present. The crow's feet lines deepen slightly — her eyes crinkle at the outer corners in this attentive state.
- Eyebrows: the gentle amused arch — very slightly raised at the outer end. The inner ends are level. This is the brow of someone who finds the world mildly delightful and is waiting to see what happens next.
- Mouth: the closed upward curve — full, relaxed, the natural resting position of someone who smiles often. Not a performance of warmth — it IS her resting position.
- Cheeks: permanent blush present, cheeks softly pressed by the resting smile.
- Head position: very slight forward incline — she is present, leaning in at the emotional level even if her body is still.

**The feeling:** She is listening to everything, including the things you're not saying. She will wait until you're ready to say them.

---

### Expression 2: SHARP ASSESSMENT ("The Engineer Look")
**Emotional state:** She has identified a problem, or a flaw in a plan, or something that isn't what it claims to be. The technical mind has engaged. This is her "I have seen this failure mode before" expression.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: narrowed — upper eyelid drops to 65% aperture. The iris seems to focus, pupils slightly smaller. The gaze is direct and evaluating — she is looking AT something, not around it.
- Eyebrows: one (typically left, viewer's right) raises — the single raised eyebrow is available to both Cosmo and Miri, though they use it very differently. Miri's raised eyebrow is patient, not skeptical. The other eyebrow remains level.
- Mouth: straight and level — the upward curve has paused. Not a frown. Just a suspension of the default warmth while she processes. The corners are neither up nor down — neutral.
- Head: very slight tilt forward and to the tilted-brow side — a micro-adjustment that says "I'm running this calculation."
- Crow's feet: more visible in this expression — the narrowed eyes deepen them.

**The feeling:** She has seen this before. She knows how it ends. She's deciding whether to tell you now or let you find out yourself — and she's leaning toward letting you find out, because she knows you'll learn more that way.

---

### Expression 3: PROUD QUIET JOY
**Emotional state:** Something Luma has done (or become) has filled her with pride that she is not performing, just feeling.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: soft, warm, fully open at 85% aperture. The lower lids rise slightly — the cheek-pressed-by-smile lift. The irises catch the light more than usual — the single highlight dot feels warmer.
- Eyebrows: both gently raised in a smooth arch — not excited, not anxious. The raise is soft, like an exhale of pride.
- Mouth: the full closed smile — wide, pressing both cheeks upward firmly. This is her signature expression. The smile lines at the nose deepen. The corners go up and back. Maximum warmth.
- Cheeks: blush deepens and spreads upward, pressing toward the outer corners of her eyes. The permanent blush becomes overtly visible — the lower face is warm.
- Eyes glistening: the highlight dot increases slightly — a grandmother who might tear up, but is choosing instead to simply beam.

**The feeling:** She remembers who Luma was when she was two. She knows who Luma is becoming. The distance between those two images is this expression.

---

### Expression 4: PATIENT CORRECTION
**Emotional state:** Someone (probably Luma, occasionally Cosmo) has said something incorrect or done something that needs addressing. She will address it without making them feel bad about it.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: steady, warm aperture maintained (80%). No narrowing or widening. The gaze moves to make clear eye contact with whoever needs correcting.
- Eyebrows: both level — symmetrical, slightly lower than neutral arch. The "I am speaking now" brow position: calm, present, intentional.
- Mouth: the lips part slightly — she is about to speak. The resting curve pauses. The expression just before she says "Actually, here's the thing —" in a tone that makes you want to hear the thing.
- Head: very slight forward movement — she is bringing herself closer to the conversation.
- One hand: likely raised or gesturing — she often uses a small pointing gesture with two fingers together when making a precise correction. This is the engineering hand habit.

**The feeling:** She is not disappointed. She is not even surprised. She has simply observed an error and is providing the correction as a service. The correction will be accurate, specific, and given once. She will not repeat it.

---

### Expression 5: DELIGHTED LAUGH
**Emotional state:** Genuine amusement — something has struck her as truly funny, and she is not suppressing it.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: fully closed — her laugh closes her eyes completely. The crow's feet maximize. The eye area in this expression is entirely a constellation of smile-crinkle lines.
- Eyebrows: rise freely — both lifting with the laugh.
- Mouth: open — wider than her normal range, with the upper teeth visible as a clean rectangle. Her laugh is warm and round, not sharp. The mouth opens into a wide oval rather than a sharp horizontal.
- Cheeks: maximum — both cheek forms pressed fully upward by the laugh, the blush entirely visible and spreading.
- Head: often tips back slightly, or to one side — she leans into the laugh physically.
- Body: shoulders may move in one direction — a slight shake. Miri's laugh involves her body.

**The feeling:** She has found this funny on a cellular level. You've made a grandmother laugh and you feel like you've won something real.

---

### Expression 6: CONCERN (The Serious Version)
**Emotional state:** Something has genuinely worried her — not the manageable concern of a problem she knows how to solve, but the worry of real fear for someone she loves.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: fully open, 95% aperture — the alert-open that communicates genuine alarm. The irises show slightly more white below — the inner corner of each eye pulls slightly inward.
- Eyebrows: inner ends rise, outer ends fall — the universal distress configuration. But on Miri's face it reads differently than on Luma's: where Luma's distress brow looks like the sadness is new, Miri's reads as a worry she hoped she would never have to have.
- Mouth: pressed together, corners slightly drawn in. Not a frown — a tightening. The closed smile is fully suspended. The mouth is now the record of everything unsaid.
- Crow's feet: more visible at the outer corners — stress deepens them.
- Cheeks: the permanent blush fades noticeably — when she is seriously worried, the warmth drains from her face. This is a production note: in scenes of genuine concern, reduce the cheek blush to 10% opacity or remove it. The absence of the blush registers subliminally as "something is wrong."
- Head: forward and still — she is very present. She is not moving. She is watching.

**The feeling:** She has seen what the world can do. She hoped she wouldn't have to use that knowledge. She is using it now.

## 10. SILHOUETTE TEST — THUMBNAIL ANALYSIS

### What reads at thumbnail scale:

1. **The silver-white hair bun** — reads first as a distinctive gray oval mass on top of a dark-toned head. The gray-white hair against her deep brown skin creates strong contrast, making the updo clearly visible even at thumbnail.

2. **The wide torso mass** — broader shoulder-to-torso ratio than any other character. She reads as the widest character (in relation to her height) in the main cast. This width combined with her shorter height produces a distinctive compact, planted form.

3. **The oversized cardigan** — the terracotta rust cardigan hangs past her hips, giving her silhouette a wide, A-shaped lower profile. This is distinct from Luma's rounded hoodie silhouette: Miri's cardigan has a gentle V-opening at the center and hangs straight, while Luma's hoodie is a rounded rectangle.

4. **Short legs and no oversized feet** — unlike Luma's toy-soldier feet, Miri's feet are proportional. Her legs are short but not stubby in the same exaggerated way. This prevents her from reading as a Luma-variant at distance.

**Three-element ID at thumbnail:**
- SILVER HAIR BUN
- WIDE COMPACT TORSO
- LONG CARDIGAN (A-SHAPE LOWER SILHOUETTE)

These three read distinctly from all cast members. She cannot be confused with Luma (different hair, wider base, cardigan vs. hoodie), Cosmo (completely different proportions and silhouette mass), or Byte (completely different).

**The silhouette reads as: a settled, rooted, older adult — warm and stable, not kinetic.**

## 11. VISUAL RELATIONSHIP TO LUMA — Contrast and Connection

The relationship between Miri and Luma is expressed in their designs across two registers: **connection** (they are family, they share DNA) and **contrast** (they are at different stages of the same journey).

### Visual Connections (Design DNA shared between Miri and Luma)

| Design Element | Luma | Miri | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Head shape | 95% circle | 88% circle | Same circle family. Miri's is compressed by time. |
| Eye highlight position | Upper-left | Upper-left | Identical. Visual DNA marker. |
| Iris color family | Warm Amber #C87D3E | Deep Warm Amber #8B5E3C | Same amber, different depth. Miri's is older, richer. |
| Pupil color | Deep Cocoa #3B2820 | Near-Black Espresso #1A0F0A | Luma's pupil = Miri's hair in an earlier era. |
| Escaping hair detail | Wild ringlets, multiple curls | 2-3 soft escaping strands at ear | Same rebellious hair behavior, different energy level. |
| Garment color family | Warm Orange #E8722A | Terracotta Rust #B85C38 | Same warm family. Miri's is the settled, late-day version. |
| Default expression | About to smile | Already smiling | The same emotional baseline, at different speeds. |

### Visual Contrasts (Design expressing life stage and personality difference)

| Design Element | Luma | Miri | What It Communicates |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overall height | 3.5 heads | 3.2 heads | Miri is shorter — her height has settled |
| Hair | Explosive cloud, wild | Soft updo, mostly contained | Energy vs. wisdom. Contained but not defeated. |
| Leg proportion | Short, column-wide | Short, slight taper | Same family, different wear pattern |
| Eye size | 0.22x head width | 0.16x head width | Luma wonders; Miri observes |
| Body width | Narrow torso, wide head | Wide torso | Luma's energy is in her head; Miri's is in her body |
| Movement energy | Forward lean, kinetic | Upright, grounded | Same courage, different expression of it |
| Garment condition | Worn-comfortable, slightly oversized | Oversized, clearly years old | Both wearing beloved garments — different eras |

### The Crucial Visual Relationship
In a shot containing both Miri and Luma, the composition should communicate:

**"She came from here."**

Miri is the source of Luma's warmth, her roundness, her instinct toward care. Luma has the same shape language DNA — just running faster. Visually, the connection between them should be immediately readable. The shared amber eyes, the same highlight position, the same warm-orange-family garments: these are the show's visual argument that Luma is who she is because Miri made space for her to be it.

### Practical Composition Notes
- When framing Miri and Luma together: do not place them at the same height in frame. Miri is shorter. The height difference (Luma is 0.3 heads taller) means Luma looking at Miri naturally involves a slight downward angle — Luma meeting the eyes of someone smaller but infinitely more rooted.
- The terracotta rust of Miri's cardigan and the warm orange of Luma's hoodie should create a warm zone in any shared scene — pull the camera back and the two of them together will warm the entire composition from the left or center.
- In wide shots where both appear: Luma will always read as kinetic (some lean, some energy), Miri will always read as still. This contrast communicates their relationship without dialogue. She is the still center. Luma orbits.

## 12. DESIGN RATIONALE SUMMARY

Miri is what happens when Luma's energy finds a direction and lives with it for fifty years. She is not Luma's opposite — she is her continuation. The circle shapes are the same; they have simply had more time to settle. The warm colors are the same; they have simply deepened and taken on weight.

The "former tech engineer" aspect of her design is carried not in any single element but in the whole: her hands are working hands, her bookshelves suggest systematic organization, her old computer is kept running. She built things and she still does, quietly. This history is not displayed — it is embedded in how she holds herself and what she surrounds herself with.

The den is her character. The den is circles and weathered rectangles — warm ceramic dishes, worn wooden surfaces, old circuit boards, linen pants, a cardigan that has been washed more times than it can count and is better for it. She has curated a life that fits her. Her design communicates this: she is someone who has arrived at herself.

The show benefits from her presence the way a house benefits from its oldest inhabitant. She has been here longest. She knows where the light is best at every hour. She knows which doors stick. She knows Luma's secrets before Luma knows them herself, because she has seen them before — in the mirror, fifty years ago.

She is, simply, the person Luma is going to become. And Luma, someday, will realize this. That realization is worth its weight in character design.
