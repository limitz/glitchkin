# COSMO — Character Design Document
## "Luma & the Glitchkin" — Main Character #2 (Best Friend / Sidekick)

**Designer:** Maya Santos
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Version:** 2.0 (Cycle 2 Revision)
**Status:** Revised — Incorporating Critic Feedback

---

## 1. CHARACTER OVERVIEW

Cosmo is the counterweight to Luma's chaos. He is cautious, meticulous, and encyclopedic — and he is perpetually surrounded by people who ignore all of that. His design must communicate "intelligent, careful, slightly stiff" while still being warm and appealing enough that we love him even as we watch him be ignored. The key is that his stiffness is ENDEARING. He's not a stick-in-the-mud — he's a person who has not yet discovered how to be in his body, and the show is partly about that journey.

The design challenge: Cosmo needs to feel like a genuine contrast to Luma in every dimension — shape, proportion, color, energy — without feeling like he exists only in opposition to her. He must work as a standalone character. His glasses are the anchor, and everything else is built around them.

---

## 2. SHAPE LANGUAGE

### Primary Shapes
- **Head:** A vertical rectangle with heavily rounded corners. **Locked proportions: height = 1.0 unit, width = 0.86 units — a 1.16:1 height-to-width ratio.** This is a precise specification. The head is measurably taller than it is wide. The corner rounding radius is **0.12 units** — enough to soften the corners without making the shape read as oval. Critical: the corner rounding must NEVER exceed 0.18 units or the head begins to read as an oval/egg shape at distance. At 0.12 unit corner radius, the top and bottom of the head retain straight-ish edges that register as rectangular even in thumbnail. **Production test:** at any distance where you cannot see his glasses, his head should still read as rectangular rather than oval. If it reads oval, the corner rounding is too high — reduce it.
- **Torso:** A longer, narrower rectangle with moderately rounded corners. He is slender relative to his height. The torso reads as a taller version of Luma's torso — same construction, more vertical, less horizontal.
- **Arms:** Relatively straight cylinders with minimal taper — they don't curve outward at rest the way Luma's do. They hang more directly alongside the torso. This communicates containment and a degree of physical self-consciousness.
- **Legs:** Longer and thinner than Luma's. They have a slight taper from hip to knee. This reinforces the "gangly" quality without being extreme.
- **Feet:** Similar to Luma's in absolute size, but because his body is taller, the proportion between foot and body is smaller. They look less oversized on him — more like normal feet, which paradoxically makes him look slightly less grounded.

### Rationale
Rectangles communicate stability, intelligence, and groundedness — but they can also read as "rigid" or "stiff." This is intentional for Cosmo. His rectangle construction says "I have organized my world into tidy categories" while the rounded corners (critically important) prevent him from being threatening or cold. He is warm — but contained. As the show progresses and he loosens up, his poses and expressions should gradually move away from rectangular rigidity, but his fundamental structure remains.

The oval element appears primarily in his glasses (enormous circles) and in his eyes (visible through those lenses) — these organic circles within his otherwise rectangular construction create a visual tension that defines him: a structured, orderly person with enormous, observing eyes that see everything and miss nothing.

## 4. FACIAL CONSTRUCTION

### Head Shape
A tall rounded rectangle. The top of the head is gently rounded — not flat. The chin is more defined than Luma's — a gentle but clear jaw angle that gives him a slightly more adult facial structure. The cheeks are the widest point of the lower face, but the overall width is controlled by the rectangular overall shape. The face looks "organized" — like the features are placed with intention, which they are, because this is who Cosmo is.

### The Glasses (MOST CRITICAL ELEMENT)
Cosmo's glasses are the character. Everything else supports them.

**Frame shape:** Two perfect circles (lens frames), connected by a short nose bridge. The circles are large — each lens is approximately 0.32x head width in diameter. Because the frames are circular and his face is rectangular, the glasses create an immediate visual contrast that reads as comedic and distinctive.

**Frame material:** The frames are thick and clearly heavy — they have visual weight. **The frame line thickness is 0.06x head width** — this is a significant increase from the previous 0.025x specification, which failed to survive thumbnail reduction. At 0.06x, the frames are approximately 5.2px thick at a 100px head reference. This thickness means the frames remain visible as distinct double-rings at thumbnail scale, not collapsing into a single thin line or disappearing entirely. In real-world terms, these are the thick plastic frames of the early 2000s — unmistakably present, slightly oversized for the face. They feel like the kind of glasses that were chosen by a parent at a discount optical chain a year ago and now need to be replaced but haven't been yet.

**Frame color:**
- Fill: **Warm Espresso Brown (#5C3A20)** — a slightly lighter version of his hair color
- Inner edge (rim visible from inside): **#7A5030** — lighter to suggest the frame's inner chamfer

**Lens effect:** The lenses magnify his eyes. This is achieved by:
1. Drawing his eyes at 1.25x their normal size when seen through the glasses
2. Adding a subtle white glare shape at the top of each lens — a thin crescent of **Static White (#F0F0F0)**
3. The glass color is a very slightly tinted **#EEF4FF** — barely visible, just enough to read as "glass"

**Perpetually crooked:** The glasses are NEVER level. **The locked neutral tilt is 7 degrees counterclockwise** (left side lower, viewer's perspective). This is an increase from the previous 3-4 degrees, which was too subtle to read at distance. At 7 degrees, the tilt is clearly perceptible in a medium shot, readable in a wide shot, and still within the range of "believably slightly crooked" rather than "comically absurd." The tilt may increase during high-emotion expressions (up to 10-12 degrees during embarrassment, up to 15 degrees in extreme physical comedy), but the neutral resting state is always 7 degrees. Animators should keyframe from 7 degrees as their neutral pose baseline. **Never draw Cosmo with glasses at 0 degrees — even in a relaxed expression, they tilt.**

**Cowlick interaction:** The single cowlick on the top of his hair often catches on the glasses frame in extreme moments of surprise or frustration, making the tilt worse. This is a comedy opportunity.

### Long-Shot Readable Hook: The Notebook

Cosmo's glasses were the primary design hook, but at distance they risk reading as simple dark circles on a pale face — providing shape but not personality. A second long-shot-readable element has been added: **Cosmo always carries his slim notebook, tucked under his non-dominant arm.**

**Notebook specifications:**
- **Position:** Tucked under his left arm (viewer's right), pressed against the side of his torso. The narrow spine of the notebook is visible from the front. The notebook protrudes 0.15 heads below the elbow. This is his default carry position — it is not a prop he picks up and puts down, it is part of his silhouette.
- **Size:** 0.3 heads tall × 0.18 heads wide. Slim. It is a field notebook, not a binder.
- **Color:** **Cerulean Blue (#5B8DB8)** cover — matching his shirt stripe. The notebook and shirt read as a visual set, as if he color-coordinated his accessories with himself. He did.
- **Why this works at distance:** The notebook creates a small rectangular protrusion on his left side at mid-torso height. In silhouette, this reads as an asymmetric body form — making him distinctly left-heavier than right at the torso. This asymmetry is readable at thumbnail size. It also explains his posture: his left shoulder is very slightly lower than his right (the weight of the notebook, minimal but habitual). This posture difference is visible even in wide shots and reinforces his "upright but slightly loaded" physicality.
- **Emotional signal:** The notebook's position shifts with his state. Notebook pressed tight against his body = he is uncertain or anxious, holding on. Notebook held out in front or open = engaged, confident, processing. Notebook absent from frame = rare and signals he has fully let go of preparation — a significant character beat.
- **Production note for wide and long shots:** In any shot where his face is too small to read expression, the notebook tuck under the arm is sufficient to confirm "this is Cosmo." The combination of tall narrow rectangle silhouette + notebook protrusion on the left side + small rectangular double-circle glasses protrusion at face level = a fully readable Cosmo ID at 10% frame height.

### Eyes
The eyes are described as they appear THROUGH the glasses.

- **Shape:** Large rounded circles (not the rectangular shape Luma has — circles, because the round glasses echo and magnify them). Each eye is a large circle.
- **Iris:** **Warm Forest Green (#3D6B45)** — a muted, medium green. Not electric green — this is a contained, intellectual color.
- **Pupil:** **Deep Cocoa (#3B2820)** — same as line color.
- **Highlight dot:** Static White (#F0F0F0), upper-right position (opposite to Luma's upper-left, a subtle detail that makes them feel different in eye close-ups).
- **Eye white:** **Warm Cream (#FAF0DC)** — consistent with the show.
- **Eyelid line:** The upper eyelid is a strong arc, slightly flatter than Luma's (less dramatically arched — his eyes are more "observing" than "wondering"). The lower lid is a simple straight-ish horizontal line — barely curved. This flat lower lid is distinctive and contributes to his "slightly serious" baseline expression.

### Eyebrows
Cosmo's eyebrows are the most expressive part of his face after his eyes.

- Thinner than Luma's — not super-thick. More precise. Slightly more "drawn" in appearance.
- In neutral, they are nearly horizontal with a very slight upward arc — thoughtful rather than worried.
- Key behavior: One eyebrow (left, viewer's right) is slightly more mobile than the other. In skeptical expressions it goes up while the other holds — the classic one-eyebrow raise that is his signature.
- Color: **Deep Cocoa (#3B2820)** — same as line work, merging visually.

### Nose
Cosmo's nose is slightly more defined than Luma's — a small but visible button nose with a gentle bridge line visible in 3/4 view. In front view it remains simple — the same apostrophe/arc as Luma — but slightly larger (0.1 head width vs. 0.08 for Luma). The nose bridge is indicated as a very slight raised area between the glasses lenses in front view.

### Mouth
- In neutral, the mouth is a straight line with the very smallest upward inflection at the corners — a closed mouth that is waiting. Not a smile, not a frown. Expectant patience.
- His smile, when it appears, is notably smaller and more contained than Luma's — his mouth corners pull back but not dramatically up. It reads as "quietly pleased" rather than "jubilant."
- His deadpan expression (see Expression Sheet) has a perfectly flat mouth line — no inflection in either direction. This flat line in combination with his flat-lidded observing eyes creates the "I told you so" power.
- Teeth are shown less frequently than Luma's — he is more controlled. When teeth appear it's a bigger emotional beat.

### Cheeks
Blush: used more sparingly than with Luma. His blush appears in embarrassment (he is easily embarrassed) and occasionally in genuine delight — which he immediately tries to suppress. The shape is the same oval as Luma's but smaller and slightly higher on his cheek — more subdued in placement.

## 5. HAIR

### Description
Neat, short, straight black hair with a precise center-right part. His hair does NOT have the wild volume of Luma's — it sits flat and controlled. The total mass adds barely 0.05 heads of height and almost nothing to his width silhouette. It reads as a simple flat shape on top of the rounded-rectangle head.

### Structure
The hair is rendered as a flat cap shape — a simple graphic zone above the skull line. The part is a clean line (a gap in the hair) slightly right of center (viewer's left of center). The left side (viewer's right) is slightly thicker — more hair swept to that side.

**The Cowlick:** One section at the very back-top of the head refuses to lie flat. This is not a dramatic vertical spike — it is a small, gentle forward-pointing curl/wave, approximately 0.07 heads in size, that can be seen in profile and 3/4 views. In front view it's visible as a slight thickening at the crown where that section resists the part. Cosmo has clearly tried to flatten it with water. It has defied him.

## 7. CLOTHING CONSTRUCTION — DETAILS

### The Striped Shirt
Long-sleeve shirt. Fitted — not baggy, not tight. The collar is a simple crew neck, low and clean. No decorative elements at the collar.

**Fold lines:** 2-3 maximum, and they are CLEAN. Cosmo's clothes don't rumple the way Luma's do. His fold lines are precise: one horizontal across the torso at the belt line area, one at each elbow when his arm is slightly bent, and that's it. The restraint of his fold lines contributes to his "contained" visual personality.

**Shirt length:** Tucked in. Always tucked in. The shirt never hangs out of the pants. This is non-negotiable for Cosmo.

### The Pants
The pants have a visible center crease (one vertical line down the center of each leg in front view). This crease is pressed and clean. It contributes to his sense of personal order.

### Optional: Thin Cardigan Layer
For certain scenes (school, colder days), Cosmo can wear a thin zip-up or button-up cardigan over his shirt. The cardigan color is **Dusty Lavender (#A89BBF)** — from the show's shadow/twilight palette. It reads as slightly old-fashioned, slightly librarian, entirely Cosmo. The cardigan does NOT cover the stripes of the shirt — it is worn open.

### Accessories
- **Notebook — PERMANENT CARRIED ELEMENT (not optional, not situational):** The slim notebook is always tucked under his left arm. It is part of his silhouette and defines his asymmetric body read. See Section 4 (The Long-Shot Readable Hook) for full notebook specifications. The notebook is never absent except as a significant character beat. In any drawing of Cosmo, the notebook must be present unless the scene specifically calls for its absence as a story moment.
- **No jewelry** — clean.
- The glasses are the defining accessory; the notebook is the defining prop. Together they constitute the two mandatory elements of every Cosmo drawing.

## 8. FRONT VIEW — CONSTRUCTION NOTES

For the front view (primary model sheet view):

1. Begin with the tall rounded rectangle for the head. Note: taller than wide.
2. Draw the glasses circles centered in the lower 60% of the face — they should feel large, slightly overwhelming the face. This is correct.
3. Eyebrows are visible ABOVE the glasses frames — they float above and are not obscured by the frame.
4. The nose fits between and slightly below the nose bridge of the glasses.
5. The mouth is in the lower third of the face, below the glasses entirely.
6. Torso is centered, narrower than the head at shoulder width.
7. Arms hang relatively straight — minimal outward curve (contrast with Luma's curved-arm stance).
8. Legs are longer and thinner than Luma's relative to the overall height.
9. In the neutral front-view pose: weight is equally distributed, feet are approximately 0.5 head widths apart, both pointing forward (not duck-footed like Luma). He stands like he's waiting for a bus he tracked on an app and knows is 3 minutes away.

### 3/4 View — Key Notes
- In 3/4 view, the glasses become crucial to manage. The near-side lens is full-circle; the far-side lens foreshortens into an ellipse. The nose bridge is visible connecting them.
- The far-side eye, seen through the foreshortened lens, is slightly compressed.
- The glasses frame casts a subtle shadow on his cheek — indicated as a thin shadow strip in his skin shadow tone.
- The cowlick is best visible in 3/4 view — it pokes up behind the crown.
- His tucked shirt creates a clean horizontal break at the waist line.

### Back View — Key Notes
- Back view is simple. Hair: flat cap shape with the cowlick visible as the only disruption.
- The shirt stripes wrap around to the back — same horizontal stripes, same precision.
- The tucked shirt creates a slight gathered pucker at the back waistband — a small V-shape of fabric where the shirt tucks in.
- The creased pants continue in back view — the crease line runs down the center back of each leg as well.
- No glasses visible from behind, but the ears can show a slight mark or slight redness where the temple arms of the glasses rest — a very small detail for close-up back views.

## 9. EXPRESSION SHEET — 6 KEY EXPRESSIONS

### Expression 1: THE DEADPAN / "I TOLD YOU SO" (The Signature Expression)
**Emotional state:** Cosmo was right. Something he predicted has come to pass. He is looking directly at the camera — through the fourth wall — sharing his experience with the audience. He is not triumphant. He is simply correct. Again.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: half-open — the upper eyelid drops to 65% aperture, creating a heavy-lidded look. The bottom lid stays perfectly flat (his characteristic flat lower lid is maximized here). The gaze is direct — straight at camera. Magnified slightly through the glasses.
- Eyebrows: completely level, perfectly horizontal. Not raised, not furrowed. Flat. Neutral. The brow above the raised-eyebrow side (left, viewer's right) is absolutely identical to the right. Perfect symmetry of resignation.
- Mouth: perfectly flat horizontal line. No inflection. The mouth is a ruler. The corners of the mouth do not turn up or down by even a pixel.
- Cheeks: no blush. No emotion on the cheeks at all.
- Glasses: the classic crooked angle — **7 degrees counterclockwise** (the locked neutral tilt). The Deadpan expression does not alter the glasses tilt; it holds at the canonical 7-degree resting position. The face is so controlled that the glasses are the only element with visible deviation, and even that deviation is fixed and permanent.

**Camera direction note:** This expression is designed to include a look at the camera — one of his recurring comedic beats. The eyes make direct contact with the audience. This should be held for 2-3 beats for maximum comedic effect.

**The feeling:** He doesn't even need to say "I told you so." The face says it. The face HAS said it. The face will probably need to say it again next week.

---

### Expression 2: ANXIOUS OVERTHINKING
**Emotional state:** A situation has presented multiple possible outcomes and he is calculating all of them simultaneously. None of them end well. He is absolutely correct about this but proceeding anyway because the alternative is worse.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: wide open — at 90% aperture. The irises shift slightly upward (looking at the ceiling/sky as he processes information). Pupils are slightly smaller than normal — stress response.
- Eyebrows: both raised, inner ends higher (worry configuration), but the raise is CONTROLLED — this is a person who manages his worry. There is a mild crease line between the brows.
- Mouth: a small horizontal tightening — lips pressed together in a thin line with a very slight downward droop at the corners. It is the mouth of calculation.
- Cheeks: no blush yet — stress, not embarrassment.
- Optional: one or two small sweat beads — classic round sweat drops flying off the temple. In the style of the show these would be in **Warm Cream (#FAF0DC)** — tiny.
- Hands (indicated as they are often part of this expression): one hand raised with the index finger extended — he's about to list the problems. The other hand may hold the notebook, already open.
- Glasses: potentially slightly MORE crooked than usual — agitation is loosening them.

**The feeling:** He is running the numbers. The numbers are bad. He will share them in approximately one sentence, using at least three "actuallys."

---

### Expression 3: GENUINE DELIGHT / RARE JOY
**Emotional state:** Something has delighted Cosmo completely and he has momentarily forgotten to be restrained. This expression should feel DIFFERENT from anything else he does — it's him forgetting himself.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: fully wide open — 100% aperture. The circle shape of his eyes through the glasses becomes maximally circular. Upper eyelids are clean high arcs. Lower lids curve upward (the "cheek-pressed-by-smile" lower lid rise). Highlight dots appear larger — his eyes are practically sparkling.
- Eyebrows: both fully raised, but in a DIFFERENT way from anxiety — these arches are smooth, high, and pleasantly curved, not pinched. There is no crease between them.
- Mouth: a genuine wide smile — THIS is the contrast. His smile is not quite as enormous as Luma's, but for him it is huge. Both corners pull back and up significantly. Upper teeth visible as a clean rectangle. The smile is still more contained than Luma's — it has corners while hers goes ear-to-ear.
- Cheeks: blush appears — the only time it appears in a positive emotion for him. Two warm oval blush marks press upward with the cheek muscle rise.
- Glasses: the smile pushes his cheeks up, which actually lifts the glasses slightly — they may become MORE level in this expression as the cheeks prop them up.
- Head: slight forward tilt — he's leaning into whatever caused this.

**The feeling:** He has just discovered something new. Something true. Something wonderful. He will remember this feeling for years. He will probably write about it in the notebook.

---

### Expression 4: EMBARRASSED / FLUSTERED
**Emotional state:** Cosmo is embarrassed. This happens regularly. It manifests as an explosive, full-face reaction that he immediately begins trying to suppress.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: the left and right eyes behave differently — one (say left, viewer's right) squints as if trying to disappear, while the other stays wider. This asymmetry reads as "flustered" and "trying to control self simultaneously with failing to do so."
- Eyebrows: the asymmetric brow configuration — one pulled in and down, one raised. Chaos in the brow region.
- Mouth: can take two forms:
  - **Form A (mortified):** A wide, rigid grin — like Luma's Guilty expression but worse, because Cosmo has no poker face. Too many teeth, wrong kind of smile.
  - **Form B (suppressed):** Lips pressed together in a tight horizontal, cheeks puffed out slightly — the face of someone trying to hold everything in.
- Cheeks: MAXIMUM blush. His cheeks go entirely **#E8A87C** — the blush overtakes the lower face. In extreme cases the warm peach tone spreads upward past the nose.
- Glasses: pushed crooked by his animated face — possibly 6-7 degrees off-level in this expression. May be in the process of sliding down his nose.
- Head: he's looking anywhere but at the person who caused this. Eyes redirected sideways or downward.

**The feeling:** If he could become the floor, he would. He is considering whether the floor is an option.

---

### Expression 5: RESIGNED EXASPERATION
**Emotional state:** Luma has done the thing. Again. He was RIGHT THERE. He told her. This is different from the deadpan — there is more emotion here. He cares too much to be completely neutral.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: a long, slow blink-adjacent state — the upper eyelids are at 50% or lower. Not sleepy — a controlled, deliberate "processing this information" closure. The lower lids may rise slightly.
- Eyebrows: one eyebrow clearly higher than the other — the signature asymmetric brow, but drooping at the outer end. The inner end pinches slightly in. It is the brow of someone who has given up being surprised while not quite managing to not be irritated.
- Mouth: a small downward tug at one corner — the opposite of his one-sided smile. One corner of the mouth drops while the rest stays level. It is a micro-expression of tiredness.
- Cheeks: no blush. This is past the embarrassment phase.
- Hands: often a palm raise — not aggressive, just "what am I supposed to do with this information."
- A small "sigh" visual indicator can accompany: a tiny curved line near the mouth indicating a breath out. Small and subtle.

**The feeling:** He loves her like a sibling and she is exhausting him in the same way. He will still run toward the thing she jumped toward. He just needs a moment.

---

### Expression 6: NERVOUS COURAGE
**Emotional state:** He is scared. He is doing it anyway. This is the expression of Cosmo at his character-growth best — the moment when his cautious calculation arrives at "go" and he goes.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: a specific configuration — wide open (worry) but also directed FORWARD (determination). The irises are angled slightly upward and toward the goal. The pupils are small (fear), but the brows are coming down (action). These contradictory signals together read as "terrified but moving."
- Eyebrows: inner ends pulled together and slightly down — determination brow. But not fully locked — one inner end is slightly higher than the other. The mix of determination and lingering fear.
- Mouth: pressed firmly together — the same flat line as the deadpan, but the corners are pulled back slightly as if bracing for impact. The jaw is set.
- Cheeks: pale — stress. The blush is absent. The skin tone reads slightly lighter through the highlight areas (the highlight tone becomes more prominent).
- Body posture (as relevant): stiff, shoulders pulled back — his natural rigidity working IN his favor for once. He looks braver than he feels, because rectangles look resolute.
- One small detail: his cowlick typically sticks up more prominently in fear/adrenaline states, as if even his hair is standing up.

**The feeling:** He calculated the odds. The odds were bad. He went anyway. This is the expression that makes the audience love him completely.

## 10. ACTION POSE

### Pose: "Rapid Running, Consulting a Reference While in Motion"

**Description:** Cosmo is running — urgently, but not gracefully. He runs with his elbows in close to his body and a slight forward lean that looks more like "controlled falling" than athletic motion. In one hand he holds his slim notebook (open), from which he is reading while running.

**Body positioning:**
- Forward lean: approximately 20 degrees from vertical — less than Luma's 30 degrees. He is less committed to his forward momentum and this shows.
- Lead leg (right, viewer's left): extended forward. The knee is bent at approximately 100 degrees — he's mid-stride but his stride is shorter than it should be for someone running this urgently.
- Trail leg (left, viewer's right): back and slightly kicked up — but barely. His rear foot is approximately 30 degrees off vertical. He doesn't kick his legs out dramatically the way Luma would.
- Lead arm (left, viewer's right): holding the notebook open. The arm is raised slightly — he's reading it while running. The fingers (mitten hands in this view) are curved around the notebook spine.
- Trail arm (right, viewer's left): raised with the hand near his face — he's about to say something important. Index finger is raised (close-up: single finger extended, mitten thumb opposed).

**Hair:** Flat and tidy even in motion — but the cowlick is flying more obviously backward (wind resistance catching that section).

**Line of action:** A gentle forward-leaning S-curve — his body arcs forward but his raised book-arm creates a secondary counterbalancing direction. He is not as unified in his line of action as Luma — his line of action has a footnote.

## 11. DESIGN NOTES — DOS AND DON'TS

### What Makes Cosmo Work
- The GLASSES do 40% of the characterization work. If the glasses are right (large, circular, thick-framed, perpetually crooked), the character reads correctly at a glance.
- His RESTRAINT is the key to his appeal. He is not less expressive than Luma — he is differently expressive. His expressions are smaller but they must be executed with complete precision or they vanish. Every Cosmo expression is read against the baseline of his near-neutral face. The delta matters more than the absolute.
- The CONTRAST with Luma is structural. In every scene they share, their shape languages should be visible in juxtaposition: her circles against his rectangles, her warm orange against his cool blues, her wild hair against his flat cap.
- His CLOTHES are character. The tucked shirt, pressed pants, matching glasses/shoes — these choices are not accidents, they are Cosmo expressing control over a world he cannot fully control. Respect the precision of his wardrobe.
- The COWLICK is his humanity. It is the one thing he cannot fix, the small rebellion of the physical world against his organizational instincts. It must always be there.

### What to Avoid
- **DO NOT** make his glasses circular frames that don't magnify. The magnification effect — drawing his eyes larger inside the lenses — is essential to his comedic visual identity.
- **DO NOT** level his glasses. They are always slightly crooked. Build this in from the ground up — even in character sheet neutral, they tilt.
- **DO NOT** make him as wide as Luma. His narrowness compared to her is compositionally important. He is the vertical accent to her horizontal presence.
- **DO NOT** give him wild hair or loose clothing. His design is specifically ordered to contrast with Luma's expressive design. The tucked shirt is not optional.
- **DO NOT** make his expressions mirror Luma's. Where she has a big open-mouthed laugh, he has a quietly pleased closed-mouth uptick. Where she has shocked-eyes wide open, he has a resigned-blink close. Find the Cosmo version of every emotion, which is usually more contained.
- **DO NOT** forget the pants crease. It sounds minor. It is not. It is character detail.
- **DO NOT** draw him bent or slouched as his default. He stands upright — this is his natural state. Slumping only appears in his most defeated expressions as a deliberate departure from his normal posture.

### Silhouette Test — Explicit Thumbnail Analysis (Revised Design)

This section reflects the Cycle 2 revised design: 0.12-unit corner radius (preventing oval drift), 0.06x head-width glasses frames, 7-degree neutral tilt, and notebook as a permanent carried element.

**Test methodology:** Render Cosmo as a solid black shape with no color, no line detail. Observe at thumbnail scale (character approximately 50px tall).

#### What reads at thumbnail scale, in order of visual priority:

1. **The tall narrow silhouette.** Cosmo is visibly taller and narrower than Luma. This reads FIRST at any scale. The height differential and the contained width are the most basic facts about him, and they read immediately. **Result: CONFIRMED — distinctly different from all other characters.**

2. **The rectangular head.** With the corner radius locked at 0.12 units, the head reads as a tall rectangle even at thumbnail size. The top and bottom edges have sufficient straight-line length that the shape registers as "rectangle" rather than "oval." This was the specific Cycle 1 failure — at distance, a corner radius that was too generous caused the head to read as oval, making Cosmo visually ambiguous. At 0.12 units, this ambiguity is resolved. **Result: RECTANGULAR HEAD CONFIRMED at thumbnail. The oval drift failure is fixed.**

3. **The glasses.** At thumbnail size, the two circular frames of the glasses now read as distinct dark rings on the face plane. This is entirely due to the increased frame thickness (0.06x vs. the previous 0.025x). The thin-framed version of the glasses collapsed to near-invisible at thumbnail — they provided no silhouette information. At 0.06x, the two circles create a **double-ring visual signature** on his face that is unique and readable even when his face is 25-30px tall. The 7-degree tilt adds a small but perceptible asymmetry to this double-ring that reinforces his personality. **Result: GLASSES READ AT DISTANCE. This is a critical design correction.**

4. **The notebook protrusion.** The slim notebook tucked under his left arm creates a small rectangular bump on his left side at mid-torso height. At thumbnail scale this reads as a slight asymmetry in his torso width — the left side extends marginally further than the right. This asymmetry is subtle but meaningful: it distinguishes him from a perfectly symmetrical upright form and communicates "this person is carrying something, always." **Result: readable at medium shots, subtle at thumbnail — functions as a confirmation element, not a primary ID.**

5. **The cowlick.** The small upward protrusion at the crown reads at medium scale. At thumbnail it may merge into the overall head silhouette. This is acceptable — it is a detail element, not a primary ID hook.

6. **Leg length.** His longer, straighter legs are visible in comparison to Luma when both are in frame, but in isolation his legs read simply as "longer than typical for this head size." This reinforces the "slightly gangly, upright" read without overwhelming the other silhouette elements.

#### The Revised Silhouette Test Summary:

**Three-element ID at thumbnail size:**
- TALL NARROW RECTANGLE BODY
- RECTANGULAR HEAD (not oval — protected by corner radius spec)
- DOUBLE-CIRCLE GLASSES RINGS on face

These three elements together produce a unique silhouette that cannot be confused with any other character in the show. **Cosmo now passes the silhouette test at all scales with the Cycle 2 design revisions.**

#### Previous Failure Mode (Documented for Reference)
With the Cycle 1 design (corner radius unspecified, frames at 0.025x head width, tilt 3-4 degrees):
- At thumbnail scale, the head read as an oval due to over-rounded corners
- The glasses frames disappeared, leaving only a faint ring suggestion
- The resulting silhouette was: tall narrow oval body — ambiguous, not distinctive
- **This failure is resolved in Cycle 2.**

## 12. DESIGN RATIONALE SUMMARY

Cosmo's design is a study in contained energy. Everything about him is trying to be organized, and succeeding to varying degrees. The rectangle-dominant construction says "I have decided where things go." The enormous glasses say "but I am watching everything very carefully." The cowlick says "I am still twelve."

The color identity — blue and sage green — places him in the calm, analytical zone of the show's real-world palette. He belongs to the warm analog world. He does NOT generate or attract glitch energy the way Luma does (and the way Byte represents). He is the anchor to normalcy, and his colors reflect that: grounded, organized, present.

The key to making Cosmo resonate is understanding that his "sidekick" role is not about being lesser — he is fully competent and often right. His character arc is about learning that competence and caution are tools, not identities, and that sometimes the better move is to be wrong with confidence. His design should always carry the latent potential for him to surprise us — the nervous courage expression is in there, underneath the deadpan. That's the promise the design makes.

He is, fundamentally, the character the audience wants to be before they admit they're actually Luma.
