# LUMA — Character Design Document
## "Luma & the Glitchkin" — Main Character #1 (Protagonist)

**Designer:** Maya Santos
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Version:** 2.0 (Cycle 2 Revision)
**Status:** Revised — Incorporating Critic Feedback

---

## 1. CHARACTER OVERVIEW

Luma is the heart of the show — a 12-year-old girl who acts first, thinks second, and somehow lands on her feet because the universe rewards audacity. She is warm, loud, reckless, and genuinely kind. Her design must communicate all of this before she speaks a single word.

The central design challenge: she needs to feel simultaneously **approachable** (rounded, soft, warm) and **kinetic** (mid-motion energy, like she just landed or is about to leap). The pixel-patterned hoodie is her visual identity anchor — it ties her to the digital world she's stumbled into without making her look like a computer program.

---

## 2. SHAPE LANGUAGE

### Primary Shapes
- **Head:** Near-perfect circle, very slightly compressed vertically (like a circle that's been gently squished — 95% circular). This reads as friendly and approachable at any scale.
- **Torso:** Rounded rectangle with heavily rounded corners — almost a stadium shape. Think of a pillow or a smooth river stone. No sharp edges anywhere on the body.
- **Arms:** Tapered cylinders — slightly thicker at the shoulder, narrowing gently to mitten-hands. They have a natural outward curve when relaxed, giving her a ready-to-grab-the-world posture.
- **Legs:** Short rounded columns. They do not taper — they are the same width top to bottom, like a toy figure. This gives her visual weight and groundedness.
- **Feet:** Slightly oversized ovals, tilting outward at rest (duck-footed stance). They anchor her to the ground even when the rest of her is in motion.

### Rationale
Circles communicate trust, safety, and energy simultaneously — they roll, they bounce. Luma IS a ball of energy, so the circle language reinforces personality at every level. The rounded rectangle torso prevents her from being TOO circular (which can read as static) — it gives her a body with directionality. Every corner that COULD be sharp has been softened. This is a character who cannot hurt you even if she's running full tilt into you.

## 4. FACIAL CONSTRUCTION

### Head Shape
A near-perfect circle. The jaw is only slightly implied — a minimal flattening at the bottom with a very gentle chin point (barely 3% of the circle's radius). The cheeks are the widest point, not the temples. This gives the face a "babyface" quality that reads as youthful and sympathetic.

### Eyes
- **Shape:** Large rounded rectangles with heavily rounded corners. Not oval — the slight squareness of the rounded rectangle gives them visual weight and prevents them from looking vacant.
- **Iris:** Solid fill — **Warm Amber (#C87D3E)**. A rich, reddish-amber that complements her skin tone and reads warm. No gradient. No complex iris detail.
- **Pupil:** Small solid circle in **Deep Cocoa (#3B2820)**, centered in the iris.
- **Highlight dot:** One small circle of **Static White (#F0F0F0)**, positioned at the upper-left of the iris. Single highlight only — no secondary catches.
- **Eye white:** **Warm Cream (#FAF0DC)** — not pure white. Slightly warm tinted.
- **Eyelid line:** Upper eyelid is a strong curved line in **Deep Cocoa (#3B2820)** at 80% of silhouette line weight. Lower lid is a thinner, shorter curved line — barely there. The eye reads "open" and "bright" by default.
- **Spacing:** Eyes sit in the lower half of the face, spaced roughly 0.35 head widths apart (center to center). This gives her a wide-eyed, expressive face.

### Eyebrows
- Thick, graphic, slightly irregular. They are NOT smooth arcs — each eyebrow has a subtle kink in the middle that makes them feel natural and expressive.
- Default position: horizontal with a very slight inward tilt (the medial end drops 2-3% lower than the lateral end). This is her neutral/curious expression.
- Color: **Deep Cocoa (#3B2820)**, same as line work. They merge seamlessly with the outlines at extreme expressions.
- Width: Each eyebrow is approximately 0.3 head widths. Thick enough to read from a distance.
- Can float well above the skull during extreme expressions — they are not tethered to the brow ridge.

### Nose
- Minimal. A small curved line — essentially an apostrophe or a tiny arc — placed just below center of the face. In 3/4 view it becomes a simple button shape with a small nostril wing indication.
- Color: same as line work, at 60% weight (internal detail line).
- The nose is deliberately underplayed so the eyes and mouth carry all expression.

### Mouth
- In neutral, a short closed-mouth curve — gentle upward arc. She looks like she's about to smile even when resting.
- The mouth can stretch to extreme widths for big expressions — up to 0.7 head widths for full-open laughs. It deforms freely.
- Lips are NOT drawn. The mouth is a simple graphic shape — the line itself IS the mouth. No upper-lip bow, no lower-lip bottom line. This keeps it stylized and animation-friendly.
- Teeth: shown as a simple white rectangle (or individual rounded rectangles for close-ups) only in big expressions. Default: no teeth.
- Tongue: a simple oval in **Terracotta (#C75B39)** for comedic open-mouth moments.

### Cheeks
- Blush: A simple, slightly elongated oval in **#E8A87C** (warm peach) placed on the lower cheek, below the outer corner of each eye. Drawn at 40% opacity feel — it should be subtle, not painted on. Applied in joyful or embarrassed states but NOT in neutral. The blush is a flat shape, no gradient.

## 5. HAIR

### Description
Luma's hair is her most distinctive feature after her hoodie. It is a massive, exuberant cloud of dark curls — almost absurdly large relative to her head size. It adds 35-40% of her head height above the skull and extends slightly beyond her shoulder width on each side.

### Structure
The hair should be thought of as a **shape**, not individual strands. Draw the silhouette first — a roughly triangular cloud that is widest at the middle and narrows slightly at the top (like a flame or an explosion mid-bloom). Then add **exactly 5 large spiral-curl indicators** within that silhouette. This is the locked production number — not 4, not 6, always 5. These are NOT individual curls — they are visual suggestions of the curl pattern. Over-rendering the hair will make it look heavy and static. Under-rendering it (just a silhouette blob) loses the personality.

**LOCKED CURL COUNT: 5 curls.** This is the production standard across all animators and all scenes. Emotional hair states (see below) may change the SHAPE and SIZE of curls but never the count. A character sheet showing any number other than 5 is incorrect and must be revised.

**Hair silhouette rules:**
- Always slightly asymmetric. The right side (viewer's left) typically has more volume.
- One or two curls always escape the main mass — a small ringlet near her ear, a curl drooping over her forehead.
- Small wispy flyaways at the top of the mass — 3-5 tiny curved lines that suggest the hair is barely contained.

### Color
- **Base fill:** **Near-Black Espresso (#1A0F0A)** — not pure black, but nearly so. Just enough warmth to avoid looking harsh against her skin tone.
- **Shadow tone:** **Void Black (#0A0A14)** — used to define the larger curl masses within the shape, indicating depth between curl sections.
- **Highlight tone:** **Deep Warm Brown (#3D1F0F)** — a single curved highlight shape on the topmost part of the hair mass, indicating the crown where light catches. Keep it small — her hair absorbs light, it doesn't reflect much.

### Emotional Reactivity
Luma's hair subtly changes with her emotional state. This is a secondary animation note but must be readable in still design documents:

- **Excited/Reckless:** Hair lifts slightly, flyaways multiply, the mass becomes slightly taller and wilder. Individual curls begin to spiral outward.
- **Scared/Deflated:** Hair droops, the whole mass loses volume and flattens against the sides of her head. The silhouette compresses.
- **Focused/Determined:** Hair pulls back slightly — the flyaways reduce and the mass becomes tighter, as if the hair is getting out of the way so she can work.
- **Near Glitch Energy:** Hair stands on end — individual curls straighten partially and lift. A subtle static-electric effect (tiny pixel confetti particles escape from the tips).

This should NOT be animated in every scene — it's a punctuation mark, used sparingly for comedic or dramatic emphasis.

### Pixel Grid Pattern on Hoodie
This is the character's visual hook. The pattern is a grid of small squares on the chest panel and the outer forearm/sleeve areas.

| Pixel Color | Hex | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| Cyan pixel | #00F0FF | Scattered throughout — most numerous (~40% of pattern pixels) |
| Magenta pixel | #FF2D6B | Accent pixels (~20% of pattern) |
| Acid Green pixel | #39FF14 | Rare accent (~10% of pattern) |
| UV Purple pixel | #7B2FBE | Rare accent (~10% of pattern) |
| Static White pixel | #F0F0F0 | Dots that look like "normal" pixels (~20% of pattern) |

**Pattern construction rules:**
- The grid square size is approximately 3-4px when the character is at standard rig size (character head = 100px reference). In print/model sheet scale, each pixel square should be clearly legible.
- The pattern appears on: center chest (a roughly rectangular panel, 0.5 head widths x 0.4 head heights), the outer forearm from elbow to wrist, and the edges of the hood rim.
- The pattern is NOT a perfect grid — some squares are slightly off-axis, some have gaps, as if the clothing itself has a mild glitch.
- **Animated behavior:** Near glitch energy, some pixel squares blink, shift color, or temporarily rearrange. Pixels at the edge of the pattern may "fall off" as small confetti particles. This effect is NOT present in the design document but must be accounted for in the rigging notes.

### Pixel Hoodie — Simplified Production Version

The full pixel pattern is correct for model sheets, close-ups, and medium-close shots where Luma's chest panel is clearly visible. For all other shot types, use the following simplified versions. **Animators must reference this section before drawing the hoodie in any shot wider than a medium shot.**

#### Medium Shot (character at waist-up, head roughly 60-80px tall in final output)
- **What is kept:** The overall rectangular chest panel boundary remains. The pixel grid is reduced to **a 4x3 block arrangement** — four columns of three squares each, 12 total squares. Colors remain multicolored (mix of cyan, magenta, and white — drop acid green and UV purple at this scale). Square size increases relative to the panel to maintain visual read.
- **What is dropped:** Individual off-axis squares, gap pixels, and any squares outside the core chest panel rectangle. The hood rim pattern becomes a simple **single-pixel-wide border line** in cyan (#00F0FF) along the interior edge of the hood. The forearm pattern becomes a **two-stripe bar** — two short horizontal lines of cyan squares on the outer forearm only.
- **Rule:** Pattern must still read as "digital/glitch texture" — if it reads as a simple stripe or solid color, it has been oversimplified.

#### Wide Shot (character at full body, head roughly 30-50px tall in final output)
- **What is kept:** The chest panel boundary only. The interior of the panel is filled as a **flat abstract texture** — a slightly irregular cyan/magenta two-tone checker pattern suggesting the grid without rendering individual squares. The specific arrangement: 6 cyan squares in an irregular scatter across the panel, 4 magenta squares, rendered at approximately 3x the normal square size. Total: 10 visible marks on the chest panel.
- **What is dropped:** All sleeve/forearm pattern. All hood rim pattern. Only the chest panel reads at this scale — protect it.
- **Rule:** The viewer should still be able to register "she has something digital on her chest." If the pattern is invisible, add contrast or increase square size.

#### Very Wide Shot (character at full body, head roughly 15-25px tall — crowd scenes, establishing shots)
- **What is kept:** The chest panel is rendered as a **single flat color suggestion** — the panel area is filled with a flat **Electric Cyan (#00F0FF)** rectangle at 35% opacity over the base orange. This creates a subtle cool-tinted zone on the hoodie chest. No individual squares. No detail.
- **What is dropped:** All pattern detail. All other pixel elements.
- **Purpose:** At very wide distances, the point is not to show the pattern — it is to preserve the **color identity** of the garment. The slight cyan tint is enough to communicate "this is Luma's glitch-patterned hoodie" to a viewer who already knows the character. For a viewer who doesn't know the character yet, the orange hoodie still reads correctly.
- **Critical rule:** Do NOT substitute a solid different color for the hoodie at this scale. The base orange (#E8722A) must remain dominant; the cyan is additive, not replacement.

## 7. CLOTHING CONSTRUCTION — DETAILS

### The Hoodie
The hoodie is Luma's signature garment. It is a standard pullover hoodie — no zipper. It fits her slightly large, in the way a beloved garment that's been worn a thousand times fits: comfortable, broken-in, slightly too big in the shoulders, sleeves that go just past the wrist bone.

**Construction details:**
- Hood: always worn down, folded back on the neck. The hood shadow line (the crease where the hood folds against the back of the neck) is always visible in front and 3/4 views as a curved line behind the head.
- Kangaroo pocket: a single large front pocket spanning the lower third of the torso. The pocket hem line is a clean horizontal graphic line. No zipper, no division — single pocket opening.
- Ribbed cuffs: a band at the wrist end of each sleeve, approximately 0.08 head heights wide. Three horizontal parallel lines indicate the ribbing texture (kept minimal — 3 lines max).
- Ribbed hem: same ribbing treatment at the bottom of the hoodie.
- Fold lines on the body: 2-3 maximum. One beneath the chest/armpit, one at the kangaroo pocket hem.

### Pants
Slim-fit but not tight. Slightly high-waisted (the waistband sits at natural waist). The pants end at the ankle, not baggy, not cropped — right length. Two side-seam pockets indicated by a simple angled line on each hip.

**Fold lines:** One at each knee (compression fold when standing), one at the back of the knee (indicated in 3/4 view). Maximum 3 fold lines total.

### Accessories
- **No jewelry.** No necklaces, earrings, or bracelets. Clean.
- **No bag** in default pose. She has her kangaroo pocket.
- The only accessory is the hoodie itself, which IS her personality.

## 8. FRONT VIEW — CONSTRUCTION NOTES

For the front view (primary model sheet view):

1. Begin with a perfect circle for the head.
2. Center-line is exactly vertical, with no head tilt in the neutral pose. (Head tilts will be used extensively in expressions and poses, but the model sheet neutral is head-on.)
3. Torso sits centered beneath the head, with approximately 0.05 head gap for the neck.
4. Both arms hang at the side in slight outward curve — not rigidly straight. The left arm has a very slight forward angle (toward the viewer), the right arm trails slightly back. This prevents the symmetry from reading as dead.
5. Legs together at the top (hips), feet angled slightly outward. The silhouette reads as a stable A-shape from waist down.
6. Hair mass is centered but asymmetric in detail (right side has one more visible curl).

### 3/4 View — Key Notes
- In 3/4 view (standard 3/4 left, showing viewer's right side of her face), the near eye is full-sized, the far eye is approximately 70% of full size and slightly obscured by the nose.
- The nose, nearly invisible in front view, becomes a small button form on the near cheek in 3/4.
- The pixel hoodie pattern on the chest rotates with the body — the far-side chest panel foreshortens. Maintain pattern legibility by keeping 60% of the pattern visible.
- Hair: in 3/4, the near-side hair mass extends further from the head (it's coming toward the viewer). The far-side is compressed. The escaping curl by the ear becomes prominent.

### Back View — Key Notes
- The back of the hoodie is plain orange — no pixel pattern on the back.
- The hood is visible as a folded flap resting on the upper back, with the drawstring tips hanging on either side of her neck.
- Hair: in back view, the hair mass is the dominant element. It spreads across the upper back. The bottom of the hair mass falls between the shoulder blades.
- The ribbed hem of the hoodie and pants waistband are visible.
- No face. The silhouette of the hair, hoodie, and shoes is sufficient to identify her character at a glance.

## 9. EXPRESSION SHEET — 6 KEY EXPRESSIONS

### Expression 1: RECKLESS EXCITEMENT (The Signature Expression)
**Emotional state:** About to do something risky and completely on board with it. Adrenaline + confidence + a touch of chaos.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: wide open, both rounded-rectangle shapes at maximum aperture (pupils visible in center). Upper eyelids lift — the top line of each eye becomes a higher arc. Eyelids do not droop at all.
- Eyebrows: shooting upward and slightly outward — arched high above the brow ridge, nearly floating. The inner ends lift higher than the outer ends (the reverse of worry — this is surprise-excitement). Space between eyebrow and eye triples.
- Mouth: enormous open grin. The mouth corners pull back AND up simultaneously, reaching from ear-to-ear proximity. Upper teeth block visible as a single white rectangle. Lower teeth barely show. The mouth corners dimple — indicated by two small curved lines at each mouth corner.
- Cheeks: **RECKLESS EXCITEMENT BLUSH — HIGH INTENSITY, CHEEKS ONLY.** Round circles (not elongated ovals) placed on the outer cheek, lateral to the eye corners. Color: **#E8A87C** at full opacity — the most saturated blush application in her expression range. Placement is OUTER CHEEK, not extending toward the nose. The blush circles are pressed upward by the wide smile but remain contained to the lateral cheek zone. This placement distinguishes Reckless Excitement from the Guilty Sheepishness blush (see Expression 4) which spreads toward the nose bridge. **Do not extend this blush toward the nose in this expression — if it touches the nose bridge, it reads as Guilty, not Excited.**
- Nose: no change.
- Hair: slightly elevated, one additional flyaway escapes from the top.

**The feeling:** You want to both high-five her and back away slowly. This expression is pure forward momentum.

---

### Expression 2: FOCUSED DETERMINATION
**Emotional state:** She's locked onto a target. For once, the impulse energy is channeled. She knows what she needs to do.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: narrowed — the upper eyelid comes down to roughly 70% aperture, creating a slight squint. But the lower lid does NOT rise — the narrowing is all from above. This reads as "calculating" rather than "angry."
- Eyebrows: pulled strongly inward and slightly downward at the medial ends. The inner ends meet over the nose bridge, creating a single connected line — a strong mono-brow shape in graphic terms. Outer ends remain relatively level. Classic determination brow.
- Mouth: lips pressed together, corners pulled back in a flat line — not a smile, not a frown. Jaw set. The line of the mouth is straighter than any other expression.
- Cheeks: no blush.
- Hair: pulled back, the mass compresses slightly and settles — fewer flyaways. She's in the zone.

**The feeling:** Don't get in her way. She's already figured it out. She just hasn't told you yet.

---

### Expression 3: GENUINE SURPRISE/SHOCK
**Emotional state:** She did NOT see that coming. Pure unfiltered reaction — no filter, no cool.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: at maximum aperture — pupils shrink to dots (roughly 40% of normal size), leaving a lot of iris and eye white visible. The upper and lower eyelids both pull away from the iris, showing white above AND below the iris. This is rare and signals extreme surprise.
- Eyebrows: shoot upward at maximum height — practically at the hairline. They also widen slightly, increasing space between them.
- Mouth: drops fully open — the jaw hangs. The mouth is an open oval/rectangle shape, no teeth visible initially (the inside of the mouth is a deep shadow — **Deep Cocoa (#3B2820)** fill). In extreme versions, teeth visible.
- Cheeks: no blush — all blood has gone to the adrenaline system.
- Hair: the whole mass puffs upward and outward. Static electricity effect. Pixel confetti escapes from the tips.
- Optional bonus: a bead of sweat flying off the temple (a teardrop-shaped graphic shape in **Electric Cyan (#00F0FF)** to give it a digital feel).

**The feeling:** She saw a ghost. Or Byte did something particularly unexpected. Or both.

---

### Expression 4: GUILTY SHEEPISHNESS
**Emotional state:** Luma has caused a problem and knows it. She's not quite sorry — more like she's hoping you won't notice, while clearly knowing you will.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: partially closed — upper eyelid drops to 60% aperture, bottom lid rises slightly. The gaze tilts slightly downward and to one side (viewer's left — her right). She can't quite make eye contact.
- Eyebrows: the classic "oh no" configuration. Inner ends rise steeply while outer ends drop — creating a diagonal slant from high-inner to low-outer on each brow. This is the brow shape of guilt, worry, and mild apology.
- Mouth: a wide, forced grin with teeth visible — but the grin is too wide and too symmetrical to be genuine. The grin of someone who has knocked something over and is hoping the smile cancels it out. Teeth clenched together, visible as a row.
- Cheeks: **GUILTY SHEEPISHNESS BLUSH — MEDIUM INTENSITY, CHEEKS + BRIDGE OF NOSE.** An elongated form that covers both cheeks AND spreads inward to meet at the nose bridge — a blush that "connects" across the face. Color: **#E8A87C** at approximately 60% opacity (lower intensity than Reckless Excitement, which is full opacity). The blush shape is wider and flatter, extending from the outer cheek across the lower nose bridge. This connected, nose-crossing spread is the key visual differentiator from Reckless Excitement. **Do NOT use round isolated cheek circles here — the form must spread toward and across the nose bridge. If the blush does not reach the nose, it reads as excitement rather than shame.** The reduced intensity (60% vs. full) combined with the wider spread creates a "flushed" read rather than a "glowing" read.
- Head tilt: approximately 15 degrees to the side (the guilty head tilt — universal across species apparently).
- Hair: one prominent escaped curl droops over her forehead, like her own hair is facepalming.

**The feeling:** She did the thing. She knows she did the thing. She would like to be somewhere else right now.

---

### Expression 5: GENUINE SADNESS/WORRY
**Emotional state:** Something actually reached her. Luma doesn't stay sad for long, but in the moment it's completely real and unperformed.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: drooping — upper eyelid falls to 55% aperture. Both eyelids curve differently: the upper lid forms a longer, flatter arc (less curve at the top), while the lower lid gains a slight upward curve in the center (the trembling lower lip of the eyes). Iris shifts slightly downward in the eye socket.
- Eyebrows: inner ends raise (the universal distress brow), creating upward-angled brows with pulled inner ends. The brow crease mark — a small graphic line between the brows at the nose bridge — appears.
- Mouth: corners pull downward and inward. The mouth line droops into a soft frown — not a cartoonish downward curve, but a subtle, honest droop. The lower lip may be indicated as a slightly thicker line at the bottom of the mouth shape.
- Nose: may show a slight scrunch — the apostrophe becomes a slightly more compressed shape.
- Cheeks: no blush, but the cheek form itself reads as softer, more compressed by the sad mouth shape.
- Eyes glistening: the highlight dot increases in size and gains a second smaller highlight below it — the classic "about to cry" shiny eye. If tears are shown, they begin at the inner corner of the eye and trail down as simple teardrop shapes.
- Hair: droops. The hair mass loses volume and settles on either side of the head. Fewer flyaways. The escaped curl by the ear droops further.

**The feeling:** She actually cares. And right now, caring hurts. Give her a minute.

---

### Expression 6: MISCHIEVOUS SCHEMING
**Emotional state:** A plan has formed. It is a bad plan. She is extremely excited about it. She has not consulted Cosmo.

**Facial muscle breakdown:**
- Eyes: half-lidded — upper eyelid drops to 65% aperture, creating a "hooded" look. The gaze is directed slightly downward and toward the camera, as if she's looking at her own scheming thoughts. The half-lidded eye is the scheming eye universally.
- Eyebrows: one eyebrow raises higher than the other — the asymmetric brow is crucial here. Right brow (viewer's left) arches sharply upward; left brow (viewer's right) remains low or slightly furrowed. The asymmetry signals cunning.
- Mouth: the classic sideways smirk — one corner of the mouth pulls upward, the other stays level or drops slightly. The raised corner shows a glimpse of the upper teeth on that side only.
- Nose: no change.
- Head tilt: slight — approximately 10 degrees in the direction of the raised eyebrow.
- One hand: in this expression, the action pose often accompanies it — one finger raised, or hands steepled (in close-up).
- Hair: a single curl near the forehead spirals more tightly — it's excited too.

**The feeling:** Whatever she's planning is going to work, be dangerous, and require everyone to run afterward.

## 10. ACTION POSE

### Pose: "Mid-Leap, Arms Out, About to Grab Something"

**Description:** Luma is caught at the peak of a reckless jump — her body angled forward at approximately 30 degrees from vertical, leaning into her momentum. This is the pose that defines her — she's already committed to a decision and is now in the air dealing with the consequences.

**Body positioning:**
- The core of the body (torso) leans forward at 30 degrees from vertical.
- Lead leg (right, viewer's left) is extended forward and downward — she's about to land on it.
- Trail leg (left, viewer's right) kicks up and back at roughly 80 degrees from vertical — the back foot kicks her hoodie hem slightly. This creates a strong diagonal across the body.
- Arms extend outward — the lead arm (left, viewer's right) reaches fully forward as if about to grab or point. The trailing arm (right, viewer's left) opens outward and slightly back for balance.
- Head is upright relative to the body (not tilted with the torso lean) — she's looking forward at her target.

**Expression in this pose:** Reckless Excitement (Expression 1) — the signature grin.

**Hair behavior:** The hair trails behind the motion — the mass shifts backward from the momentum. Individual curls trail. One or two pixel confetti particles stream from the hair tips.

**Foot/hands:** Both hands in mitten-hand form. Feet are the large oval sneakers — in this pose, the lead foot's sole is visible (showing the Terracotta sole and Cyan laces in cross-section from below).

**Line of action:** A strong C-curve runs from the rear raised foot, through the spine, to the reaching lead arm. This C-curve is the structural foundation of the pose and must be maintained. Without it the pose looks like she's just falling, not leaping.

**Pixel confetti:** Small cyan square particles trail from her hoodie, particularly from the pixel pattern area on the sleeves. Approximately 6-8 particles, each a different glitch color. This implies she's near glitch energy or has just touched something digital.

## 11. DESIGN NOTES — DOS AND DON'TS

### What Makes Luma Work
- The HAIR is the first thing you see. It must always be slightly wilder than feels "right." If it looks tame, make it bigger.
- The HOODIE is her soul. The pixel pattern must never be reduced to the point of illegibility. If she's too small for the pattern to render, the pattern becomes a simple abstract texture that hints at the grid — but never disappears entirely.
- Her FEET are the second grounding element (after the hair). They must always look solid and planted — even when she's in the air, the feet look like they want to find ground.
- The color contrast between her warm orange hoodie and her dark near-black hair is her primary visual identity at a distance. Protect this contrast.
- She must always feel like she's moving TOWARD something, even at rest. Even in a neutral pose, weight should be on the forward foot, body slightly angled into an imaginary future.

### What to Avoid
- **DO NOT** make her hair symmetrical. Ever. Not even in back view.
- **DO NOT** use a straight mouth line as her default. Even in neutral she has a gentle upward curve — she defaults to "about to smile."
- **DO NOT** draw her in a perfectly symmetrical front-facing pose in animation. It's only for the model sheet. In motion she always has a slight lean, twist, or asymmetry.
- **DO NOT** simplify the pixel pattern into a single color or solid texture. The multi-color nature of the grid is essential to communicating the glitch world's intrusion into her life.
- **DO NOT** make her eyes small. If they read as small at any model sheet scale, increase them until they're large. Her expressiveness lives there.
- **DO NOT** draw her shoulders wide. Her shoulder width being narrower than her head is intentional and gives her the "big head" approachability of a children's character. Widening the shoulders makes her look older and more serious.
- **DO NOT** add detail to the hoodie beyond the described fold lines. Two or three lines. Maximum. Resist the urge to add seam details, pocket edging lines, logo details, or texture. The graphic simplicity is the point.

### Silhouette Test — Explicit Thumbnail Analysis

This section documents the silhouette test results at three scales. Every claim is verified against the described design — not assumed.

**Test methodology:** Render Luma as a solid black shape with no color, no line detail, no texture. Observe what reads at each size.

#### Thumbnail Scale (character approximately 50px tall — the most demanding test)

At this scale, the following forms are identifiable, in reading order:

1. **The hair cloud** — reads FIRST and STRONGEST. A large triangular-to-cloud mass surmounting the head. It occupies approximately 35% of the total visual height and extends wider than the body below it. The asymmetry (right side heavier) adds a directional energy even at this scale. **Result: DISTINCT and READABLE. No other character has this.**

2. **The head** — visible as a circular shape beneath the hair mass. At thumbnail size the head and hair partially merge into a single large top-heavy form. This is acceptable and actually amplifies the "big-headed" appeal read of the character.

3. **The torso/hoodie** — reads as a rounded rectangular mass below the head. It is slightly wider than the legs below it and slightly narrower than the hair above. The width relationship (hair wider than torso wider than legs) creates a clear top-heavy triangle-of-masses reading. **Result: reads as a solid, approachable torso form.**

4. **The legs** — at thumbnail size the two legs merge into a single stubby column form. This is expected and acceptable — the legs read as "short and stable," which is correct character information. Individual leg separation is visible at medium scale but not required at thumbnail.

5. **The feet** — the slightly oversized duck-footed ovals read as a small but definite base at the bottom of the silhouette. They are wider than the legs above them, creating a stable bottom to the shape. **Result: the silhouette has a clear visual base — she looks planted.**

6. **The overall silhouette read:** Hair cloud → rounded body → stable feet. Top-heavy, rounded, grounded. Reads as a compact, energetic child character. **CONFIRMED READABLE.**

#### Challenge Cases

**Challenge 1: Luma's silhouette versus a background with similarly dark-toned elements.**
At thumbnail size, the hair mass must maintain separation from dark backgrounds. Primary protection: the hair occupies the top of frame in most shots, where backgrounds are typically lighter. Secondary protection: the hair's width extends beyond the shoulder/body below it — creating a distinctive hourglass-adjacent silhouette that reads even with partial contrast.

**Challenge 2: Luma's silhouette in the lineup next to Cosmo.**
Luma's hair must not merge into Cosmo's head at thumbnail. Because Cosmo's head is a tall rectangle and Luma's hair is a wide cloud, they maintain different spatial footprints — her horizontal spread does not overlap his vertical reach. **Confirmed: silhouettes remain distinct in side-by-side placement.**

**Challenge 3: Action pose at thumbnail size.**
In the "mid-leap" action pose, the extended arm and raised rear leg break the compact body silhouette. At thumbnail, the extended arm reads as a directional gesture (reaching). The raised rear leg creates the C-curve line-of-action that reads as "jumping." These breakaway elements actually improve thumbnail readability by increasing the silhouette's kinetic complexity.

**FINAL VERDICT: Luma passes the silhouette test at all scales. The hair cloud is the character's primary silhouette hook and it reads at thumbnail. Color adds warmth and detail, but she is fully identifiable without it.**

## 11B. HAIR REACTIVITY TIMING

This section specifies exact frame counts (at 24fps) for each of Luma's hair reactive states. Hair reactivity is a secondary animation layer — it supplements expression and should not overpower it. Use sparingly as a punctuation mark.

### Surprise / Fear
- **Spike up:** 4-frame sharp rise. The hair mass lifts and the curls extend upward. This is a hard, fast action — not eased.
- **Hold:** 8 frames at maximum elevation. Curls are extended, flyaways at maximum spread.
- **Settle:** 12-frame ease back to resting position. The settle should have a slight overshoot on frames 10-11 (hair dips 5-10% below resting before returning) — giving it a bounce. Total cycle: 24 frames (4+8+12).
- **Shape at hold:** Hair mass increases height by approximately 20% above neutral. Curls straighten partially — spiral tightness reduces. 2-3 additional flyaways appear at the mass tips.

### Excitement (Reckless / Sustained State)
- **Activation:** 4-frame rise into excited mode (same as Surprise spike).
- **Active loop:** Continuous 2-frame oscillation while the excited state persists. Each of the 5 curls bounces independently — they do NOT move in unison. **Offset stagger: Curl 1 leads, Curl 2 starts 4 frames later, Curl 3 starts 4 frames after Curl 2, etc.** The 4-frame offset per curl means the overall hair mass ripples rather than bobs. The bounce amplitude per curl is approximately 8-10% of the curl's length.
- **Deactivation:** 8-frame ease back to resting when the excited state ends.
- **Note:** Excitement oscillation is smaller in scale than the Surprise spike — the hair does not fully extend upward. It is a living, vibrating state rather than a single dramatic action.

### Calm (Idle / Neutral)
- **Movement type:** 24-frame slow drift. The hair mass shifts very slightly from side to side (or lightly forward/back) on a long, slow cycle.
- **Amplitude:** Minimal — approximately 2-3% of hair mass width. Barely perceptible but prevents the hair from looking completely static in idle scenes.
- **Curl movement:** Near-zero. Individual curls do not bounce. The mass moves as a whole, very gently.
- **Purpose:** Indicates the character is breathing and present, not frozen. This is life, not animation.

### Glitch Proximity (Near Glitch Source)
- **Behavior:** Individual curls rotate to point toward the nearest glitch source. The curls do not snap — they track with a **6-frame tracking delay**. This means if a glitch source moves, each curl follows 6 frames behind. The overall effect is that the hair "feels" the glitch field and adjusts like a compass needle — but slowly, as if the magnetic attraction is subtle.
- **Tracking range:** Curls redirect up to 45° from resting position. They cannot fully point backward without distorting the hair silhouette unacceptably.
- **Pixel confetti:** When in glitch proximity state, 1-2 tiny pixel confetti squares escape from the curl tips (per frame, cycling through glitch palette colors). This is a secondary FX indicator, not required in budget-constrained shots.
- **Deactivation:** When the glitch source is removed, curls drift back to resting position over 12 frames using the same 4-frame stagger as the Excitement oscillation.

### Cross-Reference
These timing specs pair with the hair shape descriptions in Section 5 (Emotional Reactivity). Section 5 describes WHAT the hair does; this section specifies WHEN and HOW FAST.

## 12. DESIGN RATIONALE SUMMARY

Luma's design is built around the tension between **safety and velocity**. Every rounded edge says "safe, friendly, approachable." But the proportions — short legs, big feet, slightly top-heavy head — say "unstable, kinetic, about to move." She is a bowling ball that talks.

The warm orange of the hoodie was chosen to be the most optimistic, forward-facing color in the palette — it's the color of a sunrise, a campfire, a friendly warning sign. It says "I'm here and I'm doing things." The dark hair creates maximum contrast against it, ensuring she's never lost in any background.

The pixel hoodie pattern is the emotional center of the character's visual concept: a kid who accidentally got entangled with a digital world, wearing evidence of it without fully understanding what it means. The pattern is imperfect, slightly glitched — just like her approach to the situation.

She should look like the kind of kid who could be YOUR best friend and YOUR biggest problem, simultaneously, every day.

## 13. EYE ASYMMETRY MECHANISM

Luma's eye asymmetry is not decorative — it is a structural expression system. Her **left eye is the lead eye**: at rest (Neutral/Resting state), the left eye is fractionally more open than the right (approximately 2 px aperture difference at panel scale), establishing the left eye as Luma's dominant expressive instrument by default. As emotional intensity increases, the asymmetry amplifies: in Reckless Excitement the left eye opens wider while the right receives a shifted pupil (screen-right offset), in Recognition the left brow rises sky-high while the right brow stays level, and in Mischievous Plotting the entire left brow launches above the resting arc while the right brow nearly vanishes behind the squinted left lid. The mechanism works in the opposite direction for negative states — in Worried/Determined, both eyes are forced toward symmetric tension and the asymmetry compresses nearly to zero, communicating that emotional extremity overrides personality quirks. In practice this means that **symmetric Luma reads as maximal stress or shutdown**; the degree of readable left-right eye difference is a real-time register of how much of herself she's expressing. Animators should treat the left eye aperture and brow height as the primary dials: every +10% asymmetry toward the left eye reads as +1 unit of Luma's characteristic reckless personality coming through.
