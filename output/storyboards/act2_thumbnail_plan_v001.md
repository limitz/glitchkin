## Act One Beats (After Title Card — Post-Cold-Open)

These beats establish Luma's normal world before the episode's main escalation.

---

### A1-01: MORNING — GRANDMA MIRI'S KITCHEN
**Shot type:** WIDE — Establishing interior
**Emotional temperature:** Warm, comedic normality
**Beat description:**
Daylight. Kitchen bathed in amber. Grandma Miri is at the stove making tea — seen from behind, large rounded-square silhouette, silver hair, vintage patterned housecoat. Luma is at the kitchen table, face still crumpled from sleep, cheese puff residue visible in her hair. Her notebook (the history project) is open. She is trying to write. Byte is NOT visible — he is hiding behind the sugar bowl, watching Miri with visible suspicion.

**Key visual:** Miri's knowing expression as she pours tea without turning around. She KNOWS something happened last night. She says nothing yet.

**Compositional anchor:** Miri centered-left (warm, grounded). Luma centered-right (messy, off-balance). Byte visible only if you look at the sugar bowl — plant him for rewatchers.

---

### A1-02: CU — THE NOTEBOOK PROJECT
**Shot type:** INSERT — Object CU
**Emotional temperature:** Low stakes, character-establishing
**Beat description:**
Tight on Luma's notebook. The history project is hopeless — crossed out passages, margins filled with drawings, the last sentence still mid-word from the night before. But now there are NEW margin drawings: hurried sketches of Glitchkin shapes from memory. Byte's silhouette appears three times in different poses. A small diagram: "HOW DID IT GET OUT???"

**Key visual:** The history project text is barely visible under layers of Luma's real mental activity. Story priority: her mind goes to the Glitchkin, not the homework.

---

### A1-03: SCHOOL — HALLWAY MCU
**Shot type:** MCU — Two shot
**Emotional temperature:** Social anxiety / excitement
**Beat description:**
Millbrook Middle School. Cosmo is at his locker. He has a new item: a handmade organizational spreadsheet taped inside the door. Luma arrives at a jog, grabs his arm, begins whispering urgently. Cosmo's expression goes through four stages in two seconds: confused → skeptical → alarmed → calculating. He opens his notebook to a fresh page. Byte, hidden in Luma's hoodie pocket, PEEKS OUT just enough to see Cosmo, then pulls back.

**Key visual:** The contrast between Cosmo's orderly spreadsheet and Luma's chaos is the visual thesis of their relationship. Byte as a secret object — the pocket must feel inhabited.

---

### A1-04: SCIENCE CLASS — WIDE SHOT
**Shot type:** WIDE — Interior classroom
**Emotional temperature:** Pressure / distraction
**Beat description:**
Standard classroom. Students at desks. Luma is in the back, trying to listen, but her notebook is open to the Glitchkin sketches. Byte has made a nest in her desk tray from eraser bits and is napping. Teacher (Ms. Okafor — warm, sharp, she NOTICES things) is writing on the board: "BINARY SYSTEMS — HOW COMPUTERS STORE DATA." The irony is visible.

**Key visual:** The lesson content is exactly what Luma needs to understand Byte. She is not paying attention to it.

**Background detail:** The classroom's overhead projector has a subtle pixel flicker. No one notices except Luma.

---

### A1-05: ACT ONE BREAK — GLITCH INCIDENT #1
**Shot type:** MED — Hallway
**Emotional temperature:** Jump from normal to WRONG
**Beat description:**
Between classes. A vending machine near Luma's locker starts dispensing items randomly, unprompted. Water bottles, chip bags, and one very confused action figure. The machine's screen glitches — Luma can see brief cyan flashes on the display that read like Glitchkin shapes. Nobody else notices the digital component — they just see a broken vending machine.

Byte pokes out of her pocket, alarmed. He knows what this is.

**Key visual:** Vending machine as the mundane object that signals the world is cracking. The action figure landing on the floor is the comedic punctuation — right at Cosmo's feet.

## Act Two Beats — Escalation Arc

**Act Two emotional arc:** WORRIED → INVESTIGATING → WRONG PLAN → WORSE → EMOTIONAL PEAK

---

### A2-01: GRANDMA MIRI'S HOUSE — AFTER SCHOOL
**Shot type:** WIDE — Tech den — INT
**Emotional temperature:** Investigative urgency
**Beat description:**
Back in the tech den. Now in daylight, the chaos is MORE visible — small glitch artifacts on every surface. Surfaces near the monitors have cyan pixel-dither contamination. The fish tank (background prop, never featured) has a GLITCHKIN INSIDE IT — a small one, translucent, pressing against the glass. Cosmo is mapping the room with his notebook. Luma is trying to communicate with Byte. Byte is being deliberately unhelpful.

**Key visual:** The fish tank Glitchkin — small, scared, not dangerous. This is the show's first hint that Glitchkin are not all threats — some are just lost.

---

### A2-02: BYTE EXPOSITION — OTS TWO-SHOT
**Shot type:** MCU — Luma + Byte eye level
**Emotional temperature:** Strained negotiation
**Beat description:**
Luma at eye level with Byte (he is on the coffee table, she is crouched). He is explaining something. His expression is 'resigned' — he does not want to be doing this. Luma's expression is intense focus. Cosmo is visible in background, writing furiously.

**Key visual:** Byte's cracked eye flickers during explanation — whatever he is describing is something that costs him to say. The show's first glimpse of his real vulnerability.

**Speech direction:** Byte's speech bubbles should use pixelated font. When his cracked eye flickers, the speech bubble glitches — letters scramble for one frame.

---

### A2-03: THE WRONG PLAN — INTERIOR WIDE
**Shot type:** WIDE — Tech den
**Emotional temperature:** False confidence / setup for failure
**Beat description:**
Cosmo has a plan on a whiteboard (pulled from somewhere — he carries one). It is extremely elaborate. Luma is enthusiastic. Byte is expressing maximum skepticism (arms crossed, lean away from the board, one brow up). The plan involves: a cardboard box trap, Luma's energy drink cans as "signal lures," and Cosmo's phone with a "Glitch Frequency" app he is developing in real time.

**Key visual:** The whiteboard plan is visually beautiful — color-coded, organized — and is clearly going to fail immediately. It is a portrait of well-intentioned overconfidence.

---

### A2-04: PLAN IN ACTION — SEQUENCE WIDE
**Shot type:** WIDE → MED (three panels)
**Emotional temperature:** Comic escalation
**Beat descriptions:**

**A2-04a:** Trap set. Cardboard box propped on a stick. Energy drink cans glowing faintly cyan (they are now contaminated — Cosmo hasn't noticed). Luma and Cosmo are hiding behind the couch. Byte is on the mantelpiece, refusing to participate.

**A2-04b:** The Glitchkin they are trying to catch IGNORES the trap entirely and walks past it. But FOUR NEW Glitchkin follow it in from somewhere — from the electrical outlet in the wall. They emerge in single file. They are curious about the energy drink cans.

**A2-04c:** The energy drink cans, now in contact with multiple Glitchkin simultaneously, all pop open at once. Carbonated spray everywhere. The fish tank Glitchkin panics and phases THROUGH the glass (the glass becomes briefly transparent/digital). The cardboard box trap falls.

---

### A2-05: SECOND GLITCH INCIDENT — EXTERIOR
**Shot type:** MED WIDE — Millbrook street
**Emotional temperature:** Stakes climbing — the world is affected
**Beat description:**
Outside. Daylight (the timeline is still afternoon). Millbrook street. A STREETLIGHT is flickering in the Glitchkin's visual signature — not random, it is flickering in a PATTERN. Luma reads it as some kind of signal. A DOG at the far end of the block is staring directly at something invisible. Pixel confetti is visible in the air near the street lamp.

**Key visual:** The scale shift — glitch is no longer just inside Grandma Miri's house. It is on the street. The ordinary town becoming extraordinary.

---

### A2-06: COSMO'S PLAN FAILS — MED
**Shot type:** MED — Two-shot at Cosmo's locker
**Emotional temperature:** Low stakes failure — but the emotional cost is real
**Beat description:**
Cosmo's "Glitch Frequency" app does not work as designed. His phone shows a mess of interference. He closes the notebook. For one panel, his face shows genuine disappointment — not panic, not dramatics — just the quiet of a person who prepared carefully and it did not matter.

Luma puts her hand on his shoulder. She does not say "it's okay" because she knows that is not what he needs.

**Key visual:** The emotional truth of this beat is the close-up of the notebook closing. The plan is done. The visual must hold the weight of that without over-selling it.

---

### A2-07: BYTE'S PARTIAL CONFESSION — ECU
**Shot type:** ECU — Byte's face (Luma's POV)
**Emotional temperature:** Quiet tension — the show's emotional peak for Act Two
**Beat description:**
Byte has been watching the failed plan. He says something. His cracked eye is fully open — glitching. What he says is incomplete — but it reveals he knows MORE about the Corruption and what is causing the Glitchkin to leak through. He is not ready to tell the whole truth.

**Key visual:** The ECU makes his cracked eye the center of the frame. The dead-pixel effect is now clearly more complex — it is not random, it is a WORD or a symbol that flickers in the break. Luma sees it. She does not press him. This is the moment her trust of him becomes a choice, not a default.

**Note to art director:** This panel is the emotional spine of the episode. Whatever visual choices we make here will echo through the season.

---

### A2-08: ACT TWO BREAK — GRANDMA MIRI RETURNS
**Shot type:** WIDE — Front door / hallway
**Emotional temperature:** Gentle dread — the adult is back
**Beat description:**
Grandma Miri opens the front door. She has a bag of groceries. She surveys the scene — energy drink spray on the ceiling, Glitchkin artifacts on every surface, two children and a tiny digital creature staring at her.

She sets the groceries down. She puts on the kettle. She sits in her chair.

"Tell me what happened. All of it."

**Key visual:** Miri's shape language — rounded, solid, calm. She is not angry. She is not surprised. She has been waiting for this. Her reaction is a visual promise to the audience: this adult is an ally.
