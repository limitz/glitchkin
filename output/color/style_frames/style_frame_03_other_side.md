## Frame Identity

**Frame title:** The Other Side
**Episode position:** Act 3 of a major arc episode — the moment Luma has fully crossed into the Glitch Layer and has her first moment to simply look at it. The action has paused. There is space to breathe and *see*.
**Narrative function:** The world-building promise frame. This is the image that tells the audience: this world is vast, strange, beautiful, and dangerous, and we are only beginning to understand it. It is also a quiet character moment — Luma, alone with Byte on her shoulder, confronting the full scale of the world she has chosen to engage with. No running. No crisis. Just looking.
**Mood:** Quiet awe. The feeling after your breath has been taken away and you haven't found it yet. Vast, still, and full of a kind of beauty that has no human name.

## Pixel Confetti — Governing Physics Rule (Established in Frame 01, Applied Here)

**The governing physics rule for pixel confetti across the entire show:**

> Pixel confetti originates from active glitch sources. Near the source: particles are largest (4-15px depending on source intensity), densest, and have an upward or outward drift bias (pushed by the source energy). As distance from the source increases: particles slow, diminish in size (minimum 1-2px at far range), and settle toward neutral drift or gentle downward fall under gravity. A particle that has landed on a surface is still — it does not re-animate without a new source impulse. Confetti does not orbit, maintain elevation without a source, or cluster in mid-air without a nearby active emission point.

**This frame's confetti application:**
- Source: The platform itself (circuit traces, always-active Glitch Layer ambient)
- This is NOT an event-driven emission (not a storm, not a portal opening) — it is the background radiation of the Glitch Layer itself. The Glitch Layer always emits at a low ambient level.
- Therefore: confetti here is sparse, small, and slow. This is not Frame 02's maximum-density storm confetti. This is background level.
- Particles are approximately 1-3px, slow drift, predominantly falling-upward at very low velocity (slight anti-gravity in the Glitch Layer — a quirk of this world's physics, not a violation of the governing rule).
- Many particles have already settled: on the platform surface, on Luma's left shoulder (she hasn't noticed), on her hair.
- Confetti colors: `#00F0FF`, `#F0F0F0`, `#39FF14`, `#2B7FFF` — all glitch palette, no warm colors. Acid Green is appropriate here because it is associated with the Glitch Layer's ambient biological energy (digital flora, data-moss), not storm/corruption energy.
- **Cross-frame consistency:** Frame 01 = emerging, sparse (source is newly active CRT). Frame 02 = maximum density (active storm, enormous source). Frame 03 = settled ambient (background emission, no active event). The three frames form a consistent physics continuum.

## Composition

### Framing
- **Aspect:** 16:9 widescreen
- **Shot type:** Wide shot with strong foreground element. Luma is visible full-body in the lower-left, but is small — perhaps 1/5 of the frame height. The Glitch Layer extends out behind and below and above her, consuming 4/5 of the frame.
- **Rule of thirds:** Luma is positioned at the left third-line, just below the lower horizontal third — she is in the "gravity" zone, slightly below center. The horizon of the Glitch Layer (the point where the distant structures converge in the far void) is on the upper horizontal third. The vertical space between Luma (lower-left) and the vanishing horizon (upper-right) is the frame's central journey: from the known (one person on a platform) to the unknown (an infinite digital universe).
- **No dutch angle here:** The composition is level. After the chaos of the Glitch Storm frame, this stillness is meaningful. The level horizon says: the world is not falling apart. It is simply *different*.

### Depth Layers — Designed as a Recession Study

The primary job of this composition is to communicate scale through depth. Every layer must clearly read as farther away than the last, using atmospheric perspective in the Glitch palette idiom.

**Layer 1 — Extreme Foreground (lower portion, closest):**
- The platform edge that Luma stands on. Just the front lip and a stretch of platform surface — a slab of dark geometric material with circuit-trace patterns.
- Platform color: `#0A0A14` (Void Black) base, `#00F0FF` (Electric Cyan) circuit traces, `#2B7FFF` (Data Stream Blue) secondary lines
- Pixel-art plants growing from cracks: `#39FF14` (Acid Green) geometric succulent-like forms; small, approachable, alive
- Plant shadow undersides: `#1AA800` (Dark Acid Green — GL-03a) — the shadow companion to Acid Green
- Plant top faces receive `#00F0FF` bounce light from the circuit traces (a faint cyan highlight on their upper surfaces)
- A few `#F0F0F0` pixel confetti particles resting on the platform surface — recently landed, still

**Layer 2 — Midground (the immediate environment around Luma):**
- Luma and Byte on the platform
- Floating geometric sub-platforms at varying heights near her — stepping-stone distance, navigable
- One data-stream waterfall (columns of scrolling `#2B7FFF` code) descends a few meters to Luma's right — close enough to be detailed, far enough to be safely distant
- These elements are at full-detail level, maximum saturation, sharpest edges

**Layer 3 — Mid-distance (middle portion of frame):**
- A cluster of larger floating structures — some are abstract geometry (hexagonal slabs, broken cubes, tessellated planes). Some are recognizable corrupted Real World objects: a fragment of a building wall (terracotta `#C75B39` now cracked and glitch-infected at the edges), a lamppost, a fragment of road surface.
- These corrupted Real World fragments carry `#FF8C00` (Corrupted Amber) glow at their edges — they are in transition, partly here, partly there
- Detail level: 60% relative to foreground. Edges slightly softened using blue-purple atmospheric haze

**Layer 4 — Far distance (upper half of frame, dissolving toward the void):**
- Enormous structures at a scale that contextualizes everything: vast flat geometric planes, data-continent shapes, geometric megastructures that tower in the mid-distance
- Detail level: 30%. Colors shift toward `#7B2FBE` (UV Purple) as the atmospheric haze increases — the inverted atmospheric perspective of the Glitch Layer
- Multiple data-stream waterfalls visible at this distance — thin, pale `#2B7FFF` lines
- Distant Glitchkin, barely visible as tiny bright dots of `#39FF14` and `#00F0FF` moving between structures

**Layer 5 — Sky/Void (upper quarter, infinity):**
- The void itself. `#0A0A14` (Void Black) at the very upper edge, transitioning through `#7B2FBE` in the middle depth, to a faint `#2B2050` at the layer-4 transition
- Distant static points of `#F0F0F0` at 1px scale, randomly distributed. Not a real sky, but the void has these light-artifacts that echo the idea of stars. They are corrupted pixel noise, not celestial bodies.
- Impossible geometry visible at void-scale: an enormous circular ring structure (like a corrupted storage device rendered at planetary scale) in the far, far distance — visible as a `#7B2FBE` outline shape, barely distinguishable from the void

### Character Positioning
**Luma:**
- Standing at the very front edge of the platform. Her toes are at the edge — a slightly reckless position.
- Pose: Her weight is back (she has approached the edge and instinctively pulled back slightly), her arms are at her sides with her hands slightly raised — the body language of someone bracing themselves to look at something too large to fully comprehend.
- She is looking forward and slightly up — toward the middle-distance where the vast structures begin.
- Her silhouette must read perfectly against the mid-distance Glitch background. The saturated orange of her hoodie vs. the blue-purple void mid-distance is the primary silhouette contrast.
- Her face is in three-quarter view toward the viewer — we can see her expression, but only partly. We see one wide eye, the set of her jaw, the faintest hint of her mouth slightly open. She has not decided what she feels yet.
- Expression: An emotion that doesn't have a single name — something like grief-adjacent wonder. She has left her warm world. She is looking at the world that threatens everything she loves. And it is extraordinary. And she is going to have to fight it. All of that is in her face right now.

**Byte:**
- On Luma's right shoulder, gripping the collar of her hoodie.
- His body is turned toward the same view — looking out with her. He has seen all of this before. He knows what it is. He is watching her see it.
- His pose: still, settled, neither frightened nor awed. A creature returning to a world it was born in. But there is something in his stillness that is not entirely comfortable — he is back in the Glitch Layer, and his cracked eye (the `#FF2D6B` eye) is the one facing the deepest void.
- His `#00F0FF` eye faces toward Luma's warmth; his `#FF2D6B` eye faces the void. This is intentional and meaningful.
- At this scale (Luma is 1/5 of frame height; Byte is 1/10), he is a small shape — but his two different colored eyes must be visible.
- **Byte visibility note:** Unlike the storm scene (Frame 02), the Glitch Layer ambient is UV Purple, not Cyan-dominant. Byte's Void Black base reads more distinctly against the purple ambient than against a Cyan ambient. The Corrupted Amber outline exception (from Frame 02) does NOT apply here — the base contrast is adequate. Byte reads as a small dark form with cyan glow-edges against purple space.

## Lighting Breakdown

### Primary Light Source — The Void Ambient
- **Color:** `#7B2FBE` (UV Purple) — the ambient light of the Glitch Layer is not from a single source. It is everywhere and nowhere — a deep purple luminescence that emanates from the void itself.
- **Direction:** Omnidirectional ambient — slightly stronger from below (the abyss below the platforms glows more than the void above, because data structures emit light from below). Under-lighting is the default in the Glitch Layer.
- **Effect on Luma:** The purple ambient fills all horizontal surfaces (the top of her head, shoulders, the top of her hoodie) with a faint `#7B2FBE` tint. Her orange hoodie under UV Purple reads approximately as `#C07038` (DRW-14 — Glitch Layer Hoodie). Her skin becomes `#A87890` (DRW-11 — Glitch Layer Skin). See skin highlight variants below.
- **Effect on her skin:** `#A87890` — a delicate, slightly otherworldly lavender-skin tone. She looks like herself but also like she has been changed by being here.

### Secondary Light Source — Platform Glow (Circuit Light)
- **Color:** `#00F0FF` (Electric Cyan) — the platform's circuit traces emit their own light, which bounces up from the platform surface and illuminates Luma from below and slightly behind (her feet and lower legs, the underside of her arms at her sides)
- **Direction:** From directly below — bounce light up from the platform
- **Effect on Luma:** Her feet and ankles receive the most intense Cyan bounce. Moving up her body, it fades through `#00A8B4` (Deep Cyan) to negligible above the waist. This creates a gradient light effect on her lower half.

### Tertiary Light Source — Data Waterfall Blue Light
- **Color:** `#2B7FFF` (Data Stream Blue) — the code waterfall to her right casts a soft blue-white light across the right side of the scene
- **Direction:** From the right, slightly forward — the waterfall is a column, so the light emanates from a vertical line source
- **Effect on Luma:** A soft blue-white rim on her right shoulder and right side. Her hoodie's orange right-edge reads as `#9A7AA0` (DRW-16 — Shoulder Under Waterfall Blue) where the blue waterfall light mixes with the orange — the most complex color mixture in the frame, on her right shoulder blade.
- **Effect on platform:** The platform surface to Luma's right has a soft `#2B7FFF` light pool from the waterfall proximity

### The Warm Remnant
- **There is no warm light in this frame. That is the point.**
- Luma carries her warmth in her own colors — her orange `#E8703A` hoodie (modified to `#C07038` by UV ambient), her warm tan `#C4A882` skin (modified to `#A87890`), the warm cocoa `#3B2820` of her hair. All of those warm colors exist in this frame, but they are not *lit*. They survive as material color, not as light.
- In the Glitch Layer, warmth is something you carry from the other world. It is not given to you here.
- The absence of warm light in this frame is the loneliest thing about it, and it is the frame's most important emotional statement.

## Glitch Layer Skin Highlight Variants — Full System

**Problem (Cycle 1 gap):** The original frame spec defined skin modification for Luma (standard `#C4A882` warm tan base) but did not address how different warm-skin-tone characters should be rendered in the Glitch Layer. Grandma Miri, background human characters, and any future characters with different warm-skin bases will need Glitch Layer equivalents.

**Governing rule:** The UV Purple ambient modifies warm skin by adding a violet cast and slightly desaturating the orange/yellow warmth. The effect is: skin becomes more lavender-pink, less orange. The darker/cooler the original warm skin, the more dramatic the shift. A very light warm skin may read as nearly lavender in the Glitch Layer.

**Documented skin highlight variants for the Glitch Layer:**

| Character Type | Base Skin | Glitch Layer Base | Glitch Layer Shadow | Glitch Layer Highlight (platform bounce) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard warm tan (Luma, Cosmo) | `#C4A882` | `#A87890` (DRW-11) | `#5A3A5A` (DRW-12) | `#4AB0B0` (DRW-13) |
| Cooler warm tan (Grandma Miri, some background characters) | lighter/cooler variant | `#8A6A7A` (DRW-13b) | `#4A2A4A` | `#3A9898` |
| Darker warm skin (hypothetical future character) | `#9A7A58` (hypothetical) | `#845A72` | `#4A1A42` | `#3A8880` |

**Notes on the cooler variant (`#8A6A7A`):**
- Characters whose base skin sits in a slightly cooler warm-tan range (less orange, more pink-beige) will receive a more pronounced lavender shift under UV Purple ambient. The cooler the skin undertone, the more the UV ambient "wins" and pushes toward lavender.
- DRW-13b (`#8A6A7A`) is the documented cool-variant Glitch Layer skin. Painters should assess each character's base skin and determine whether they fall into the standard or cooler variant category.
- **Rule of thumb:** If the character's base skin reads as "warm orange-tan," use the standard system (DRW-11). If the character's base skin reads as "neutral to slightly cool tan," use DRW-13b.

**Skin highlights in the Glitch Layer:**
- The platform Cyan bounce provides a second highlight layer on top of the UV ambient base: `#4AB0B0` (DRW-13) on forehead, nose tip, cheekbone catches (i.e., the upward-facing planes that catch the under-bounce light).
- For cooler-skin characters: `#3A9898` (slightly dimmer cyan highlight — cooler bases absorb the bounce differently).
- There is NO warm highlight in this frame. Do not add warm highlights to skin in the Glitch Layer. If a surface reads as warm-highlighted, it is a Real World fragment or a painting error.

## The Color Story of This Frame

This frame is about **scale vs. self**, and color is the primary narrator.

**The warm-color inventory of this frame:**
- Luma's hoodie: `#E8703A` (modified by ambient light to `#C07038`)
- Luma's skin: `#C4A882` (modified by ambient to `#A87890`)
- The Corrupted Real World fragments: `#C75B39` terracotta with `#FF8C00` amber edges
- That is it. That is all the warmth in this frame.

Count it: three warm elements in a frame filled with cyan, purple, blue, and void black. And all three warm elements belong to Luma's world — her body, her clothes, and the broken pieces of the world the Glitch has already taken.

The color story says: you are here, Luma. Your warmth is here. But look how much cold there is. Look how vast the cold is. Look at what has already been consumed.

**Byte's two eyes:**
- `#00F0FF` (Cyan) eye facing Luma — facing warmth, facing the friend, facing hope.
- `#FF2D6B` (Magenta) eye facing the void — facing danger, facing what he was, facing what the Glitch is.

In this frame, his two eyes are literally pointed in opposite directions. His whole character in a single color detail.

**The absence of warm light:**
Luma carries warmth in her *pigment*, but no light source in this frame is warm. Her orange hoodie is lit by purple, blue, and cyan — it reflects those lights back, modified. She is not *surrounded* by warmth; she is carrying it. This is the difference between being at home and being away from home.

The frame argues: she can carry her warmth anywhere. Even here. But she will have to carry it herself.

## Technical Spec Notes

- Maximum dominant colors (in frame): 7 — Void Black `#0A0A14`, UV Purple `#7B2FBE`, Electric Cyan `#00F0FF`, Data Stream Blue `#2B7FFF`, Acid Green `#39FF14` (distant flora and pixel confetti), Warm Tan modified `#A87890` (Luma's skin), Hoodie Orange modified `#C07038`. Supporting: Static White `#F0F0F0` for void stars and confetti; Hot Magenta `#FF2D6B` for Byte's eye and ambient corrupted fragments; Corrupted Amber `#FF8C00` for Real World debris.
- Note: This frame has a longer supporting color list than the others because the Glitch Layer is more complex. But the 7 dominant colors still hold — no more than 7 should appear in any single major area of the frame.
- Character saturation check: Luma's modified orange `#C07038` is still substantially more saturated than any background element at equivalent distance. She reads in silhouette.
- No dutch angle — level horizon, deliberate stillness.
- Atmospheric perspective reminder: bright and saturated in the foreground, purple and dark in the mid-distance, near-void at infinity. This is the INVERSE of Real World atmospheric perspective. Make sure every artist on this frame knows the Glitch Layer atmospheric rule.
- Do not add warm light. There is no warm light in the Glitch Layer. If any element reads as warm, check whether it is intentionally a Real World fragment (acceptable) or whether it has accidentally picked up ambient light coloring (incorrect — fix it).
- Pixel confetti: governing physics rule applied — ambient/settled, not storm-density. Colors: `#00F0FF`, `#F0F0F0`, `#39FF14`, `#2B7FFF`. Acid Green confetti is appropriate here (associated with Glitch Layer biological ambient, not storm energy).
- Skin highlight variants: standard (`#A87890` base, `#4AB0B0` highlight) for Luma and Cosmo. Cooler-skin variant (`#8A6A7A`) available for other characters. See Glitch Layer Skin Highlight Variants section above.
