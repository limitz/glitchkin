## Overview

**Episode title:** "Dead Pixels"
**Runtime target:** 22 minutes
**Format:** Pre-credits tag + cold open + three acts + tag stinger
**Tone:** Warm. Reckless. Earned. Comedy-first; emotional landing is the reward for patience.

**Episode function in season:** Pilot. Establishes world, main cast, emotional architecture, and show promise. The pre-credits tag gives us who Luma is. The cold open gives us first contact. Acts 1–3 give us the show's engine: Luma's recklessness + Byte's reluctance + Cosmo's documentation, under genuine threat.

## Pre-Credits Tag (~30 seconds)

### Setting
Millbrook Middle School — classroom — last day of school before summer break. Morning light. End-of-year energy.

### Scene

A math class. Projector screen at the front: a presentation playing. The room is distracted in the soft way of students counting down to summer.

LUMA (12) is staring at the upper-right corner of the projector image. There is a single dead pixel — a tiny absence — pulsing at the edge of the bright image. She alone is watching it.

The TEACHER asks Luma a question — clearly expecting she isn't listening. Luma answers correctly, without looking away from the pixel.

COSMO (12), seated beside her, watches this exchange with the look of someone who has catalogued it many times. He writes something in his notebook.

Two rows back: DEV PATEL-HUANG (12) leans over to the kid beside him, says something. The kid shrugs. Dev's expression: fond bafflement, no malice.

In the hallway window behind them, PREETHI OKAFOR (13) passes — heading somewhere. She pauses. Half a second. She has noticed Luma noticing. Her face registers something brief and unnameable. Then she moves on.

The dead pixel PULSES. Once. Brighter.

Luma's eyes go wide.

**BELL RINGS.**

**CUT TO BLACK.**

### What the audience has without being told
- Luma's perceptual signature (she sees what no one else looks at)
- Her social position (present but not tracked; interesting but not followed)
- Cosmo as her patient witness
- The dead pixel as this show's visual motif
- Dev and Preethi: supporting world, not exposition
- No dialogue that names anything

## Cold Open (~90 seconds)

*Reference: `PANEL_MAP.md` P01–P13, `LTG_SB_pilot_cold_open.png`, `fx_spec_cold_open.md`*

### P01 — EXTERIOR NIGHT
Residential street. Grandma Miri's house. One upstairs window warm. Everything else dark. Quiet.

### P02 — GRANDMA MIRI'S DEN
Wide establishing. The room is dim, familiar, lived-in — bookshelves, snack wrappers, a worn couch, a notebook with margin doodles. Luma is on the couch, but we see her feet first, then the rest of her: not asleep yet, facing away from us, facing something.

The something: a wall of old monitors and shelving. Center shelf: the HERO CRT TELEVISION. It is on. Static.

### P03 — CU MONITOR
The static. A single CYAN PIXEL appears in the lower-right corner of the screen face. First glitch palette contact of the episode.

### P04 — WIDE INTERIOR
The room around the CRT. The cyan is bleeding slightly beyond the screen — a directional source. The warm domestic light is still dominant, but something is contesting it.

Luma has turned toward it. She has been watching this. She does not look surprised.

### P05 — MCU MONITOR (looking out from inside shelf)
The pixel cluster is growing: 8–12 cyan pixels now. Through the static, Luma's warm form is visible blurred in the background.

### P06 — CU MONITOR SCREEN
BYTE's face, pressed flat against the screen glass from inside. His expression: DISGUSTED. Curious. Assessing. He is looking at her.

He has been watching her back.

### P07 — MED WIDE (LOW ANGLE, DUTCH TILT 8° CW)
The monitor BULGES. The screen bows outward — the glass under pressure from inside. Dutch tilt: the room's geometry cannot be trusted anymore. Crack lines at the bezel corner. Cyan distortion rings breaking past the screen boundary.

BYTE phases through — lower half still inside, upper body emerging. Not gliding: pushing. This costs something.

*FX reference: `fx_spec_cold_open.md` FX Moment 1.*

### P08 — MED
BYTE — full reveal, standing in the Real World. Six inches tall. Oval body. One eye clean electric teal; the other cracked. HOT MAGENTA scar diagonal. Pixel confetti drifting.

He looks around. He looks at his own body in the Real World. He looks at the room.

"The flesh dimension."

He says this like it offends him. Like he has been here before and liked it even less the second time.

### P09 — MED WIDE
Byte floats up off the floor (18 inches). Cracked eye scanning. He spots Luma on the couch.

Normal eye iris shifts LEFT — toward her. He has found what he came through for. The cautious approach begins. He does not know she is already awake.

### P10 — OTS (BYTE'S POV)
Looking at Luma's sleeping face. Byte in cool silhouette FG. Luma's warm skin and orange hoodie in the BG. Cyan glow catching her cheek from Byte's proximity. Two temperatures, one frame.

### P11 — ECU LUMA'S FACE
Closed eyes. Cyan glow on her face from Byte hovering nearby. Long hold. Nothing happening. Then — brow twitch. Eyes SNAP open.

### P12 — CU TWO-SHOT (NOSE-TO-NOSE)
Luma and Byte. Equal frame weight. Negative space between them. CRT visible camera-right background.

Both of them very still. Both of them deciding.

*Staging spec: P12 notes in `PANEL_MAP.md` — equal presence ~30% each, ~40% gap, Byte at Luma's eye level.*

### P13 — MIRROR COMPOSITION (THRESHOLD BEAT)
Byte camera-right. Luma camera-left. The show's moral argument in one frame: the organic eye (open, wondering) facing center; the cracked eye (processing, not ready) facing outward.

## Act One (~6 minutes): The Problem Named

### Scene A1-01 — Kitchen, Morning
Grandma Miri's kitchen. Warm morning light. GRANDMA MIRI (older, rounded-square shape language, silver-streaked hair) is making tea. She moves through the kitchen like someone who owns time.

Luma comes in. She has the look of someone who has not slept and is trying to decide whether to admit it.

**Luma:** "Your TV is broken."

**Grandma Miri:** "Define broken."

**Luma:** "The static moves."

**Grandma Miri:** (without looking up from the kettle) "The static has always moved."

**Luma:** "Not like *that* it hasn't."

Grandma Miri hands her tea. The conversation is over. (The television hums in the den.)

Luma looks at the doorway toward the den. Something is waiting for her there and she can feel it.

*Staging note: Grandma Miri never confirms or denies. She asks questions that are actually answers. The kitchen is warm, specific — burned toast smell, crossword on the table, the hum of a house that knows things. This scene is ~45 seconds.*

### Scene A1-02 — Den, Late Morning
Luma has been back at the CRT all morning. The static is different now. It has texture. She has a notebook open on her lap and she has been drawing the pattern — or trying to. The drawings in the margin look like something that has been living there for years.

She powers the CRT off. Goes to do something else.

She lasts about four minutes. She comes back and powers it on.

The static has a pattern. The pattern has a shape. The shape has a face.

"Hello," she says.

*[This line echoes the story bible, Section 6. It is the moment the show actually starts.]*

### Scene A1-03 — The Eruption
The screen ERUPTS. Byte phases through — this time fully, no half-emergence. He ricochets around the den for thirty seconds, breaking absolutely nothing but creating the impression that everything is about to break.

*Comedy beat. Physical. Fast. Luma attempts: (1) a laundry basket, (2) a throw pillow, (3) reasoning. None of these work.*

Byte lands on top of the CRT. He looks at Luma.

Luma looks at Byte.

A long beat. They are both assessing each other very carefully.

**Byte:** "You can actually see me."

Not a question. The beginning of something neither of them is ready for.

*This is the Act One turn: the problem exists. Byte is real. Luma was right.*

### Scene A1-04 — CRT Context
Byte explains: there is a breach in the Glitch Layer. It is leaking. It is spreading. He needs her to help close it.

He gives the minimum possible information in the shortest possible sentences.

The cyan contamination is visible: halos on electronics, a pixel-dither pattern starting on the refrigerator door. It's slow. It will not stay slow.

Luma asks three questions. Byte answers one of them. She presses. He points to the contamination on the fridge.

**Byte:** "That is currently spreading at a rate that makes this conversation wasteful."

She looks at the fridge. She looks at him. She looks at her notebook. She starts a new page.

## Act Two (~8 minutes): The Complication

### Scene A2-01 — Cosmo Arrives
Luma calls Cosmo. Cosmo arrives within the hour with a worn notebook, a portable EMF meter, and an air of someone who has accepted their life is now like this.

COSMO cannot see Byte. Not yet. He can see the contamination — the cyan halos, the pixel-dither, the way the electronics are behaving wrong. He takes systematic notes.

**Byte:** (to Luma, not to Cosmo) "He cannot see me. Your situation is not improved."

**Cosmo:** (without looking up from his notes) "I know you're talking to something."

A beat.

**Cosmo:** "I'm going to call it 'the entity' until you name it."

**Byte:** (offended) "He will not call me 'the entity.'"

**Luma:** "He's calling you the entity."

**Byte:** "I understand that."

*Cosmo and Byte's relationship is immediately: mutual respect between two people who will not show it. Cosmo takes notes on what he cannot see. Byte corrects the notes via Luma. They work efficiently.*

### Scene A2-02 — Mapping the Contamination
The three of them map the contamination. It has a source point — the CRT — and it's moving toward the house's electronics, working outward from the den through the internal wiring.

Cosmo calculates rate of spread. Byte confirms, through Luma.

**Cosmo:** "At current rate, the router goes in four hours. After the router — everything wireless in range."

Beat.

**Cosmo:** "Which, in this neighborhood, is approximately thirty-seven devices. Including the school's projector."

Luma and Byte exchange a look.

*The projector. The dead pixel from the pre-credits tag. The show is building its echo.*

### Scene A2-03 — The Social Cost Beat
Luma's phone lights up on the kitchen table. Dev Patel-Huang. Third text in a row.

She does not see it. She is watching the CRT screen, where the contamination pattern is developing an internal structure she is trying to map.

Cosmo glances at the phone. At Luma. He says, with careful lightness:

**Cosmo:** "Dev asked me if I knew where you were."

**Luma:** (not looking up) "What did you say?"

**Cosmo:** "I said you were busy."

She does not ask with what. They both know she should have texted back. They both know she won't, because she doesn't see it as a choice — she doesn't register the moment she could have paused. That's the cost. It lives in the small pause between Cosmo's line and her non-response.

*This scene is 15 seconds maximum. No resolution. No drama. Just the specific weight of a pattern she doesn't know she has.*

### Scene A2-04 — Escalation
The contamination reaches the router. Three seconds of silence.

Then: every screen in the house activates simultaneously. The living room TV. The tablet on the counter. Grandma Miri's kitchen radio. The old microwave display. They are all showing the same cyan static.

And through every one of them, something is pressing against the glass from the inside.

**Byte:** (very quietly) "We are out of time."

Luma looks at the CRT. The breach is visible through the screen — a torn place in the Glitch Layer, leaking cyan light. Small. Growing.

**Cosmo:** (already writing) "The source is still the CRT. The others are secondary breach points — sympathetic resonance with the primary."

**Byte:** "He is correct."

**Cosmo:** (without looking up) "Tell the entity I'll take that as a compliment."

### Scene A2-05 — The Reach Decision
She looks at the breach through the CRT screen. She looks at Byte.

**Luma:** "Can I reach in?"

Byte does not answer immediately. This is the tell — he always answers immediately. When he pauses, something matters.

**Byte:** "You can. Whether you should is a different conversation and we do not have time for it."

**Luma:** "So that's a yes."

She looks at her phone once. Dev Patel-Huang. Four texts now.

She puts the phone face-down. This is not a dramatic choice. She doesn't even register that she made it. She turns to the CRT.

She reaches in.

*Act Two ends here. The breach is Luma's entry point — not a portal she goes through, but a puncture she can reach into and work.*

## Act Three (~6 minutes): The Breach

### Scene A3-01 — Inside the Reach
Luma is leaning through the CRT screen — not fully inside, physically. Her face and arms are through; the rest of her is in Grandma Miri's den. The breach is right in front of her: a tear in the Glitch Layer's fabric, cycling through corrupt data. A seam that has come undone.

Byte is on her shoulder. His pixel confetti trails have stopped — in the Layer, they read differently. He looks like he belongs here.

**Byte:** "The breach needs a signal. A clean data packet. Something with geometric regularity."

**Luma:** "I don't have a clean data packet."

**Byte:** "No. You have a phone."

**Luma:** "That's the same thing?"

**Byte:** "In this context, yes. Point it at something with a consistent grid structure."

### Scene A3-02 — The Doubt-in-Certainty Beat
*The emotional center of the pilot.*

Luma is leaning through a CRT screen into a dimension she has no framework for. She needs to photograph something with a consistent grid. She knows exactly what to photograph — the kitchen floor. The day she arrived, she noticed the linoleum tile had a grid pattern that was very slightly off-axis, and she traced it with her finger and knew it was real.

She holds the beat.

She hears the version of herself that has been told her whole life: *you notice things that aren't there.* You see patterns in noise. The static was just static. The pixel was just a dead pixel. And now you're leaning through a television, and the six-inch creature on your shoulder is telling you to photograph the floor that you decided mattered on the first day, and you have been right about these things your whole life, and you also might not be right this time, and you cannot tell, standing here, which kind of moment this is.

She holds the half-beat.

Her eyes go to the floor.

The grid is exactly where she knew it would be.

**Luma:** (to Cosmo, through the CRT screen) "The kitchen floor. Angle the phone at fifteen degrees to the left. You'll see the grid."

**Cosmo:** (from the den) "You mean the one you pointed out the first day?"

**Luma:** "You wrote it down."

**Cosmo:** (already positioning the phone) "I write everything down."

### Scene A3-03 — The Patch
Cosmo positions the phone. Luma guides the angle via Byte, who calculated it in the forty-second knowledge dump he won't be repeating. The clean geometric input goes in.

The breach responds. The corrupt data cycling through it slows — stutters — stops.

**Byte:** "Hold."

The breach seals. Not dramatically — it doesn't snap shut or explode. It just... stops being there. Like a knot pulled tight.

Every screen in the house goes dark simultaneously.

The house is quiet.

### Scene A3-04 — The Return
Luma pulls back through the CRT into the den. She sits down on the floor. Byte is on her shoulder.

Cosmo is in the doorway, looking at the exact spot where, based on Luma's description, Byte should be standing. He is not looking at the air slightly above and to the right. He is looking at the precise location.

He says nothing. He opens his notebook and writes for a long moment.

Grandma Miri appears in the doorway behind him. She has tea. She looks at all three of them. Her eyes go to the spot on Luma's left shoulder.

There is a long beat.

**Grandma Miri:** (to the spot on Luma's shoulder) "I thought that might be you."

*The room changes. Byte does not answer. His cracked eye glitches once.*

---

## Tag (~1.5 minutes): What It Costs

Grandma Miri goes to make more tea. Cosmo opens his notebook to a fresh page.

Luma looks at Byte.

**Luma:** "You knew her."

**Byte:** "Drink your tea."

He floats off her shoulder and disappears behind the CRT television.

Cosmo writes, without looking up:

*"Known to Grandma Miri. Prior relationship. Investigate."*

The CRT screen pulses once — a dead pixel. Then goes still.

On the counter, Luma's phone lights up. It's Dev. Five texts now. She picks it up.

She doesn't text back yet. But she picks it up and looks at it for a moment. That's new.

## Tag Stinger (~30 seconds)

*Behind the CRT. Dark.*

BYTE, alone. He sits down heavily against the back of the television. Pixel confetti stills around him. His cracked eye is still.

A long moment of nothing.

Then, very quietly, to nobody:

**Byte:** "I should have mentioned this earlier."

He sits there in the dark.

**CUT TO BLACK.**

*End credits.*

## Visual / Tonal Notes for Directors and Storyboarders

**Color temperature architecture:**
- Pre-Credits Tag: SUNLIT_AMBER classroom (warm, ordinary)
- Cold Open: warm den + increasing ELEC_CYAN intrusion (two temperatures beginning to contest)
- Act One: warm domestic kitchen / CRT glow (intimacy)
- Act Two: contamination spreading — cyan bleeds into the warm world
- Act Three: the reach scene — Luma framed in the CRT glow, both temperatures simultaneously
- Tag: warm kitchen again, but the CRT is still present in the background. The world has changed.

**Comedy pacing:** Physical comedy (Byte's arrival, ricochet, A1-03) plays at maximum pace. Emotional beats need room. The pilot's director should resist filling every beat with action — the quiet moments (A2-03, A3-02) are where the show's personality lives.

**Dialogue register:** Luma speaks fast, incomplete sentences, present tense. Byte is clipped, declarative, manages bandwidth. Cosmo is precise and warm, longer sentences, qualifiers. Do not write them in each other's registers.

**Grandma Miri rule:** She never escalates audibly. She goes very still. In staging: if the scene is tense, Grandma Miri should be the stillest element in the frame. Her knowledge should look like patience.
