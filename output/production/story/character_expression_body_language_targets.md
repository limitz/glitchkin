## Purpose

This document translates character personality into concrete visual construction targets. It answers: **what should this character's body be doing when they feel X?** Every entry gives Maya and the character artists a buildable description — not adjectives, but body positions, weight distribution, shoulder angles, and limb states. These are the targets the expression sheets and turnarounds must hit.

The document has four sections:
1. **Expression-to-Body-Language Targets** — per character, per key expression
2. **Personality-to-Silhouette Mapping** — what each character's silhouette communicates at a glance
3. **Scene-Specific Character Requirements** — priority poses/expressions by pilot scene
4. **Production Bible Gap Analysis** — where written descriptions and rendered assets diverge

## 1. EXPRESSION-TO-BODY-LANGUAGE TARGETS

### Principles (All Characters)

These apply across the cast. They are the story rules behind the body language:

- **Weight tells the truth.** Where a character's weight sits (forward, back, centered, off-balance) communicates intent before the face does. A character leaning forward with wide eyes is curious. The same face with weight on the back foot is scared. The body resolves the ambiguity.
- **Hands are the second face.** In the reference shows that work (Hilda, The Owl House), hands carry as much emotional information as eyes. Hands clasped at chest = vulnerability. One hand reaching = agency. Arms crossed = self-protection, not defiance. Hands hidden behind back = withholding.
- **Shoulders are the barometer.** Raised shoulders = tension. Dropped shoulders = exhaustion or surrender. Asymmetric shoulders = internal conflict. The shoulder involvement rule (image-rules.md, C47) is a technical implementation of this story principle.
- **The silhouette test.** Every pose described below must be readable as the correct emotion in pure black silhouette at 80px character height. If it needs the face to read, the pose is not doing enough work.

### LUMA

Luma's body language vocabulary is defined by one principle: **she leads with her center of gravity.** Her weight is almost always forward — toward the thing she wants, the problem she is solving, the person she is listening to. The only time her weight moves backward is when she is genuinely afraid, and even then it is momentary, because her instinct to engage overrides her instinct to retreat.

**Key trait in body:** Luma is rarely symmetrical. One hand is always doing something the other is not. One foot is always ahead of the other. Her body is a record of interrupted motion — she looks like she stopped mid-step, and she probably did.

| Expression | Weight | Torso | Shoulders | Arms/Hands | Legs/Feet | Head | Hair State | Silhouette Read |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **CURIOUS** | Forward, on front foot (60/40 split) | Slight forward lean, 5-8 degrees from vertical | Left shoulder slightly higher (reaching side), right relaxed | One hand reaching forward or touching surface of interest, other hand at side or pushing off a surface behind her | Front foot flat, back foot on ball — ready to step or lunge | Tilted 10-15 degrees toward object of interest, chin slightly up | Lifts slightly, flyaways increase — hair is alert | Asymmetric forward lean with one arm extended — reads as "reaching toward" |
| **EXCITED / RECKLESS** | Rising — on balls of both feet, almost bouncing | Upright but coiled, torso compressed slightly as if about to spring | Both raised 3-4px, creating a "ready to explode" tension line | Both hands up, palms open, fingers (mitten-hands) spread — or one hand grabbing Cosmo's arm, other pointing | Both feet close together, weight high, heels barely touching ground | Thrown back slightly, chin up, mouth wide open | Maximum volume — lifts and expands outward, curls spiraling, flyaways everywhere | Elevated, expanded silhouette — reads as "about to launch" |
| **FOCUSED / DETERMINED** | Forward and low — knees slightly bent, center of gravity dropped | Lean forward 10-15 degrees, torso narrowed (arms pulled in) | Both dropped and pulled forward — hunching toward the task | Hands working — gripping, adjusting, building. Elbows pulled in close to body. Fingers purposeful, not loose | Wide stance, feet planted, weight even but low | Level, eyes narrowed, chin down slightly — the only time her gaze is steady and horizontal | Tightens and pulls back — flyaways reduce, mass compresses. Hair getting out of the way | Compact, low, forward triangle — reads as "locked on" |
| **SCARED** | Back foot, momentary — weight shifting rearward but body still facing threat | Slight backward lean, torso twisting 10-20 degrees away from threat while head stays aimed at it | Raised and pulled inward — protective hunch, asymmetric (threat-side shoulder higher) | Hands pulled to chest or gripping own arms. One hand may still be reaching forward (she cannot fully retreat) | Back foot planted, front foot lifted or stepping back — but not running | Eyes wide, eyebrows high, mouth small. Head tilted back but eyes still aimed forward and down at the threat | Droops and flattens against head — volume loss. Hair reads the fear her face is trying to hide | Contracted, hunched, retreating silhouette — but with head still aimed forward. Reads as "afraid but not leaving" |
| **JOYFUL** | Centered but light — on balls of feet, almost floating | Upright, open, chest expanded | Relaxed and slightly back — the opposite of tension. Wide shoulder line | Arms wide and open, or hands clasped at her own cheeks (the "oh!" gesture), or hugging someone with full commitment | Feet slightly apart, toes out (her natural duck-foot stance exaggerated) | Tilted back, chin up, eyes closed or squinting with smile force | Bounces and lifts — maximum volume, outward spray of curls | Open, wide, elevated — reads as "pure delight" |
| **DOUBT-IN-CERTAINTY** (the show's signature expression) | Split — body leaning forward but one foot stepping back. Other hand pulled back, touching her own chest or gripping the hem of her hoodie | Split stance — front foot forward flat, back foot turned 45 degrees outward, weight oscillating | Eyes focused on the goal but eyebrows drawn together in the middle — not confident. Mouth closed, a line. The face says "I think I'm right" and the face also says "what if I'm not" | Partially lifted — not full excitement, not full droop. In between. Tense | Asymmetric split — reads as "torn between going and stopping." This is the most important silhouette in the show. |
| **ANGRY / FRUSTRATED** | Forward and heavy — flat-footed, stamping | Torso upright, chest puffed slightly, hands fisted at sides or gesturing sharply | Both raised and pushed forward — aggression posture, not protective | Fists clenched at sides, or one hand pointing accusingly, or both hands thrown up in exasperation | Feet wide, planted, weight even — she is not going anywhere | Chin down, brows down, eyes narrowed, mouth open in mid-word | Lifts and bristles — curls push outward, the static-electric flyaway effect at the tips | Aggressive forward stance — reads as "confrontation" |

**Luma's ONE forbidden pose:** Luma is never posed with both hands behind her back and weight centered. That posture communicates patience and waiting, which is the opposite of her character at every level. Even when she is waiting, she fidgets — one hand is always doing something.

### COSMO

Cosmo's body language vocabulary is defined by one principle: **he organizes himself before he acts.** His weight is centered and vertical by default. His body is a controlled system — arms close, posture upright, notebook secure. When emotion breaks through the control, the body language change is dramatic precisely because the baseline is so contained.

**Key trait in body:** Cosmo is the most symmetrical character in neutral. His asymmetry (lower left shoulder from notebook weight, 7-degree glasses tilt) is subtle and habitual, not expressive. When he becomes truly asymmetric — one arm flung out, shoulders uneven, stance wide — it means the plan has failed and he has not yet built a new one. Those moments are comedy gold and emotional beats simultaneously.

| Expression | Weight | Torso | Shoulders | Arms/Hands | Legs/Feet | Head | Glasses | Silhouette Read |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **OBSERVING** (default neutral) | Centered, slightly back — the weight of someone who is watching, not entering | Perfectly upright, vertical | Level (left fractionally lower from notebook habit — 1-2px) | Notebook tucked under left arm, right hand may hold pen or rest at side. Elbows close to body | Feet together or nearly together, parallel, flat | Level, slight chin raise — looking over glasses slightly | 7-degree tilt (neutral). Glare crescent catching light | Tall, narrow, vertical rectangle — reads as "standing still, watching" |
| **ANXIOUS / PREPARING** | Centered but raised — on balls of feet, body pulled upward as if trying to make himself smaller | Slightly compressed — shoulders pulling up toward ears, torso narrowing | Both raised 4-6px — classic tension posture | Notebook clutched against chest with both hands (rare — he normally tucks it under one arm). Knuckles white around the spine | Feet together, weight high, heels lifted slightly | Pulled down between raised shoulders — neck shortened | Tilt increases to 9-10 degrees. He has bumped them nervously | Compressed, tall, narrow column with raised shoulders — reads as "bracing" |
| **INTELLECTUALLY EXCITED** | Forward — the ONLY time Cosmo's weight moves ahead of his center | Forward lean 5-8 degrees, torso opening up | Both pulled back and slightly down — chest opens, the opposite of his anxious pose | Notebook open in one hand, other hand gesturing (pointing at a detail, tracing a diagram in air, adjusting glasses). Pen active | Slightly wider stance than usual — one foot has stepped forward without him noticing | Head tilted forward, chin down, eyes wide behind glasses — looking INTO something | Tilt unchanged (7 degrees) but pushed slightly down nose from the forward lean — he is looking over them | Leaning forward with open posture and active hand — reads as "engaged, discovering." This is the Cosmo the audience roots for |
| **DEADPAN / "I TOLD YOU SO"** | Centered, flat, heavy — weight evenly distributed, no movement | Perfectly upright, arms crossed OR one hand on hip, notebook dangling from the other | Level and still. No tension, no excitement. Aggressively neutral shoulders | Arms crossed over chest (notebook still visible, tucked against his forearm), OR one hand on hip with notebook in the other | Feet slightly apart, one hip slightly cocked — the most casual stance he ever takes | Level. Flat lower eyelid line. Mouth a perfect horizontal. He is not going to say it, but his entire body is saying it | Tilt at 7 degrees. The glasses are doing nothing different. It is his face that has become the statement | Upright rectangle with crossed arms — reads as "waiting for you to figure it out" |
| **GENUINELY FRIGHTENED** | Back and off-balance — weight on back foot, upper body recoiling | Backward lean, torso twisted partially away from the threat | Dramatically asymmetric — threat-side shoulder flung up (protective), far-side shoulder dropped (recoil) | Notebook dropped or clutched to chest. One lens may catch a glare that the other does not | Recoiling rectangle — reads as "the plan is gone." The dropped notebook (if applicable) is a character-defining visual beat |
| **QUIETLY PLEASED** | Centered, settled, weight slightly lower than usual — he has relaxed | Upright but softer — less rigid than his observing pose | Relaxed, both slightly lower than neutral. This is visible because his neutral is so precisely held | Notebook loosely under arm (not clutched). Pen tucked behind ear or in notebook spine. Hands at rest | Feet slightly apart, weight even, flat — grounded | Very slight chin drop, eyes soft, the tiniest upward pull at mouth corners. The smile is contained. The blush spots appear (subtle, higher on cheek than Luma's) | 7 degrees. Untouched. He does not adjust them when he is happy because he has forgotten about them | Relaxed vertical — reads as "at ease for the first time." The contrast with his usual tension is the emotional signal |

**Cosmo's ONE forbidden pose:** Cosmo is never posed in a wide athletic stance with both arms thrown out and weight forward. That is Luma's body language. Cosmo's version of the same emotion is always more vertical, more contained, and centered through the glasses and the notebook rather than through physical expansiveness.

**The Notebook as Expression:** The notebook position is an emotional barometer all by itself:
- **Tucked under arm (default):** Neutral, prepared, monitoring
- **Clutched to chest with both hands:** Frightened or overwhelmed
- **Open, held out in front:** Engaged, confident, processing a problem
- **Dangling loosely from one hand:** Relaxed, pleased, off-duty
- **Dropped on the ground:** Crisis. The plan has failed. This happens at most twice in the entire season and both times are major emotional beats
- **Absent from frame entirely:** Rare. He has fully let go of preparation. A significant character moment

### BYTE

Byte's body language vocabulary is defined by one principle: **his emotions leak through a body he is trying to hold still.** He wants to float impassively. He wants to be a cube of detachment hovering at shoulder height. But his limbs betray him, his cracked eye glitches when he cares, and his pixel confetti scatter changes color and density based on emotional states he has not acknowledged.

**Key trait in body:** Byte has the smallest physical vocabulary of the main cast (oval body, stubby limbs, no visible shoulders or spine), which means every small change is proportionally enormous. A 10-degree limb angle change on Luma is a shrug. A 10-degree limb angle change on Byte is a declaration. Maya and the artists must treat Byte's body as a precision instrument — small adjustments, large reads.

| Expression | Hover Height | Body Tilt | Upper Limbs | Lower Limbs | Cracked Eye | Confetti | Silhouette Read |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **GRUMPY NEUTRAL** (default) | Standard — 0.25 units above surface | Upright, no tilt | Flat against body sides, angled slightly downward (10 degrees from horizontal). Tips pointing toward lower-front corners | Straight down, parallel, even | Stable — no active glitching. Crack line visible but static | Standard teal/cyan scatter, low density (4-6 particles), even spread | Compact oval with stubby limbs — reads as "present but unimpressed" |
| **PROTECTIVE / ALERT** | Rises 15-20% — increased hover, putting distance between himself and Luma's shoulder surface | Slight lean toward the threat — his body turns before his limbs do | Both extended forward and slightly outward — a blocking gesture. The closest Byte comes to "arms wide" | Both angled forward, 15 degrees from vertical — bracing posture | Glitching at medium rate — the crack pixel is cycling between DIM and ACTIVE states | Density increases (8-10 particles), spread widens. Colors shift toward ELEC_CYAN — higher energy | Oval with limbs spread forward — reads as "blocking." This is the pose that confirms Byte cares, even though he will never say so |
| **GENUINELY ANGRY** | Drops to minimum hover — barely clearing the surface. Getting low = getting serious | Forward lean, 8-12 degrees. Aggressive for his proportions | Both thrust forward and down, tips pointing at the target — accusatory. Angle: 30 degrees below horizontal | Spread wide, 25 degrees from vertical outward — a power stance on a body that has no legs | Glitching rapidly — the crack pixel is alternating HOT_MAG and VOID_BLACK at high frequency. This is Byte at maximum emotional exposure | Dense scatter (14-18 particles), concentrated tightly below body. Colors: HOT_MAG dominant. The confetti is angry too | Low, wide, aggressive — reads as "small creature, enormous fury." Comedy and sincerity simultaneously |
| **RELUCTANT JOY** | Standard hover, but with a slight bob — he is bouncing without admitting it | Slight backward lean — he is trying to pull away from his own happiness | One limb (upper-left) reaching slightly toward the source of joy, then pulling back. The other held firmly against the body. The asymmetry is the tell | One lower limb relaxed (dangling at a slight angle), the other held in rigid parallel — half of him has relaxed and the other half has not | Glitching in slow, warm pulses — DIM to BRIGHT to DIM. The gentlest glitch cycle. He does not notice it. Everyone else does | Low density (3-4 particles), warm-shifted colors. SOFT_GOLD appearing where it normally does not. | **POWERED DOWN / DEFEATED** | Minimum hover, barely above surface — nearly sitting on whatever he is resting on | Slight forward slump — the top of the oval dips forward. The eye is barely on | Zero or near-zero confetti. One or two particles drifting slowly. Desaturated. The floating mechanism is failing | Deflated oval with limp limbs — reads as "giving up." This is the pose that makes the audience protective of him |
| **HIDING SOMETHING** | Standard hover, but unnaturally still — no bob, no drift. Holding position with effort | Rigidly upright — overcompensating for the internal tension | Both pressed tight against body. Not moving. He is holding them still on purpose, which is different from their natural slight sway | Both straight down, locked, parallel — military posture on a creature with no military | Stable but with micro-glitches — tiny single-frame flickers that are too fast to read consciously but create a subliminal "something is wrong" signal | Reduced density, but the particles are staying closer to his body than usual — clustering, not scattering. The confetti is being contained | Unusually compact and symmetrical oval — reads as "holding very still." The audience learns to read this as "Byte knows something he is not saying," which is one of the show's most frequent Byte states |

**Byte's ONE forbidden pose:** Byte is never posed with all four limbs extended outward in a relaxed, open posture (the "starfish" pose). That communicates comfort, trust, and physical ease in his own body — none of which Byte feels. His maximum openness is one limb reaching while the others stay controlled. Full four-limb extension would read as a different character entirely.

### GRANDMA MIRI

Grandma Miri's body language vocabulary is defined by one principle: **she has already arrived.** Her weight is always centered. Her movements are deliberate and unhurried. She does not lean toward things — she allows things to come to her. This is not passivity; it is the body language of someone who has already figured out most of the situation and is waiting for others to catch up.

**Key trait in body:** Miri's stillness is her power. In a cast of characters defined by motion (Luma), tension (Cosmo), and containment (Byte), Miri is the anchor. She does not fidget. She does not adjust. The cup of tea is always exactly where she wants it. When she does move, it matters — every step, every gesture, every turn of the head is loaded with intention.

| Expression | Weight | Torso | Shoulders | Arms/Hands | Legs/Feet | Head | Silhouette Read |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **KNOWING CALM** (default) | Centered, low, settled — the weight of someone sitting in their own house | Upright but soft — no rigidity. The cardigan creates a wide, settled trapezoid | Relaxed and slightly wide — the cardigan's inverse-trapezoid shoulders are her visual anchor. Neither raised nor dropped | One hand holding tea cup at chest height. Other hand at rest — on table, on armrest, or in lap. Never both hands idle. The tea cup is the constant | If seated: feet flat, knees slightly apart (the wide base her shape language demands). If standing: feet wide, settled, weight even | Level, chin slightly raised. Eyes observing over the tea cup. The expression of someone who is listening to what you are really saying, not what you think you are saying | Wide base, settled, warm — reads as "the person this room belongs to" |
| **PROTECTIVE CONCERN** | Forward shift — 5 degrees. Rare. She does not lean easily, so when she does it registers | Forward lean, torso rotating toward the subject of concern. Cardigan falls forward | Slightly raised — the ONLY time Miri's shoulders lift. This is how you know she is worried | Tea cup lowered or set down (significant — putting down the tea = something is serious). Both hands moving toward the subject — reaching, but not grabbing. She offers support, she does not impose it | If standing: one step toward. Weight shifts to front foot. If seated: feet move to under-hip position, preparing to stand | Tilted forward and down — looking at the person she is protecting. Eyes soft but direct | Forward-shifted wide form — reads as "maternal alarm." The set-down tea cup is the visual alarm bell |
| **THE LOOK** (the "I already know" expression) | Dead center, immovable | Perfectly upright, perfectly still. No lean in any direction | Level, wide, motionless — aggressively relaxed | Tea cup held at exact center-chest height. Other hand resting. Nothing is moving. She is a painting | If seated: unchanged from default. If standing: weight even, feet planted, no sway | Slight chin tilt upward, eyes looking down over cheekbones at the speaker. One eyebrow very slightly raised. Mouth closed — not a smile, not a frown. The most powerful neutral face in the show | Wide, settled, unmoving column — reads as "she knows exactly what you did." This is the expression Grandma Miri uses when Luma is about to lie, and Luma always folds |
| **GENUINE DELIGHT** | Settles even lower — she sinks into her happiness like sinking into a favorite chair | Slight backward lean — opening up, chest expanding | Drop 2-3px — relaxation. The inverse-trapezoid softens | Both hands wrapped around tea cup (both hands on the cup = she is savoring the moment, not monitoring the room). Or: one hand on Luma's shoulder, the other holding the cup | If seated: feet tuck back slightly, a subtle comfort shift. If standing: weight rocks back to heels briefly | Eyes crinkle (the under-eye crinkle is her version of Luma's full-face smile). Chin drops. Mouth pulls back — a contained, warm smile. She does not grin. She glows | Settled, wide, grounded — reads as "contentment." The two-hands-on-cup is the identifier |
| **THE HANDOFF** (passing knowledge to Luma — SF06 reference) | Forward, toward Luma — a rare directed lean | Rotating toward the CRT / toward Luma, upper body opening on one side | Asymmetric — reaching-side shoulder extended forward and slightly up, other shoulder back. The cardigan falls asymmetrically | One arm extended toward the CRT or toward Luma (the reaching arm). Palm up or open — offering, not pointing. Other arm at her side or holding the chair/table for balance | If standing: weight on front foot, back foot as anchor. One step taken or about to be taken. If seated: turned in chair, one foot extended as if to stand | Turned toward Luma, eyes on Luma's face (not on the CRT — she knows the CRT, she is watching whether Luma is ready) | Asymmetric wide form reaching toward a smaller form — reads as "transmission." **Miri's ONE forbidden pose:** Miri is never posed in a rushed, off-balance position with weight on one foot and arms flailing. She does not lose her balance. She does not scramble. Even in genuine danger, her body language is deliberate and controlled — she has faced worse than this, and her body remembers.

### GLITCH

Glitch's body language is defined by one principle: **it does not have body language.** Where every other character's body communicates internal states involuntarily, Glitch's movements are geometric operations — precise, calculated, with no waste. The absence of organic body language IS the body language. It tells the audience that whatever made Glitch a person has been overwritten.

**Key trait in body:** Glitch's diamond body does not flex, bend, or organically deform. It tilts, rotates, scales (squash/stretch), and translates. The arm-spikes adjust their angles through mathematical parameters, not muscular impulse. The only organic-adjacent signals come from the interior state eyes (YEARNING, COVETOUS, HOLLOW) — and those are the moments that prove something is still in there.

Glitch's body language targets are already well-specified in `glitch.md` (Section 5: rotation rules, Section 4: arm-spike positions). The story-level additions below specify what those mechanical states mean narratively:

| Expression | Narrative Function | What the Audience Should Feel | Scene Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NEUTRAL** | Default surveillance mode. Glitch is present and monitoring | Unease — it is too still, too precise. Something is watching that should not be watching | Any scene where Glitch appears in the background of the Glitch Layer. It has arrived without approaching — it was just suddenly there |
| **MISCHIEVOUS** | Active manipulation. Glitch is executing a plan | Dread — the tilt (20 degrees right) and asymmetric arm-spikes communicate "I have already won this interaction and you do not know it yet" | Confrontation scenes where Glitch is ahead of Luma. The mischief is not playful — it is predatory |
| **CALCULATING** | Decision point. Glitch is choosing its next action | Tension — the perfect stillness (0 tilt, 0 body movement) combined with one arm-spike raised (the "chin-touch equivalent") communicates intelligence at work. The audience should feel that the smartest thing in the room is thinking, and that is bad news | Scenes where Glitch observes Luma solving a problem. It is learning from her. This is the expression that should make the audience realize Glitch is not just powerful — it is strategic |
| **TRIUMPHANT** | The plan succeeded. Maximum display | A specific kind of horror: the victory pose (both arms raised, spike at maximum extension, full gold eyes) looks celebratory, but there is no joy in it. The audience should feel the victory is mechanical — a system confirming task completion, not a person feeling satisfaction | End-of-episode cliffhangers where Glitch has achieved a step in the portal plan |
| **YEARNING** (interior state) | A memory surfaces. Glitch remembers something from before the Corruption | Sadness and hope — the bilateral eyes (both identical UV soft glow), arms hanging, no confetti, body still. For one moment, Glitch is not an instrument. It is a person remembering. The audience should feel the tragedy: the person is still in there, but can never get out | RARE. One per act MAXIMUM. Used when Glitch observes Byte and Luma together. What it yearns for is connection — but it can no longer name the yearning |
| **HOLLOW** (interior state) | The yearning has passed. Nothing came back | Grief — the deflated body, empty white center-pixel stare, zero confetti. This is what the Corruption leaves behind after it uses you. The audience should feel that this is the future the show is fighting to prevent for Byte | EXTREMELY RARE. End-of-episode quiet moments only. The emotional gut-punch of the show's antagonist is not their power scenes — it is their empty ones |

**Glitch's ONE forbidden behavior:** Glitch never moves with organic easing (slow-in, slow-out). Its movements are linear or instantaneous — A to B with no curve. The moment Glitch moves with easing, it would read as alive, which it is not. The mechanical precision of its motion IS the tragedy.

## 2. PERSONALITY-TO-SILHOUETTE MAPPING

Each character's silhouette must communicate their personality at 80px height, without color, without face detail. If you cannot tell which character is which from silhouette alone, the design is not doing its job.

### What Each Silhouette Must Say

**LUMA — "I am already moving."**
- Dynamic, forward-leaning, asymmetric
- Hair cloud is the dominant read — wider than the body, wilder than the head shape would suggest, providing the top-heavy energy that makes her feel ready to tip forward
- One arm always in a different position than the other
- Feet offset (duck-footed, one ahead of the other)
- The hoodie adds bulk at the torso that grounds the hair volume — without it she would look top-heavy in a way that reads fragile. With it she reads sturdy and kinetic
- **Silhouette test question:** Does it look like this person was frozen mid-step? If yes, it is Luma. If she looks like she is standing still and posing, it is wrong

**COSMO — "I am holding everything together."**
- Vertical, contained, tall, rectangular
- The glasses protrusion at face level is the first identifier — two circles on a rectangular head
- Notebook protrusion on the left side at mid-torso creates the only major asymmetry
- Arms close to body, elbows in
- Feet together or nearly together
- The cowlick at the crown (0.15 heads tall, visible at thumbnail) breaks the rectangular head's top line — a small imperfection in an otherwise organized silhouette
- **Silhouette test question:** Does it look like someone standing at attention who forgot one thing (the cowlick, the notebook, the crooked glasses)? If yes, it is Cosmo. If it looks relaxed or athletic, it is wrong

**BYTE — "I am not going to explain myself."**
- Compact oval, hovering above a surface — the gap between his lower limbs and the ground is a defining silhouette feature
- Stubby limbs give the oval a slight cross or asterisk read at distance
- The cracked-eye glyph and hot magenta scar are not silhouette features — at silhouette distance, it is the oval + float + scale (tiny) that identifies him
- When riding on Luma's shoulder, Byte's silhouette is a small oval protrusion on one side of Luma's head-hair mass. The audience should be able to identify "Luma-with-Byte" versus "Luma-alone" from silhouette
- **Silhouette test question:** Is it a small floating oval that looks slightly annoyed even without a face? If yes, it is Byte. If it looks cute and round and friendly, it is wrong — the limb angles must communicate resistance even in silhouette

**GRANDMA MIRI — "I have been here longer than you."**
- Wide base, settled, grounded — the inverse-trapezoid of the cardigan shoulders creates a shape that is wider at the top than at the feet, like a mountain or a church bell
- The tea cup silhouette at chest height is a constant identifier — a small protrusion from the hand area
- Hairpins in the bun create two small spikes above the head's rounded-square top line
- The overall silhouette is a stable trapezoid — it is the most geometrically simple shape in the cast, which communicates solidity and permanence
- **Silhouette test question:** Does it look like a warm, wide, immovable object that is holding a cup? If yes, it is Miri. If it looks thin, active, or hurried, it is wrong

**GLITCH — "I was not built. I was overwritten."**
- Diamond / rhombus — immediately distinct from every other character's rounded or rectangular language
- Top spike (crown) and bottom spike (hover point) extend the diamond vertically into a more aggressive shape
- Arm-spikes on left and right create a horizontal spread that makes the silhouette read as a six-pointed angular form
- The confetti scatter below (when present) creates visual noise at the base that contrasts the geometric precision above
- No rounded curves anywhere. Every angle is a decision
- **Silhouette test question:** Does it look like a shard of something that was broken on purpose? If yes, it is Glitch. If it looks rounded, organic, or friendly in any way, it is wrong

### Lineup Silhouette Priority Order

When all five characters appear together (lineup, group shot, style frame), the silhouette read from left to right should progress from most organic to most geometric:

**Luma (circles) > Grandma Miri (rounded squares) > Cosmo (rectangles) > Byte (oval) > Glitch (diamond)**

This is not a strict staging rule — scene composition takes priority — but when there is no other constraint, this order gives the viewer a visual gradient from warm/organic to cold/geometric that mirrors the show's world progression from Real World to Corruption.

## 5. C51 LIBRARY CAPABILITY REVIEW — TARGET ADJUSTMENTS

The C51 library stack (pycairo, Wand, bezier, Shapely, colour-science, freetype-py, scikit-image) fundamentally changes what character artists can render. Several targets in this document were implicitly conservative because they assumed PIL polygon construction. This section identifies targets that should be raised and new targets that are now achievable.

### Targets Raised

| Character | Expression | Original Target | Revised Target | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Luma** | DOUBT-IN-CERTAINTY | "Split stance, torso twisted" — described as a static pose | **Add weight-shift animation keyframe pair**: pycairo bezier curves can render the oscillation between forward and back as two distinct keyframes with continuous curve deformation. The split should read as *in motion*, not frozen. Build both the "leaning in" and "pulling back" keyframes so storyboarders can interpolate between them. | pycairo bezier deformation makes continuous curve changes between keyframes buildable. PIL could only approximate each pose independently. |
| **Luma** | Hair State (all expressions) | "Lifts slightly," "maximum volume," "tightens and pulls back" — described qualitatively | **Specify hair as bezier curve clusters with per-expression control points.** Each hair state should be defined as a set of bezier control point offsets from the neutral state. Maya can now build hair as actual curves that deform organically, not as polygon silhouettes. Target: 8-12 bezier curves per hair mass, each with 3-4 control points. | bezier library enables arc-length-parameterized hair strands. Hand-rolled polygon hair was the reason hair "volume" was described vaguely — there was no way to build it precisely. |
| **Cosmo** | GENUINELY FRIGHTENED | "Glasses tilt spikes to 12-15 degrees" | **Add glasses slide down nose (2-3px) as a separate deformation.** The tilt increase plus the slide communicates physical disruption more precisely. pycairo can render the glasses as a compound bezier shape that both tilts and translates. | PIL rendered glasses as a fixed rectangle group. pycairo can deform the bridge and lens shapes independently. |
| **Cosmo** | Notebook positions (all) | Five positions described as locations only | **Add notebook page splay.** When clutched to chest (frightened), pages compress. When open in front (engaged), pages fan 5-10 degrees. When dangling (relaxed), spine curves under gravity. When dropped, pages separate on impact. pycairo bezier fills make page edges buildable as curves, not flat rectangles. | PIL notebooks were flat rectangles. Curved page edges are now trivially renderable. |
| **Byte** | Confetti system (all expressions) | Confetti described by count and color only | **Add confetti particle shape variation.** With pycairo, confetti particles can be tiny bezier shapes (not just circles/squares): teal sparks for neutral, elongated streaks for anger, soft rounded puffs for reluctant joy, desaturated fragments for defeat. Shape vocabulary adds a read layer that count/color alone cannot. | PIL confetti was limited to `draw.ellipse` and `draw.rectangle`. pycairo can draw arbitrary small shapes efficiently. |
| **Grandma Miri** | Cardigan (all expressions) | "Inverse-trapezoid shoulders" — described as a fixed silhouette shape | **Cardigan drape responds to pose.** When Miri leans forward (PROTECTIVE CONCERN), fabric bunches at the front fold. When she opens toward Luma (THE HANDOFF), the reaching side pulls taut while the other side drapes. Wand compositing + pycairo curves can render fabric folds as gradient-shaded bezier shapes. Target: 3-5 fold lines per pose variant, with Wand soft-shadow between folds. | PIL could not render fabric folds. The "inverse-trapezoid" was the best approximation of a cardigan that flat geometry could produce. |
| **Grandma Miri** | Tea cup | "Held at chest height" — static object | **Tea cup catches scene light.** Wand compositing can apply CRT tint, warm ambient, or pre-dawn cool to the tea cup surface and the liquid within. The cup should be a micro-lighting reference in every frame Miri holds it — it tells the audience the color temperature of the room. colour-science can validate that the cup's tint matches the scene palette. | Manual NumPy blending made per-object lighting impractical. Wand makes it a compositing pass. |
| **Glitch** | All mechanical movements | "Linear or instantaneous" motion only | **Target unchanged** — but add: the ABSENCE of bezier easing on Glitch's motion is now a deliberate artistic choice that reads more powerfully against a cast whose bodies are built from bezier curves. The contrast between organic (pycairo curves) and mechanical (linear interpolation) is the new library stack's strongest narrative tool for Glitch. No target increase needed — the quality increase comes from the contrast, not from Glitch's own rendering. | The gap between Glitch and the organic cast widens automatically when the organic cast improves. |

### New Targets Now Achievable

These were not in the original C50 document because they were not buildable with the PIL pipeline:

1. **Variable line weight on character outlines.** pycairo variable-width strokes mean thick-at-joints, thin-at-curves line work is now standard. Target: 2:1 thick-to-thin ratio (e.g., 4px at shoulder joint, 2px along arm curve at output scale). This single change eliminates the "uniform thickness = mechanical" read that critics flagged since C15.

2. **Form-following shadows on character bodies.** Wand compositing + pycairo gradient fills enable soft directional shadows that follow body curvature. Target: one primary shadow direction per scene (matching the environment lighting spec from Hana's system), with shadow edge softness proportional to distance from the light-facing edge. This eliminates the flat cel-shadow look that made characters read as paper cutouts.

3. **Silhouette distinctiveness QA gate.** Shapely can now compute pairwise IoU between all five character silhouettes automatically. Target: every character pair must have a Shapely distinctiveness score (1 - IoU) of at least 0.15 at neutral pose. C50 baseline: Miri vs. 3 characters was 0.02 (FAIL). The new construction approaches (bezier curves, variable proportions) should push all pairs above threshold, but the QA gate catches regressions.

4. **Expression range QA gate.** scikit-image SSIM can measure whether two expression renders for the same character are visually distinct. Target: any two different named expressions for the same character must have SSIM below 0.85 (i.e., they look meaningfully different). C50 baseline: Glitch had 12/15 expression pairs above 0.90 (barely distinguishable). The new rendering capabilities make larger visual differences achievable, and this gate ensures they are achieved.

5. **Per-expression hair deformation for Luma.** The bezier hair system makes it possible to define Luma's hair as a deformable curve mesh. Target: 5 distinct hair states (alert/lifted, compressed/scared, exploded/joyful, tense/focused, split/doubt) each defined by control point offsets from neutral. The hair states were described qualitatively in the C50 tables above; they can now be specified as buildable geometry.
