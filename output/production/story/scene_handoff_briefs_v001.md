**Environment:** `LTG_ENV_classroom_bg.png` — Millbrook Middle School classroom, morning light, end-of-year energy
**Characters present:** Luma, Cosmo, Dev Patel-Huang, Preethi Okafor (hallway window only), Teacher (background)
**Key visual beat:** Luma staring at a single dead pixel in the upper-right corner of a projector screen while the rest of the class is visibly distracted. She alone is looking at it.

**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** The dead pixel PULSES once, brighter, at the end of the scene. This is the episode's motif introduced in miniature — a tiny absence that is also a tiny signal. No full Glitch palette yet. Bell rings. CUT TO BLACK.

## Act One (~6 minutes): The Problem Named

### A1-01 — Kitchen, Morning
**Environment:** `LTG_ENV_grandma_kitchen.png` (v005) — Grandma Miri's kitchen, warm morning light
**Characters present:** Grandma Miri, Luma
**Key visual beat:** Grandma Miri, unhurried, making tea — Luma entering with the look of someone who hasn't slept and is deciding whether to admit it. The kitchen is warm, specific, layered with lived-in detail (crossword on table, burned toast smell implied, CRT hum audible from the den).
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Grandma Miri:** Calm, patient, moving like she owns time. SOFT_SURPRISE is her default — she registers everything and escalates nothing. She hands Luma tea without looking up. Her stillness is information.
- **Luma:** Tired but alert. The "you see things that aren't there" muscle is already flexed — she is trying to figure out how to say what she experienced without triggering that response. Her opening line ("Your TV is broken") is a test.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** None in this scene — Real World only. Faint CRT hum implied from the den beyond the doorway. The pixel-dither contamination has not yet reached the kitchen in A1-01.
**Staging note:** Grandma Miri never confirms. She asks questions that are actually answers. Scene target: ~45 seconds. The conversation ending with tea is the scene's closing beat — do not extend it.

### A1-02 — Den, Late Morning
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's den — morning light now, slightly warmer and more present than the cold open
**Characters present:** Luma (at the CRT), no other characters
**Key visual beat:** The pattern in the static has developed a face. Luma says "Hello." to it.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Luma:** Restless, drawn, unable to walk away (comic beat: she powers the CRT off, leaves, comes back in ~4 minutes). The moment she says "Hello" is not triumphant — it is matter-of-fact. She has been right about something and she is acknowledging it quietly. DETERMINED expression transitioning to soft CONFIDENT as she speaks.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** The static pattern has developed texture and structure since the cold open. It now has a face visible through the noise — not Byte's face pressed against the glass (that was cold open), but the pattern itself resolving into a face shape. The face is in the static, not through the glass. This is a different kind of presence.

---

### A1-03 — The Eruption
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's den
**Characters present:** Luma, Byte
**Key visual beat:** Byte phases fully through the CRT — no half-emergence — and ricochets around the den for thirty seconds. Luma attempts (1) laundry basket, (2) throw pillow, (3) reasoning. None of these work.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Byte:** ALARMED to SEARCHING in rapid sequence. The ricochet is not a panic — it is an extremely fast recalibration of a 6-inch creature in an unfamiliar-scale space. He is not afraid. He is computationally overwhelmed.
- **Luma:** Physical comedy mode. Fast, improvising, failing. The joke is that she is trying sensible things in sequence and they are all wrong for this situation. ALARMED snapping to DETERMINED snapping to CONFUSED per attempt.
- Landing beat: "You can actually see me." — Byte says this looking directly at her. Expression: SEARCHING, but held. This is not relief — it is the beginning of a calculation he did not expect to make.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** Full Byte emergence FX — screen eruption, more forceful than cold open. Pixel confetti scatter during ricochet. The ricochet itself is a physical comedy sequence — Byte is not a poltergeist, he does not break anything, but he creates the impression that he is about to. This distinction matters for staging.

---

### A1-04 — CRT Context
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's den, now with early contamination visible
**Characters present:** Luma, Byte
**Key visual beat:** The contamination is visible — cyan halos on electronics, a pixel-dither pattern starting on the refrigerator door visible through the kitchen doorway. Byte is explaining the breach in minimum information packets.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Byte:** RELUCTANT DISCLOSURE mode. He answers the minimum required. He points to the contamination on the fridge when Luma presses for more. His line ("That is currently spreading at a rate that makes this conversation wasteful") is delivered as pure efficiency, not hostility. RELUCTANT JOY is NOT the right expression here — this is RESIGNED / RELUCTANT DISCLOSURE per expression sheet. He knows he needs her.
- **Luma:** Rapid-fire question mode. She asks three questions. She starts a new notebook page when she understands the situation. FOCUSED to DETERMINED — she is now committed.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** Cyan contamination: halos on electronics (CRT most saturated, spreading to nearby devices). Pixel-dither pattern on the refrigerator door — this is the first contamination visible outside the den. It is slow and subtle at this point. Do not overdraw it — it needs to escalate through Act Two.

## Act Two (~8 minutes): The Complication

### A2-01 — Cosmo Arrives
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's den (Cosmo arrives and sets up here; contamination spreading)
**Characters present:** Luma, Byte, Cosmo
**Key visual beat:** Cosmo enters with a worn notebook and a portable EMF meter, takes one look at the contamination, and starts taking notes — without being able to see Byte. The three-way dynamic establishes immediately: Luma relays, Byte is offended, Cosmo is precise.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Cosmo:** CONFIDENT, methodical. He cannot see Byte but he can see the contamination, and he treats the observable with the same rigor he would apply to anything. His expression is DETERMINED — this is a puzzle and he is here to document it. His line "I'm going to call it 'the entity' until you name it" is delivered warmly, without drama. This is how Cosmo operates.
- **Byte:** GRUMPY → ALARMED at being called "the entity." His offended reaction is visible to Luma only. The joke is that he is offended specifically by the label, not by being undetected.
- **Luma:** Mediating, amused, overwhelmed. She is relay-translating between two people who are already building a rapport through her.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** Contamination more developed than A1-04 — cyan halos on multiple devices visible. EMF meter presumably behaving unusually (Cosmo is noting this).

---

### A2-02 — Mapping the Contamination
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's house — den to kitchen, contamination mapping
**Characters present:** Luma, Byte, Cosmo
**Key visual beat:** The three of them working as a functional unit. Contamination map taking shape. The projector reference lands — Cosmo's line "Which, in this neighborhood, is approximately thirty-seven devices. Including the school's projector." — Luma and Byte exchange a look.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Cosmo:** In full documentation mode. His expression is CONFIDENT, precise. He is the most calm person in the scene — the data tells him what the situation is and he trusts the data. His calculation of the projector is delivered without drama, which is what makes it land.
- **Luma:** Beginning to grasp the scale. ALARMED beat when the projector registers — she knows what that projector means (pre-credits tag, dead pixel). Her look to Byte is not communication, it is recognition.
- **Byte:** (through Luma) confirming Cosmo's analysis. His confirmation of Cosmo's calculation is the closest he comes to a compliment. Expression: SEARCHING — processing the implications of the scope.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** Contamination map in progress. The visual language of contamination spreading through internal wiring should be implied by the cyan halos forming a pattern — a path from the CRT toward the router, moving outward.

---

### A2-03 — The Social Cost Beat
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's kitchen (Luma's phone on the kitchen table, Luma at the CRT in the den doorway)
**Characters present:** Luma, Cosmo (Byte implied but not focal)
**Key visual beat:** Luma's phone lights up on the kitchen table — Dev Patel-Huang, third text in a row. She does not see it. Cosmo does. The scene is the pause between Cosmo's line and Luma's non-response.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Luma:** Completely absorbed in the contamination pattern. FOCUSED — but the focus is the problem. She is not choosing not to respond; she does not register the moment she could. That's the cost.
- **Cosmo:** His expression when he says "Dev asked me if I knew where you were" is careful lightness — he is registering a pattern and noting it without escalating it. He is not judging her. He is documenting.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** None. This scene must be clean of FX — it is entirely about two human beats in the middle of the genre plot. The contamination should not be visually present in this frame.
**Staging note:** 15 seconds maximum. No resolution. No drama. The weight is in the small pause.

---

### A2-04 — Escalation
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's house — den, kitchen, all rooms
**Characters present:** Luma, Cosmo, Byte, Grandma Miri (implied — her radio is activating)
**Key visual beat:** Every screen in the house activates simultaneously. Living room TV, tablet on the counter, kitchen radio display, old microwave display — all showing the same cyan static. Through every one of them: something pressing against the glass from inside.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Byte:** "We are out of time." — delivered quietly. This is the most alarmed Byte will ever look in the pilot. ALARMED expression, bilateral eye configuration (this is not Glitch, this is Byte facing scale he didn't expect).
- **Cosmo:** CONFIDENT — he processes the escalation into data immediately. "The source is still the CRT. The others are secondary breach points — sympathetic resonance with the primary." He is the calm center while the house activates around him. His line "Tell the entity I'll take that as a compliment" is earned and warm.
- **Luma:** ALARMED to DETERMINED. The breach through the CRT screen is now visible to her.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** Simultaneous activation of all screens — this is the pilot's visual escalation peak. Cyan static on every screen. Something pressing against glass from the inside on multiple surfaces simultaneously. The breach visible through the CRT screen: a torn place in the Glitch Layer, leaking cyan light. Small but growing.

### A2-05 — The Reach Decision
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's den, at the CRT
**Characters present:** Luma, Byte, Cosmo
**Key visual beat:** Luma looks at the breach, looks at Byte, asks "Can I reach in?" Byte's pause is the tell. She reaches in.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Luma:** DETERMINED with one quiet beat of SOFT_SURPRISE (the phone — four texts now, she looks at it once, puts it face-down). She does not register this as a choice. She turns to the CRT.
- **Byte:** His pause before answering is the most important staging note for this scene. He always answers immediately. When he pauses, something matters. Expression: SEARCHING holding — calculating something he knows and won't say. "You can. Whether you should is a different conversation and we do not have time for it." — this is not evasion; it is the first sign that he is protecting her from information.
- **Cosmo:** Present, not speaking in this beat. He is watching the decision happen.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** The breach through the CRT is the focal FX — growing, cycling through corrupt data, a visible tear. Luma reaching in is the act-two close. She puts her hands through the screen surface.

## Act Three (~6 minutes): The Breach

### A3-01 — Inside the Reach
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's den at the CRT — Luma framed in the screen aperture, face and arms through, body in the den. Glitch Layer visible through the breach.
**Characters present:** Luma (straddling the boundary), Byte (on her shoulder), Cosmo (in the den behind her)
**Key visual beat:** Luma is through from the waist up. Byte is on her shoulder and in this space he looks like he belongs — his pixel confetti trails stop. The breach is right in front of her: a seam in the Glitch Layer that has come undone.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Luma:** DETERMINED, present, methodical. She is not panicking. She is in a new space and she is documenting it in real time. Fast incomplete sentences.
- **Byte:** Changed. In the Glitch Layer, Byte reads differently — more confident, less reluctant. His confetti stops. His expression (from Luma's shoulder) is CONFIDENT or SEARCHING rather than GRUMPY. He belongs here and it shows.
- **Cosmo:** Cannot see what Luma sees but is relay-receiving her voice from through the CRT. He is writing.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** The Glitch Layer visible through the breach: ELEC_CYAN dominant, data streams, pixel flora, the general vocabulary of `LTG_ENV_other_side_bg.png`. Breach itself: a torn, cycling, corrupt-data seam. Byte's pixel confetti stops on the Glitch Layer side of the boundary.

### A3-02 — The Doubt-in-Certainty Beat
**Environment:** Luma's POV from inside the breach aperture, looking into the Glitch Layer and back toward the den
**Characters present:** Luma, Byte (on shoulder), Cosmo (voice only from den), kitchen floor visible through the CRT aperture
**Key visual beat:** Luma holds still for a half-beat. The internal monologue of a life told "you see things that aren't there" plays through her eyes — not through exposition. Her eyes go to the kitchen floor. The linoleum grid is exactly where she knew it would be.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Luma:** This is the pilot's emotional center. The expression journey is: DETERMINED → moment of naked SOFT_SURPRISE (doubt surfacing) → DETERMINED again, but different. The second DETERMINED is earned — she has done the math and chosen to trust herself. Her line to Cosmo ("The kitchen floor. Angle the phone at fifteen degrees to the left. You'll see the grid.") is delivered with precision, not drama.
- **Byte:** Still. He does not prompt her during the hold. He waits. This is the most important non-action Byte takes in the pilot — he does not fill her silence.
- **Cosmo:** "You mean the one you pointed out the first day?" — his delivery is warm, matter-of-fact. He wrote it down. He trusted her then. He is trusting her now.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** The Glitch Layer environment is present but not the focus — this scene is about Luma's interior and the physical detail of the kitchen floor. Minimize FX activity during the hold beat.
**Staging priority (per outline):** A3-02 is the storyboard's highest priority scene in Act Three. The half-beat of doubt is everything. If the storyboard does not land the hold, the pilot does not land.

---

### A3-03 — The Patch
**Environment:** Breach point, Glitch Layer edge
**Characters present:** Luma (in aperture), Byte (on shoulder), Cosmo (in den with the phone)
**Key visual beat:** Cosmo positions the phone. The clean geometric input from the kitchen floor grid enters the breach. The breach seals. Not dramatically — it stops being there, like a knot pulled tight. Every screen in the house goes dark simultaneously.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Byte:** "Hold." — one word, precise, authoritative. This is Byte in competence mode. CONFIDENT expression.
- **Luma:** Guiding the angle via Byte, watching the breach respond. DETERMINED throughout.
- **Cosmo:** Already positioning the phone before Luma finishes the instruction — he is ahead of her because he trusts her completely.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** The breach response: corrupt data cycling slows, stutters, stops. The seal is quiet — a knot pulled tight, not a dramatic closure. Every screen in the house going dark simultaneously is a beat of sound design as much as visual FX. The silence after is required.

---

### A3-04 — The Return
**Environment:** Grandma Miri's den, floor, near the CRT
**Characters present:** Luma, Byte (on her shoulder), Cosmo (in doorway), Grandma Miri (in doorway behind Cosmo)
**Key visual beat:** Grandma Miri appears in the doorway with tea. Her eyes go to the spot on Luma's left shoulder. "I thought that might be you." — to the shoulder. Not to Luma. To the shoulder.
**Emotional arc / expression context:**
- **Luma:** Sitting on the floor, coming down from the adrenaline. DETERMINED holding to SOFT_SURPRISE as Grandma Miri's line lands.
- **Cosmo:** Looking at the precise location of Byte on Luma's shoulder — not estimating, knowing. He is already writing when Grandma Miri speaks. His notebook is his way of holding what he cannot say.
- **Grandma Miri:** Absolute stillness. She sees Byte. She knows Byte. Her expression is the scene — do not let staging add warmth or softness; she should be the stillest element in the frame. Her knowledge looks like patience.
- **Byte:** Does not answer Grandma Miri. His cracked eye glitches once. The glitch is involuntary — it is the only thing he does.
**FX / Glitch Layer elements:** The cracked eye glitch on Byte — single-frame pixel scramble on the cracked eye only, not the full body. Involuntary. This is the scene's punctuation.
**Staging priority (per outline):** A3-04 is the storyboard's second-highest priority scene in Act Three. Grandma Miri's line must land on the shoulder — the staging of her eyeline is everything.
