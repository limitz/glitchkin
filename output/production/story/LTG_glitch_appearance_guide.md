# ANTAGONIST APPEARANCE GUIDE — GLITCH
**"Luma & the Glitchkin" — Episodes 5+**
**Author:** Priya Shah, Story & Script Developer
**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Cycle:** 39
**Status:** Canonical — staging reference for storyboarders

---

## About This Document

This guide gives storyboarders and directors concrete, reproducible staging language for Glitch's first appearance and subsequent entrances. It covers: how Glitch arrives, how it communicates, and how it exits. The goal is that any storyboarder new to the project reads this once and draws the same character.

For Glitch's visual design specification (body geometry, pixel eye system, expressions, color constants), see `output/characters/main/glitch.md`.

---

## 1. WHO GLITCH IS — THE ONE-LINE STAGING PREMISE

Glitch is a Glitchkin who chose order over chaos and was consumed by that choice. It is not a monster. It is a warning. Storyboarders must resist staging it as a villain-enters-room moment — it arrives like a decision that has already been made.

---

## 2. FIRST APPEARANCE — EPISODE 5 STAGING

### 2.1 Context

Glitch does not appear until Episode 5. Episodes 1–4 show only the Corruption's ambient signature: geometry at the edges of Glitch Layer scenes that is too organized, too precise. The first time the audience sees Glitch, they should already feel that something has been wrong for a while — and that this is what "wrong" has been building toward.

### 2.2 Entrance Behavior

**Glitch does not arrive — it was already there.**

The preferred first-appearance staging: Luma enters a Glitch Layer location she has visited before and something is different. The background geometry — always slightly chaotic, always slightly broken — has one organized section. Small. Precise. A platform corner with perfect right angles. Pixel flora arranged symmetrically. The kind of stillness that doesn't belong.

Glitch is found, not introduced. It is present in the organized section before Luma (or the audience) realizes what they're looking at. The CALCULATING expression is the default for first reveal.

**Beat sequence for Episode 5 first reveal:**
1. Establishing shot: familiar Glitch Layer location. Normal-ish chaos.
2. Luma and Byte moving through — routine. Byte's cracked eye glitches once (unprompted). He does not say anything.
3. Cut to: a corner of the environment. Something too still. A single organized object in the frame. Hold for one beat.
4. Cut back to Byte. His cracked eye glitches again. He goes quiet.
5. Camera drifts toward the organized corner. Glitch is there. Has been there. NEUTRAL expression, body slightly counter-clockwise (it is always slightly *wrong*). Hovering still.
6. Glitch registers Luma. Expression shifts: CALCULATING → COVETOUS (bilateral acid slit eyes — this is genuine, not performed). Lean toward subject.
7. Byte positions himself between Luma and Glitch. Does not explain why.
8. Glitch does not pursue. It observes.

**Director's note on the reveal beat:** The COVETOUS expression (bilateral — both eyes identical, acid slit) is a real interior state, not a performance. The audience should feel they are seeing something Glitch did not intend to show. This is the first crack in Glitch's control. Use it once per episode, maximum.

### 2.3 What Glitch Looks Like When It Arrives

- **Body posture:** NEUTRAL or CALCULATING on entry. Upright, level. The counter-clockwise resting tilt (see glitch.md §2.1) is always present — this is structural, not chosen.
- **Motion:** Glitch does not walk, run, or jump. It repositions. One frame it is in one place; the next it has shifted without visible transit. Easing curves do not apply. Treat as a cut, not a move.
- **Confetti:** Performance states = HOT_MAG + UV_PURPLE scatter below the bottom spike. Interior states = no confetti (YEARNING, HOLLOW) or minimal UV_PURPLE (COVETOUS). When Glitch is being watched, confetti is deliberate — part of the presentation. When Glitch is alone, confetti is absent.
- **Hover height:** Glitch hovers with its bottom spike approximately 8–10px above the ground plane at standard character scale. It does not touch the ground. Ever.

## 3. COMMUNICATION STYLE — NO ENGLISH

**Glitch does not speak English. It does not speak Glitchkin.** It communicates in geometric patterns and pixel-perfect commands.

### 3.1 What Communication Looks Like

Communication from Glitch occurs as:
1. **Eye glyph shift:** The pixel eyes cycle through states — one glyph to another in sequence — in a pattern that repeats. This is not a display. It is the communication itself, the same way Morse code is not a display of language but the language.
2. **Confetti geometrics:** The confetti below the bottom spike briefly organizes into shapes — grids, angles, specific pixel arrangements — before dispersing. These are not decorative. They are commands or statements.
3. **Environmental response:** When Glitch "speaks" to corrupted Glitchkin under its influence, those Glitchkin respond physically — not because they heard words, but because they received instructions through the digital substrate. Storyboarders: if Glitch directs a corrupted Glitchkin, draw the command as a visible pulse traveling from Glitch's confetti trail to the target, one beat before the Glitchkin changes behavior.

### 3.2 What Communication Feels Like to Luma and Byte

**Luma** cannot parse Glitch's geometric communication directly. She sees patterns. She wants to translate them. She cannot. This is a consistent frustration — Luma is used to noticing things and having them mean something, and Glitch is a language she cannot read.

**Byte** can parse it, or at least partly can. He will not say this. Storyboarders: when Glitch communicates, give Byte a beat of stillness before he moves or responds — not a verbal reaction, a physical one. This tells the audience that Byte received something, without Byte saying so.

### 3.3 What Glitch Does NOT Do

- **Does not posture for effect.** The MISCHIEVOUS expression (asymmetric eyes, scheming lean) is rarely used in front of Luma. Glitch's version of threat is stillness, not motion.
- **Does not taunt.** Glitch does not explain its plans, mock the heroes, or announce intent. It observes, assesses, and acts. Monologuing is not in its vocabulary — literally.
- **Does not make noise.** Glitch is silent. No hum, no crackle, no audio signature when present. The absence of the Glitch Layer's ambient chaos is itself the audio cue that Glitch is nearby.

## 4. EXIT BEHAVIOR

**Glitch leaves the same way it arrived: by already being gone.**

Exit staging: the scene continues. Characters react to Glitch. When the camera returns to Glitch's position, or drifts back to it after a cutaway, the organized section is no longer organized. The stillness is over. Glitch is absent without transition.

**One exception:** Episode 7 (or whenever the first full confrontation occurs) should show Glitch choosing to leave rather than being driven out. This is important. Glitch is never defeated in an Episode 5–8 encounter — it departs. The distinction matters for the season's pacing. If Glitch departs voluntarily, the audience should feel that the encounter went exactly as Glitch intended.

Exit expression: typically CALCULATING (after observing/assessing) or HOLLOW (after a genuine interior-state moment). Never exits on TRIUMPHANT — that reads as final defeat, which is a season finale beat.

---

## 5. INTERIOR STATE MOMENTS — USE SPARINGLY

Glitch has three interior state expressions (YEARNING, COVETOUS, HOLLOW). These are the moments when Glitch is not performing — when something genuine is visible. Rules for staging:

- **One interior expression per act, maximum.** These are rare. If used too often, they lose meaning.
- **YEARNING** is the hardest to stage correctly. Glitch is watching Luma have something — connection, purpose, the confidence of perception — that Glitch cannot name and cannot access. It was a Glitchkin. It had a community. It surrendered that to the Corruption and gained power and lost the thing it was watching Luma have. Stage YEARNING at a distance, always. Glitch is never close when it YEARNS. UV eyes, arms hanging, no confetti. Hold the beat one frame longer than comfortable.
- **HOLLOW** is for aftermath — after a failed attempt, after a loss. It should feel like absence. The body is still. The eyes are a single white center pixel. Nothing moves. Do not fill the beat.
- **Do not explain interior states in dialogue.** Not Byte's dialogue, not Luma's. The audience should read these moments without narration.

## 6. BYTE-GLITCH HISTORY — STAGING IMPLICATIONS

Byte and Glitch have shared history. Byte resisted the Corruption and was exiled from the Glitch Layer community. Glitch surrendered to the Corruption and was elevated. This is the inversion at the heart of their relationship.

**What this means in staging:**
- When Byte and Glitch are in the same frame, Byte is always between Glitch and Luma. This is not direction — it is instinct. Storyboarders should not need to be told this; it should be the natural block.
- Byte does not face away from Glitch. Ever. Even when Byte is pretending not to see Glitch, his body is angled such that his functional eye (the non-cracked one) has Glitch in its field.
- Glitch does not direct communication at Byte. It addresses Luma, or the corrupted Glitchkin, or nothing. Byte is not an audience for Glitch's performance — Glitch knows Byte can see through it.
- The ONE exception: in the season finale, Glitch will communicate with Byte directly. That communication is bilateral, not geometric — both of Glitch's eyes identical, an interior state. Reserve this.

## 7. QUICK REFERENCE — STAGING DO/DON'T

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Arrive without transit — cut, don't move | Walk, run, or ease into frame |
| Enter at NEUTRAL or CALCULATING | Enter at TRIUMPHANT or MISCHIEVOUS |
| Hold one beat past comfort on interior states | Cut away from interior states early |
| Show Byte's physical response to Glitch's communication | Have Byte translate aloud what Glitch said |
| Let silence be the audio cue for Glitch's presence | Add ambient hum or crackle to Glitch |
| Use the counter-clockwise body lean (it's structural) | Draw Glitch perfectly upright — it is never level |
| Place Byte between Glitch and Luma by instinct | Direct this explicitly in panel direction |
| Exit by absence — Glitch was there; now it isn't | Chase scene, dramatic retreat, or driven-off-frame |
| Interior states at distance | Interior states in close-up (these are private) |
