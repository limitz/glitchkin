# Luma & the Glitchkin — Pitch Brief

**Prepared by:** Alex Chen, Art Director
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Version:** v001

---

## Premise

When twelve-year-old Luma discovers that a glitching CRT television in her grandmother's kitchen has been broadcasting living, self-aware digital creatures called Glitchkin, she must help a rogue Glitchkin named Byte escape the corrupting force tearing apart the Glitch Layer — before it tears apart the real world too.

## Tone

A comedy-adventure with genuine emotional stakes: fast, funny, and warm on the surface, but underneath it is a story about trust between an unlikely pair — the kind of trust that can only exist when one of you is technically imaginary.

## Audience

Children 8–12 and their families; speaks directly to kids who feel like they see the world differently than everyone around them.

## Format

22-minute episodic animated series; pilot season of 13 episodes; self-contained episode stories with a serialised season arc building toward Byte's permanent integration into the real world.

## Visual Identity Statement

*Luma & the Glitchkin* lives at the intersection of hand-drawn warmth and digital geometry. Luma's world is rendered in amber, terracotta, and cream — the palette of analogue life. The Glitch Layer is void-black, electric cyan, and UV purple — the palette of broken data. Every scene the two worlds touch, the color systems collide. Characters are drawn on clean three-tier line weight, legible at thumbnail scale, and designed to read in silhouette at any frame. Nothing in the show looks like a 3D render. Everything looks like it was drawn by someone who loves cartoons.
