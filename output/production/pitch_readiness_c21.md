## 1-2 Remaining Risks Entering Critique 10

### Risk 1 — SF02 Window Pane Alpha (LOW-MEDIUM)
`output/color/style_frames/LTG_COLOR_styleframe_glitch_storm.png`

Sam's Cycle 20 verification flagged the window pane rectangles at alpha 160–180, above the recommended 90–110 target. The projected glow cones are correctly calibrated and carry the atmospheric warmth reading. Sam's assessment is that this is not blocking — panes function as near-field lit windows, not competing key lights. However, critics with strong lighting instincts (Naomi, Takeshi) may flag the panes as too dominant against the cold storm palette. Mitigation is a single-pass v005 reducing win_colors alpha to 100/90 — no structural rework required. Risk is manageable but should be on the brief sheet going into the critique.

### Risk 2 — No Standalone One-Page Pitch Brief (MEDIUM)
The pitch_package_index.md flags this as NOT VERIFIED. The production bible covers the narrative premise in full, but a standalone one-page summary — premise, tone, target audience, episode format, and visual identity statement — is standard practice for any external pitch presentation. If Critique 10 includes critics evaluating the package as a buyer would receive it, the absence of a pitch brief leaves the package requiring navigation rather than delivering an immediate first impression. This is the one gap that cannot be addressed retroactively with a regen — it requires original writing.
