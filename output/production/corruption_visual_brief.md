# The Corruption — Visual Design Brief
# "Luma & the Glitchkin" — Season 1 Antagonist

**Document Author:** Alex Chen, Art Director
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** APPROVED — BRIEF ALL VISUAL TEAM MEMBERS ON THIS DOCUMENT

---

## Premise

The Corruption is the visual villain of Season 1. This document defines what it IS, what it LOOKS LIKE, and how it BEHAVES visually so that every artist on the team can draw it consistently. Read this document before designing any frame, scene, or asset that involves The Corruption.

---

## Part 1 — What IS The Corruption?

The Corruption is not a creature. It is not a person. It is not an evil mastermind giving orders.

**The Corruption is a system failure.** It is what happens when data rots. It is the digital equivalent of a disease that has achieved a kind of terrible coherence — not intelligence, but *pattern*. Like a fungus that has grown large enough to direct its own growth. Like a forest fire that has burned long enough that it starts making the weather that feeds it.

It is old. It predates Byte. It predates the Glitchkin. It predates Grandma Miri's CRT monitor. It has been growing in the deep architecture of the Glitch Layer for a long time, slowly consuming, slowly spreading. It does not hate Luma. It does not hate the Glitchkin. It does not *hate* anything. It consumes because that is what it does. It corrupts because that is its nature.

This is what makes it frightening: **it has no face to reason with, no voice to negotiate with, no heart to change.** It is a process. And processes don't stop until they're interrupted or they run out of data to consume.

For storytelling purposes, The Corruption manifests local "expressions" — concentrated masses of corrupted data that function like limbs or appendages, seeking out new material to consume. These expressions are what the characters interact with directly. They are not individuals. They are the same Corruption, reaching.

## Part 2 — What Does It Look Like?

### Core Visual Concept
**The Corruption looks like data that has been eaten from the inside out.**

Imagine a perfect pixel-art image that has been attacked by entropy. The colors bleed. The geometry dissolves. The edges become uncertain. Where the Corruption touches something, it overwrites that thing's visual logic — its shapes, its colors, its motion. The Corruption does not add anything to what it touches. It subtracts. It voids.

The Corruption does not have clean edges. It does not have a definitive boundary. Its outer perimeter is a contested zone — a fringe where the Corruption is actively fighting the surrounding data, consuming it into itself. This fringe is the most visually active part of it.

At its core, The Corruption is darker and stiller. The core is where the fight is already over.

---

### Shape Language

The Corruption's shape language is **inverted triangles and fragmented shapes** per the style guide. More specifically:

- **At small scale (a tendril, a localized infection):** Long, thin, jagged spears — like crack lines in glass. They branch irregularly, always pointing outward and downward (gravity toward the void). They taper to sharp 1-pixel points.

- **At medium scale (an expression, a concentrated mass):** Irregular polygon clusters — a central mass of interlocking rectangular and trapezoidal chunks, like a 3D shape that's been badly voxelized. The mass has no perfectly right angles — everything is 1–3 degrees off from orthogonal, creating a subtle wrongness. Nothing about it is stable.

- **At large scale (a Stage 3 event, the full manifestation):** A churning, shifting void-mass. The silhouette is constantly changing — chunks of the outer layer break off and re-absorb, tendrils extend and retract, the whole form breathes with slow, terrible rhythm. No frame should show the same silhouette as the previous frame. It is never still.

---

### Color

The Corruption's palette is built entirely from the **Void/Dark end** of the show's color range. It does NOT use the Glitch palette's bright, energetic colors. Those colors are life. The Corruption has consumed life.

**Core Colors:**

| Name | Hex | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Corruption Core | #0A0A14 | Void Black — the deepest, stillest part. Where the Corruption has fully claimed territory. |
| Corruption Mid | #1A0A20 | A near-black with a faint purple undertone. The main body color. Suggests UV Purple (#7B2FBE) drained of energy. |
| Corruption Fringe | #3D0030 | A very dark, desaturated magenta — the color of Hot Magenta (#FF2D6B) after it's been corrupted. The fringe where active consumption is happening. |
| Corruption Pulse | #FF2D6B | Hot Magenta at 30–40% opacity, in brief strobing flashes — NOT the body color, but a visible pulse that occasionally fires through the mass, like a terrible heartbeat. These pulses are the only bright color the Corruption ever shows. |
| Corruption Artifacts | #7B2FBE | UV Purple — appears in corrupted objects and environments, left behind after the Corruption has passed. The "stain" it leaves. Not part of the Corruption itself. |

**What the Corruption does NOT contain:**
- Electric Cyan (#00F0FF) — that is Byte's color, life energy, hope
- Acid Green (#39FF14) — that is Glitchkin vitality
- Soft Gold (#E8C95A) — the warmth of the real world
- Any warm-palette color from the real world

When the Corruption approaches the real world, the real-world colors in that zone begin to drain — they desaturate toward grey before being consumed entirely. This desaturation is a visual early warning.

---

### How It Moves

The Corruption has two modes of motion:

**Passive/Ambient Motion (always occurring):**
The body texture constantly shifts. Think of it as a very slow, very dark lava lamp — or a black oil slick being disturbed by something beneath the surface. Individual blocks within the mass slide and rotate incrementally. Never more than 2–3 pixels of movement per frame at the body level. This is subtle — the audience should feel it rather than see it. It communicates "this thing is alive" without spending FX budget.

**Active/Seeking Motion (when the Corruption extends toward a target):**
When the Corruption senses data to consume (Glitchkin, digital objects, people carrying glitch energy), it extends **tendril expressions.** These move fast — much faster than the body's ambient motion. A tendril can cross a full room's width in 8–12 frames. But they are not graceful. They move in sharp RIGHT-ANGLE paths — they travel horizontally, then vertically, then horizontally. Never diagonally. This gives them a deeply mechanical, grid-bound quality, like data moving through a circuit board. This is the most alien thing about the Corruption's movement. Nothing organic moves like this. Nothing natural moves like this. It looks *wrong.*

**Consumption Motion (when it touches something):**
When a tendril contacts a target, the consumption begins as a bloom of Corruption Fringe color (#3D0030) spreading outward from the contact point, like ink dropped in water. The bloom moves in all directions simultaneously, replacing the target's color and geometry with Corruption Mid (#1A0A20) chunks. The target's linework breaks into fragments. Within 12–18 frames of full consumption, the target has been replaced entirely with a static chunk of Corruption material, indistinguishable from the main body.

## Part 3 — How It Differs From Byte and the Glitchkin

This is critical for visual clarity. The audience must always be able to distinguish The Corruption from the show's protagonists and friendly characters.

### The Core Visual Opposition

| Dimension | Byte & Glitchkin | The Corruption |
|---|---|---|
| **Color** | Bright, saturated: Cyan, Green, Magenta in full brightness | Dark, drained: near-black with only faint color echoes |
| **Shape** | Triangular/jagged but with ROUNDNESS at edges | Strictly rectilinear, hard 90-degree geometry |
| **Edges** | Jittery, lively, oscillating — feels like a heartbeat | Blocky, consuming — feels like an erasure |
| **Motion** | Drift, tumble, float — dimensional but natural-feeling | Right-angle paths, grid-bound, mechanical |
| **Particle FX** | Pixel confetti: bright, cheerful, scattered | Corruption bloom: dark, spreading, directional |
| **Eyes** | Glitchkin have expressive eyes, personality | The Corruption has no face, no eyes, no expression |
| **Energy Direction** | Radiating outward — Glitchkin give off light and color | Consuming inward — The Corruption drains light and color |
| **Sound (reference)** | High-pitched digital chirps, electric sparks | Sub-bass drone, digital grinding, silence |

### The Key Distinction
**Glitchkin have personality. The Corruption has none.** This is the deepest visual difference. When you draw a Glitchkin, even a mischievous or threatening one, there is some element of character — a pose, an expression, a shape that suggests interiority. The Corruption has no interiority. It has no front and back. It has no "face you." It extends toward what it wants with the same indifference in all directions. Drawing the Corruption should always feel like drawing a force, not a character.

## Part 4 — The Three Visual "Tells"

These are the three recognizable signatures of The Corruption's presence. The audience will learn to read these tells over the course of Season 1. They should appear consistently, early, and in subtle ways before the first full Corruption event — so that by the time the crisis lands, the audience realizes they've been seeing warnings for episodes.

---

### Tell 1 — The Desaturation Halo

**What it is:** In any environment where The Corruption has been active or is approaching, there is a zone of color desaturation. Warm colors lose their warmth. Greens go grey-green. Reds go muddy. The glitch palette colors (where they appear) dim and lose saturation. This halo is centered on the Corruption's location or its recent path.

**How to draw it:** A full-composition Hue/Saturation adjustment layer, masked to a soft-edged region around the Corruption's zone. Saturation reduction: 30–50% within the zone. The edge of the zone should be gradual, not sharp — the audience should feel the desaturation before they can consciously identify it.

**Early season use:** In early episodes, before the Corruption is revealed, show brief moments where a corner of the frame seems slightly grey — a patch of floor that looks washed out, a monitor screen that's dimmer than it should be. Plant the tell before the explanation.

**Key rule:** No desaturation halo in scenes where the Corruption is absent. The tell must be reliable to be meaningful.

---

### Tell 2 — Right-Angle Cracks

**What it is:** Wherever The Corruption has touched or passed through, it leaves geometrically precise CRACK patterns — right-angle fracture lines. These are NOT natural-looking cracks (which are irregular and branching). These are pixel-art cracks: perfectly horizontal and vertical, meeting at 90-degree angles, forming grid-like fracture patterns.

**How to draw them:** They look like the gridlines of a broken tile floor, but applied to any surface — wood, metal, air, light. They are UV Purple (#7B2FBE) at 60–70% opacity, with a 1px soft glow in #3D0030 (Corruption Fringe) at the fracture edges. They do not move after being placed — they are permanent until the Corruption or the heroes address them.

**Scale:** Tendrils leave thin cracks (1–2px wide). A Stage 2 event leaves wide cracks (4–6px). A Stage 3 event leaves fractures large enough to walk through.

**Early season use:** A broken monitor that shows suspicious grid-like fracture lines along its screen edge rather than the natural star-burst of normal impact damage. A hallway in the school with a patch of wall where the paint seems to have cracked in unusually regular patterns. These are Easter eggs for attentive viewers.

---

### Tell 3 — The Pulse

**What it is:** The Corruption has a "heartbeat" — a periodic pulse of Hot Magenta (#FF2D6B) that travels through its body and, at larger scales, through corrupted objects and environments. At Stage 1, this pulse is slow and faint (every 3–4 seconds, 1–2 frames of faint Magenta 20% opacity moving through the mass). At Stage 3, the pulse is fast and bright (every 0.5 seconds, full Magenta flash).

**How to draw it:** A radial wipe (not a flash — it TRAVELS, from core to fringe) of Hot Magenta at low opacity, animated as a frame-by-frame effect. The pulse begins deep in the core and radiates outward through the body before dissipating at the fringe. It looks like bioluminescence in a deep-sea creature — something inside the void briefly lighting up.

**Why it works as a visual tell:** Hot Magenta is the show's danger color. Its presence in The Corruption is the visual system's alarm signal. The audience learns: when you see that Magenta pulse, something is wrong. This conditions the audience to feel dread at Magenta in ways that pay off during the full Corruption events.

**Early season use:** A monitor screen that briefly shows a Magenta pulse crossing it horizontally (one frame) in the background of a normal scene. Easy to miss. Very frightening in retrospect.

## Part 5 — The Three Stages of a Corruption Event

A "Corruption event" is any scene where The Corruption is actively manifesting and posing a threat. There are three stages of escalation.

---

### Stage 1 — Contamination
**"Something is wrong, but the characters might not know yet"**

#### Visual Description
A Stage 1 event is localized. The Corruption is present as a small mass — perhaps the size of a basketball to a microwave — or as a cluster of tendrils that have not yet formed a coherent body. It may be confined to a single object (a monitor, a digital storage device, a piece of Glitch Layer infrastructure).

**Visual markers:**
- The Desaturation Halo is present within a 2–3 meter radius of the site.
- Right-angle cracks visible on the affected object or surface.
- Pulse: slow (every 3–4 seconds), dim (20% opacity Magenta).
- Particle confetti in the area: Level 1–2, but with a notable difference — some of the confetti particles near the Stage 1 site are NOT bright cyan or green. They are the grey-purple (#1A0A20 / #3D0030) of Corruption material. A few corrupted particles among the normal ones. Easy to miss. Deeply wrong.
- The texture of the affected surface shows "pixel rot" — individual pixels within the affected zone have been swapped to Corruption Fringe (#3D0030) or Corruption Mid (#1A0A20) in irregular patches, like dead spots in a lawn.

**Character reaction cue:** Byte responds first — a physical flinch, ears back, a held breath. Byte knows what this is before anyone else does.

**Duration before escalating (if not interrupted):** Stage 1 can persist for a while — it's the incubation period. In a single episode, Stage 1 might be present for 15–20 minutes of screen time before becoming Stage 2.

---

### Stage 2 — Spread
**"The characters are in active danger; the environment is changing"**

#### Visual Description
The Corruption has grown. It now fills a room or a section of the Glitch Layer. Tendrils extend actively and purposefully toward targets. Multiple corrupted objects visible. The scale of the presence makes it unmissable.

**Visual markers:**
- The Desaturation Halo now covers the entire visible environment. Background art color-script must account for this — the background should be re-colored with reduced saturation for any shot inside the Stage 2 zone.
- Right-angle cracks across multiple surfaces, some large enough to see clearly (4px wide).
- Pulse: medium pace (every 2 seconds), 35% opacity Magenta, now visible as a surface wash across the Corruption body.
- Particle confetti: Level 3 (40–80 particles), but with 30–40% of particles being "corrupted" — dark-colored, moving in right-angle paths instead of drift paths. The particle system itself is being consumed.
- Full Corruption body visible: a churning, shifting mass of #0A0A14 / #1A0A20 chunks, fringe of #3D0030 active at all contested edges.
- Tendrils visible: extending in right-angle paths toward the protagonists. Characters must actively dodge.
- Objects partially consumed show the "mid-state" of consumption: half the object's color is replaced by Corruption colors, geometry is fragmenting, the consumed half has lost all line-art (replaced by flat Corruption Mid blocks).

**Character reaction cue:** Byte manifests at maximum intensity (all visual tells active: glow bright, fins/features at maximum expression). This is a fight, not an evasion.

**Environment requirement:** Background artists must prepare a "Stage 2 variant" of any Glitch Layer or digital-world environment — the same location, but with reduced saturation, cracks, and Corruption mass presence. This is a separate BG layer that can be composited over the clean BG.

---

### Stage 3 — The Cascade
**"This is a crisis. The rules have changed."**

#### Visual Description
The Corruption has achieved a scale where it threatens the structural integrity of a significant system — a district of the Glitch Layer, a large section of reality, something irreversible. This is the season climax visual state. Use once.

**Visual markers:**
- Desaturation is near-complete. The environment's original colors are barely legible — a ghost of what the location once looked like. Only character art should retain strong saturation (this is intentional: the characters are the only color left in a grey, consumed world).
- The sky (if exterior) shows color banding — like a corrupted JPEG: bands of near-black separated by thin lines of #1A0A20 and #3D0030. The sky itself is being eaten.
- The main Corruption body is enormous — filling the background of compositions, or visible past the edges of frame (it is larger than the frame can contain).
- Tendrils are now MULTIPLE and SIMULTANEOUS — the body extends in several directions at once, overwhelmingly.
- Pulse: Rapid (every 0.5 seconds). Full Hot Magenta (#FF2D6B) washes crossing the body. At Stage 3, the Magenta is no longer a tell — it is a dominant visual feature. The Corruption at Stage 3 actually GLOWS. This is the terrifying inversion: the dark void that drains light is now, at maximum power, emitting terrible light.
- Particle confetti: Level 4 Chaos, with 40–50% corrupted particles. The distinction between "Byte's particles" and "Corruption particles" is breaking down — the show's visual vocabulary is being destroyed. This feels like the world coming apart at the seams.
- Corrupted objects and environments: fully consumed areas show NO texture, NO linework, NO color variation. They are flat Corruption Core (#0A0A14) — pixel-perfect void. Like looking at a hole in the image.

**The visual that defines Stage 3:** There should be at least one composition where Byte's Electric Cyan (#00F0FF) glow and the Corruption's Hot Magenta pulse are both fully visible in the same frame — the two extremes of the show's color spectrum fighting each other. Cyan vs. Magenta. Life vs. entropy. This is the show's thesis image, and Stage 3 is when it finally, fully manifests.

**Resolution visual cue:** When The Corruption is defeated or driven back, Stage 3 reverses in cascade: Magenta glow extinguishes first. Then the body retreats. Then the right-angle cracks slowly close (unlike normal cracks, these HEAL, pixel by pixel). Then color returns to the environment — a slow, beautiful re-saturation that reverses the desaturation sequence. The return of warm amber light (#E8C95A Soft Gold) flooding back into the frame is the visual confirmation that the crisis is over.

## Summary Checklist — Drawing The Corruption

Before submitting any frame containing The Corruption for review, verify:

- [ ] No bright Glitch palette colors (Cyan, Green) in the Corruption's body
- [ ] Movement of tendrils is right-angle only — no diagonals, no curves
- [ ] Hot Magenta appears ONLY as a pulse/flash (brief and internal) not as a body color
- [ ] Desaturation halo is present in surrounding environment
- [ ] Right-angle cracks are visible on any surfaces the Corruption has contacted
- [ ] Character art is MORE saturated than the Corruption-affected environment (characters pop)
- [ ] The Corruption has NO FACE — no eyes, no expression, no readable emotion
- [ ] Stage designation is marked in your file — Stage 1, 2, or 3 — so density is appropriate
