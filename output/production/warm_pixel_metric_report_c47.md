## Methodology

### Why warm-pixel-percentage replaces hue-split

The prior metric (hue-split) measured the circular distance between the
median PIL HSV hue of the top half and bottom half of the image. This fails
for single-temperature-dominant interiors: when both halves are warm, the
separation is near zero even though the scene is unambiguously warm-lit.

C46 calibration showed 6/7 real interior reference photos fell below the
REAL_INTERIOR=12.0 hue-split threshold (median separation: 2.30).

Warm-pixel-percentage directly measures what we care about: how much of the
image is warm-toned. It correctly classifies all reference photos and all
generated assets with a 24-point gap between Real World and Glitch.
