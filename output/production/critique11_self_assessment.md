# Art Director's Self-Assessment — Pre-Critique 11
## "Luma & the Glitchkin" Pitch Package

**Prepared by:** Alex Chen, Art Director
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Cycle:** 24
**Context:** This is an honest internal assessment written before critics review the package. Its purpose is to identify real weaknesses before they are surfaced externally, and to document what I would fix if given one more cycle.

---

## What Is Genuinely Strong

**The style frame triptych (SF01 → SF02 → SF03)** is the backbone of this pitch. Taken in sequence, it tells the entire visual grammar of the show in three images: warm/handmade real world, disruption at the border, cold/digital other side. SF01 is the strongest single asset we have — Victoria's A+ grade is deserved. The ghost-Byte calibration in v003 (the alpha fix from Cycle 13) was the right call. SF02's storefront shattered window and per-window warm glow (v005) resolved four cycles of critic complaints and now reads correctly: domestic warmth vs. cold storm sky. SF03's Byte body fix (GL-01b replacing the critical Void Black error) transformed a spec-violation into a strong statement about digital space. All three are pitch-ready and form a coherent arc.

**Grandma Miri's expression sheet v002** is the single biggest quality leap in the package's history — from Dmitri's C grade (face-only, no body language) to a full-body rebuild with five distinct postures and silhouettes. A buyer who glances at this sheet understands who Miri is emotionally. This is the asset that shows the most visible improvement across the review cycle.

**The storyboard package** is structurally complete and tells a real story. Cold open (25 panels), Act 1 contact sheet, Act 2 contact sheet (12 panels) — the arc from first discovery to emotional climax is documented. The A2-07b kitchen doorway bridge panel is a small beat that earns a lot: it gives Miri weight and foreshadows the Act 2 emotional turn without announcing it.

**The Glitch character (Cycle 23 addition)** is a smart, clean antagonist design. Diamond/rhombus body is an instant visual antonym to the protagonist ensemble's rounded/organic shapes. CORRUPT_AMBER primary reads as digital-corruption-danger without being generic villain orange. The dual-pixel-eye system (stable glyph left, destabilized bleed right) is a strong piece of character writing expressed through design.

---

## What Is Vulnerable

**The Glitch character sheet is too small.** This is a functional issue, not an aesthetic one. On a 2×2 grid at 800×800px, each expression panel is 400×400 — Glitch's rendered body occupies roughly 25–30% of that area. At thumbnail or deck scale, the expression reads are hard to parse. Buyers flipping through a pitch deck at speed will not get the MISCHIEVOUS vs. TRIUMPHANT vs. PANICKED distinctions without leaning in. Every other character sheet — Luma 3×2 at 1200×900, Byte 3×3 at 784×1074 — gives the character more real estate. Glitch is being undersold.

**Color model visual PNGs for Luma, Byte, and Cosmo do not exist as pitch-facing assets.** We have `.md` specification documents, but a buyer cannot show a `.md` file on a screen. Grandma Miri and Glitch both have visual color model PNGs (Cycle 17 and Cycle 23 respectively). The three leads — Luma, Byte, Cosmo — do not. This is a meaningful asymmetry in the character package. If a studio color department asks "can I see the palette," we hand them an MD file.

**The logo placeholder question is unresolved on disk.** `show_logo.png` may still carry "A cartoon series by the Dream Team" as a tagline. `LTG_BRAND_logo_asymmetric_v002.png` is the safe external-facing alternative, but the index still carries a caveat note on this. A critic looking at the package for the first time and seeing internal production credit in the pitch logo will flag it immediately. This should have been cleanly resolved, not "use the alternative."

**The Luma Act 2 lean (-5° vs. target -8°) has been marked LOW for three cycles.** It is not blocking the pitch, but it is a known spec deviation that has been deferred cycle after cycle. After 23+ cycles, this pattern of deferral is visible. A director who lets a documented spec deviation sit for three cycles is not inspiring confidence in their own standards.

---

## What I Would Fix With One More Day

1. **Expand Glitch expression sheet to a larger canvas** — regenerate at 1200×900, 3×2 grid (even with only 4 expressions, the larger panels would allow one empty labelled slot: "MORE TO COME"). The character needs more presence on the page.

2. **Generate visual color model PNGs for Luma, Byte, and Cosmo** — the Grandma Miri color model generator (Sam Kowalski, Cycle 17) is a template. Three 30-minute generation passes would close this gap completely.

3. **Audit and resolve the show_logo.png vs. logo_asymmetric_v002.png question** — pick one canonical external logo, strike the caveat from the index, and call it done.

---

## Headline Assessment

The package is structurally complete and defends itself at a first pitch meeting. The visual narrative is coherent. The strongest assets are genuinely strong. The weaknesses are real but none of them will cause a pitch to fail — they will cause a critic to mark down "polish" and "completeness" rather than "concept" or "vision." The concept and vision are in good shape. The polish has two or three visible seams.

**Honest grade: A-**
Strong enough to pitch. Not strong enough to walk away from critique without a correction list.
