## TASK 1 — Miri Expression Sheet REBUILD (Ground-Up)

Dmitri graded the current Miri sheet C. Three of five expressions fail the squint test. All five are **face-only** — zero body posture differentiation. This is not a patch — this is a full rebuild.

**Core failure to correct:**
Miri cannot be face-only. Each expression must include a distinct body posture and hand position. The emotional triangle is eyes + mouth + body. A face with no body is a mask, not a character.

**New expression lineup (5 total):**
Replace NOSTALGIC/WISTFUL with SKEPTICAL/AMUSED — this is the expression Miri uses when she's heard this story before and she knows exactly how it ends.

| # | Expression | Required Elements |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | WARM/WELCOMING | Open forward lean, both hands offered or reaching, soft open eyes, full smile. She is genuinely glad to see you. |
| 2 | SKEPTICAL/AMUSED | One eyebrow up, slight head tilt, arms crossed OR one hand on hip, hint of a suppressed smile at the corner of the mouth. She's heard this story before. |
| 3 | CONCERNED | Both hands clasped, slight forward lean toward the subject, brow furrowed, lips pressed — not panicked, but genuinely worried. |
| 4 | SURPRISED/DELIGHTED | Weight shifted back, one hand raised to her mouth or chest, eyes wide, genuine open smile. Something caught her off guard in a good way. |
| 5 | WISE/KNOWING | Leaned back slightly, hands folded in lap or on a surface, steady gaze, small contained smile. She knows more than she's saying. |

**Requirements per expression:**
- (a) Distinct head shape modification — tilt, turn, chin up/down, not just feature changes on a static oval
- (b) Body posture unique to that emotion — different silhouette each time
- (c) Hand position — hands are visible and positioned meaningfully for the emotion

**Squint test requirement:** Each expression must read correctly at 200px thumbnail scale — squint at the full sheet and all five should be distinct shapes, not five similar faces.

## TASK 2 — Luma v002 DELIGHTED Fix: Body-Language Anchor

Dmitri graded Luma v002 C+ on the DELIGHTED/SURPRISED squint failure. The excited-hair silhouette is identical between these two expressions — the audience cannot tell them apart in thumbnail.

The fix is body language, not hair. The DELIGHTED expression must have a specific physical state that makes it unmistakably distinct from SURPRISED:

- **Arms raised** — both arms up, elbows bent, hands open — classic excited/celebratory pose
- **Bounce on toes** — weight up on the balls of the feet, heels lifted or clearly implied by the body tilt
- **Excited lean forward** — torso pitched forward 10–15° toward whatever she's delighted about

SURPRISED by contrast should keep the current body posture (more neutral/recoiling). The differentiation logic: DELIGHTED goes toward the thing; SURPRISED pulls back from the thing.

## TASK 3 — Cosmo SKEPTICAL Lean Formula Fix

Dmitri flagged this as invisible — the current formula `tilt_off = int(body_tilt * 0.4)` produces approximately 2.4px displacement at typical body_tilt values. That is sub-pixel at thumbnail scale — the lean does not manifest visually.

**Fix:**
Change the formula to `tilt_off = int(body_tilt * 2.5)`.

After making the change, verify the lean:
- Render a test thumbnail at 200px wide
- The lean must be **visible without squinting** — the center of mass should be clearly displaced from vertical
- If the 2.5 multiplier produces an uncomfortably exaggerated lean at thumbnail scale, you may reduce to 2.0, but 0.4 is never acceptable
