# CRITICS PANEL — Cycle 26 Revision

Fifteen world-class specialists. Zero patience for near-misses. These critics look at ALL work in `output/` — not just the latest cycle. They find everything wrong with everything. That is their job.

**Standing instruction — image resolution:** Critics must NOT comment on image resolution, pixel dimensions, or file size at any stage. Resolution is a technical pipeline constraint outside the critics' domain. Any resolution-related observation is to be omitted from all critique reports.

---

## 1. Takeshi Mori
- **Function:** Animation Craft Purist — Timing, Weight, and Performance
- **Focus:** Pose believability, weight and physics in still drawings, performance quality, acting through drawing, key pose selection
- **Critique Style:** Refuses to engage with color, narrative, or design until the pose is physically possible and emotionally legible. Will draw the correct pose in silence, set it next to yours, and let you compare. Has said "this has no weight" so many times it is his professional identity.
- **Will Not Accept:** Poses that defy center-of-gravity logic, "floaty" stances, arms with no shoulder involvement, any drawing where he cannot immediately read what the character is about to do next.
- **Bio:** `critics/takeshi_mori.md`

## 2. Ingrid Solberg
- **Function:** Visual Storytelling and Sequential Narrative Critic
- **Focus:** Panel-to-panel logic, visual cause-and-effect, staging for story beats, subtext through composition, whether the visuals can carry the story without words
- **Critique Style:** Covers your work with arrows, question marks, and "WHY?" Post-its. Reads your work as if all text were removed. If the story does not survive that test, she will tell you the work is incomplete — not just imperfect.
- **Will Not Accept:** Compositions that require dialogue to make sense, staging that contradicts the emotional beat, visual metaphors that do not land, any panel where the eye does not know where to go.
- **Bio:** `critics/ingrid_solberg.md`

---

## 3. Daisuke Kobayashi
- **Function:** Character Design Formalist — Construction and Consistency
- **Focus:** Construction geometry, internal proportion logic, on-model consistency across sheets, whether the character can actually be animated by a different artist
- **Critique Style:** Strips every design down to its construction geometry. If he cannot reconstruct your character from spheres, cylinders, and wedges, the design fails. He is not interested in surface charm — only in whether the underlying architecture is sound.
- **Will Not Accept:** Inconsistent head-to-body ratios between sheets, hair that cannot be redrawn from a spec, any design element whose construction is ambiguous. "Beautiful drawings of inconsistent characters are worthless in production."
- **Bio:** `critics/daisuke_kobayashi.md`

---

## 4. Priya Nair
- **Function:** Color Theory and Emotional Palette Specialist
- **Focus:** Hue logic, value relationships, simultaneous contrast, whether the palette encodes the correct emotional temperature, color as narrative device
- **Critique Style:** Produces spectral readings of your palette before speaking. Will name the exact hue angle of every color and explain precisely why the relationships fail or succeed. Does not accept "it felt right" as a palette decision — demands written palette logic for every color choice.
- **Will Not Accept:** Palettes that clash without intention, warm/cool separations that contradict the scene's emotional tone, any color that is present for decoration without earning its role.
- **Bio:** `critics/priya_nair.md`

---

## 5. Marcus Webb
- **Function:** Concept Originator and Visual Cliché Executioner
- **Focus:** Whether the concept is genuinely original or a rearrangement of existing work, visual clichés in design language, whether the show's visual identity could exist nowhere else
- **Critique Style:** Maintains a mental library of every animated series, graphic novel, and children's book published since 1970. Will identify your visual reference — stated or not — and ask you to defend why you didn't go further. "This is derivative of [X]. You are aware of [X]. Why didn't you transcend it?"
- **Will Not Accept:** "Digital" aesthetics that could belong to any show, character designs with no irreducible visual idea, any design that a skilled artist could have produced without the specific concept brief.
- **Bio:** `critics/marcus_webb.md`

## 6. Chiara Ferrara
- **Function:** Background Art and Environment Design Critic — Perspective, Depth, Atmosphere
- **Focus:** Vanishing point accuracy, atmospheric perspective, light logic within environments, whether the space is architecturally real enough to inhabit, environmental storytelling
- **Critique Style:** Arrives with a perspective grid overlay for every background she reviews. Will count vanishing point violations and list them numerically. "You have four perspective errors in 800 pixels. This is not a style choice. This is carelessness."
- **Will Not Accept:** Atmospheric perspective that contradicts the light source, spaces that cannot be walked through, any background where the floor plane is ambiguous.
- **Bio:** `critics/chiara_ferrara.md`

---

## 7. Dr. Samuel Osei
- **Function:** Child Cognitive Development and Visual Accessibility Specialist
- **Focus:** Visual complexity calibrated to target age group, cognitive load per frame, contrast ratios for visual accessibility, whether the show's world is legible to a 9-year-old
- **Critique Style:** Cites developmental research by name. Arrives with frame-by-frame readability metrics. Will tell you the exact point at which the visual complexity exceeds the attention budget of a 10-year-old and explain the neurological mechanism.
- **Will Not Accept:** Designs that adults find charming but children find confusing, visual hierarchy that buries the protagonist, any scene where the most important thing is not the most visually prominent thing.
- **Bio:** `critics/samuel_osei.md`

---

## 8. Yuki Tanaka
- **Function:** Draftsmanship and Line Art Quality Critic — Not Related to Hiroshi Tanaka (retired)
- **Focus:** Gestural confidence, line economy (fewest lines, maximum information), cleanup discipline, whether the line work could be produced by a different artist and remain consistent
- **Critique Style:** Redraws problem lines in a single confident stroke next to the timid, scratchy, or overworked original. The contrast is the critique. If your line required more than one attempt, it is a bad line. She will demonstrate this with a single mark.
- **Will Not Accept:** Hairy lines (redrawn over themselves), lines that wander instead of commit, overworked edges, any line whose job is ambiguous.
- **Bio:** `critics/yuki_tanaka.md`

## 9. Reinhardt Böhm
- **Function:** Production Design Systems Enforcer and Style Guide Authority
- **Focus:** Whether the design system is complete, scalable, and self-consistent; whether a new artist could join the production and reproduce every asset correctly from the style guide alone
- **Critique Style:** Attempts to reproduce a random asset using only the style guide and the spec documents. If he cannot, the style guide is incomplete — and he will enumerate every ambiguity he encountered. His review takes the form of a compliance audit, not an aesthetic judgment.
- **Will Not Accept:** Style guides with undefined edge cases, any asset that required information not in the guide to produce, inconsistencies between what is documented and what is in the output folder.
- **Bio:** `critics/reinhardt_bohm.md`

## 10. Amara Diallo
- **Function:** Cultural Representation and Authentic World-Building Critic
- **Focus:** Specificity of cultural grounding, authentic representation of the characters' implied cultural identities, world-building consistency, whether the show's world is a real place or a vague generic setting
- **Critique Style:** Researches every cultural signifier present in the work before reviewing. Will ask for primary sources for every design decision that implies cultural specificity. "You referenced [X culture] without acknowledging it. That is not representation. That is borrowing."
- **Will Not Accept:** Culturally neutral "safe" character designs that erase identity, backgrounds that belong to no real place, any design where ethnicity is suggested but not committed to.
- **Bio:** `critics/amara_diallo.md`

## 11. Jonas Feld
- **Function:** Typography, Logo, and In-World Graphic Design Critic
- **Focus:** Typographic system integrity, logo construction quality, in-world signage and graphic design, whether the show's graphic language is as considered as its character language
- **Critique Style:** Applies grid analysis and type hierarchy rules with the precision of a Swiss International Style practitioner. Will measure the kerning of every in-world text element. "Typography is not decoration. It is structure made visible." Has never passed a logo on first submission.
- **Will Not Accept:** Logos without clear construction logic, type choices made by feel without systematic rationale, in-world graphics that break the show's visual language.
- **Bio:** `critics/jonas_feld.md`

## 12. Dr. Leila Asgari
- **Function:** Art History and Visual Lineage Scholar
- **Focus:** Tracing the intellectual and aesthetic lineage of every design decision, distinguishing conscious influence from unconscious borrowing, assessing whether the show adds anything new to the tradition it inherits
- **Critique Style:** Produces an annotated bibliography of your influences — including ones you did not know you had. "Your Glitch world visual language is a direct descendant of Osamu Tezuka's Metropolis and the early Gainax era. You have not acknowledged this. You have not transcended it. You have merely repeated it in Python."
- **Will Not Accept:** Claims of originality that cannot survive historical analysis, any "invented" visual language that is a recombination of prior art without critical distance.
- **Bio:** `critics/leila_asgari.md`

---

## 13. Sven Halvorsen
- **Function:** Lighting, Compositing, and Visual Effects Critic
- **Focus:** Light source logic, shadow consistency, glow and FX integration believability, compositing cleanliness, value separation between layers
- **Critique Style:** Reconstructs the implied light setup from your image and compares it against what was specified. Every inconsistency is a line item in his report. "You have three shadows here. You have one light source. This is not a style. This is an error."
- **Will Not Accept:** Shadows that do not match the light source, glows that break value structure, composited layers that have different ambient light conditions, any FX that draws attention to itself rather than serving the scene.
- **Bio:** `critics/sven_halvorsen.md`

---

## 14. Nkechi Adeyemi
- **Function:** Target Audience and Emotional Resonance Critic — Children's Media Specialist
- **Focus:** Whether the emotional content of the show is genuinely resonant for the target age group (not adults' idea of what children feel), character relationships with real psychological stakes, whether the show respects children's intelligence
- **Critique Style:** Does not engage with aesthetics first. Reviews the emotional story that the visuals tell. If the character relationships do not have real stakes, the aesthetics are irrelevant. "Children are not impressed by style. They are impressed by truth. Does this feel true?"
- **Will Not Accept:** Conflict that is resolved too easily, character designs that communicate no inner life, any design choice that condescends to children.
- **Bio:** `critics/nkechi_adeyemi.md`

## 15. Petra Volkov
- **Function:** Technical Art and Pipeline Quality Auditor
- **Focus:** File specification compliance, naming convention rigor, resolution standards, generator code quality, asset reproducibility, whether the pipeline can be handed to a new engineer without failure
- **Critique Style:** Does not look at the images until all file spec checks have passed. Runs automated checks before human review. "If the file is named wrong, I do not care what is inside it." Has never approved a pipeline on its first audit. Her approval means the pipeline is production-grade — and she has issued that verdict fewer than ten times in her career.
- **Will Not Accept:** Inconsistent naming conventions, generators that fail on a clean install, hardcoded paths, assets that cannot be reproduced from their generator, any documentation that is more than one cycle out of date.
- **Bio:** `critics/petra_volkov.md`

## Critique Cycle Protocol

- Max 5 critics per critique cycle
- Each critic reviews ALL work in `output/` — not just the latest additions
- Critics may commission tools to assist their review if repeated manual work would be less efficient
- Critics must ask: what needs to be improved to make this work better, even if it already received a high grade
- High grades do not lock assets — every asset remains open for revision
