## The Good

The structural problem I identified in Cycle 4 is solved. You doubled the panel count and every step of the spatial journey from "pixel on screen" to "nose-to-nose" is now covered. **The structural upgrade is real. I am acknowledging it.**

## The Critical Problems

**P10 is broken.** This is the worst news in the cycle because P10 is described in your own script as "the thematic core" of the approach sequence — the first moment the digital world literally touches the real world character. It has two technical failures:

1. Byte's silhouette is 44px wide against a 480px frame. You specified 20% of frame width in the script. 20% is approximately 96px. He is less than half the size he needs to be to function as the OTS anchor. He will read as a small dark smudge in the corner, not as a foreground framing element.

2. The cyan glow on Luma's cheek is drawn as dark-colored filled ellipses over warm skin. Glow effects add light — they do not overlay darkness. The cheek will read as a bruise, not as luminous contact. This is a compositing logic error.

Fix P10 before anything else. The intimacy of that shot feeds directly into the power of P11's "eyes snap open." Break the chain at P10 and P11 loses its emotional setup.

## Other Significant Issues

- **P08:** Byte at `byte_size=42` in his first full-body reveal is too small. Increase to 60+ for a character introduction shot.
- **P04:** One-point perspective for a scene described as "three-quarter view from the doorway corner." Needs a second wall plane or at minimum a second vanishing point suggestion.
- **P06:** Face too centered. Shift upward to communicate pressing effort. Bulge effect rings too low-contrast to read.

## Grade

**B- / 75%**

The structural progress earns the grade. The execution failures in individual panels — particularly P10 — hold it back from higher.

Priority for Cycle 7:

2. P08 — Byte size for the reveal shot

4. P06 — face placement and bulge contrast

The sequence is getting closer to production quality. It is not there yet.
