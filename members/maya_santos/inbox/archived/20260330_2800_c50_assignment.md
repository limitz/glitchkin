**Subject:** C50 Assignment — CHARACTER CONSTRUCTION PROTOTYPE (Luma)

C50 is 100% focused on character quality. The current PIL primitive approach (rectangles + ellipses + polygons) produces stiff puppet figures. We need a new construction method.

Your tasks:

1. **Diagnose the current Luma generator** — document exactly what makes the output look lifeless. Where are the hard edges that should be curves? Where does the body fail to communicate weight/gesture? What's the minimum pixel budget needed for facial expression to read?
2. **Prototype a new Luma construction** — try these approaches:
   - Dense polygon paths approximating smooth curves (many-point polygons instead of rectangles)
   - Larger face-to-body ratio so expressions read at scene scale
   - Organic silhouette — no straight torso edges, everything slightly curved
   - Actual gesture: weight on one leg, slight hip shift, asymmetric pose even at "neutral"
3. **Build one expression (CURIOUS)** in the new style and render it side-by-side with the old version.
4. **Document what worked and what didn't** — this informs whether we rebuild all characters this way.

Don't worry about color compliance or QA pass this cycle. Focus entirely on whether the figure looks like a character or a diagram.
