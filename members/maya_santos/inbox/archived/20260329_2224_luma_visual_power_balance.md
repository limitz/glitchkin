## The Issue

Critique 15: Zoe Park (age 11) said "Byte is the best thing in this pitch." Luma is currently losing to her own supporting character for visual dominance on screen.

This is not a failure of Byte's design — Byte is strong. It's a gap in Luma's design. Luma needs more reckless energy and visual assertiveness to own the protagonist role.

## Direction: What Luma Needs

**In expression sheets and character work:**

1. **Reckless physicality** — Luma's reckless energy must be more physically expressed. In THE NOTICING (v011), in DETERMINED, in CURIOUS — her body should always be forward, off-balance, doing something. She leans into things. She commits too hard. A protagonist who leads with her body before her brain catches up.

2. **Line weight contrast** — In panels where Luma and Byte appear together (lineup, style frames), Luma's silhouette outline should read as slightly heavier (1px more) than Byte's. She is the protagonist. Her visual mass must anchor the frame.

3. **THE NOTICING (Luma v011 work)** — Reinforce this with your existing C38 fixes:
   - Right-eye lid fix: top lid DROPS (focusing squint) not bottom lid rises (wince). This was already in your C38 queue.
   - After the lid fix, THE NOTICING should read as: "she's LOCKED ON" not "she's in pain."
   - Add: a very slight chin-forward thrust (jaw_fwd +HR*0.02) — the instinct to get closer to the thing she can't quite believe.

4. **DETERMINED pose** — when she's DETERMINED, Luma leans forward at the hips (not just a head tilt). Her weight is on her front foot. It's the posture of someone about to start running.

## What NOT to do

- Do not reduce Byte's visual presence. He is excellent.
- Do not make Luma "cute" — her reckless energy is what makes her the protagonist. Cute is the wrong direction.
- Do not change Luma's color palette or proportions.
