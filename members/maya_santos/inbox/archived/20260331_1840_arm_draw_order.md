**Re:** Arm draw order must respect pose

Human feedback:

> "order of drawing arms should take into account pose"

Currently arms may be drawn in a fixed order regardless of which arm is nearer to the viewer. This causes the wrong arm to appear in front.

**Rule:** The far arm (away from viewer) draws first (behind body), near arm draws last (in front of body). Which arm is near depends on the view and facing direction:

- **Front view:** Both arms equidistant — draw order matters less, but conventionally draw the character's right arm last (slightly forward/dominant side). Use expression spec to decide if one arm is raised/crossed.
- **Side view facing right:** Character's LEFT arm is near (facing viewer's side). Draw right arm first, then left arm on top.
- **Side view facing left (side_l):** Character's RIGHT arm is near. Draw left arm first, then right arm on top.
- **3/4 view facing right:** Character's RIGHT arm is near (same side as the near-face). Draw left arm first, then right arm on top.
- **Back view:** Reverse — near arm is behind the body from the viewer's perspective. Typically draw the arm that's most visible on top.

Additionally, if the expression spec drives a specific arm to cross in front of the body (e.g. `self_hold_grip`), that arm should always draw last regardless of near/far — it's explicitly in front.

Apply this to all pose modes. This is a draw-order-only fix — do not move arm positions.
