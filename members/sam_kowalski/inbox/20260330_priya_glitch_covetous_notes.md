## What Is Glitch Coveting?

Glitch is coveting **access** — specifically, the breach point that Luma represents.

More precisely: Glitch covets what Luma can do that Glitch cannot. Luma can move freely between the Real World and the Glitch Layer. She is a living bridge. Glitch, by contrast, is trapped — its compliance with the Corruption has given it power inside the Layer, but it cannot breach to the Real World without a CRT anchor (same rule as all Glitchkin). The CRT in Grandma Miri's house is the only stable breach point in Season 1.

What Glitch COVETOUS reads as, underneath the target-lock acid slit eyes: *I could have that. I should take that.* It is appetitive, not angry. There is no hatred in COVETOUS. There is only want, focused to a point.

Season 1 arc: Glitch's goal is to use the CRT breach as a permanent Corruption portal. The COVETOUS state is visible when it is assessing that path — seeing Luma and seeing the breach at the same time.

Subtext (for color): Glitch was once a community Glitchkin, the same as Byte. It surrendered connection for power. What it covets may also be Luma's freedom — she moves between worlds and belongs fully to neither, the same way Glitch once belonged to the Layer community before the Corruption reorganized it. The color spec doesn't need to surface this directly, but it is the emotional depth behind the expression.

## Where This Beat Falls in Episode Structure

COVETOUS first appears in **Episode 5**, during Glitch's first reveal.

Beat sequence (from LTG_glitch_appearance_guide.md §2.2):
1. Glitch is found, not introduced — it was already present in an organized section of a familiar Glitch Layer location.
2. Glitch starts in **NEUTRAL** (operational, watching).
3. Glitch registers Luma. Expression shifts: **CALCULATING → COVETOUS** (bilateral acid slit eyes — this is a genuine interior state, not a performance).
4. The lean forward is physical: tilt +12°, arms slightly raised, forward-reaching body posture.
5. Byte positions between them. Glitch observes and does not pursue.

The COVETOUS beat is **the first crack in Glitch's control** — the first time it shows something it did not intend to show. It is a character revelation moment, not a confrontation.

---

## Is This the First Time the Audience Sees Glitch?

**Yes.** Glitch does not appear before Episode 5. Episodes 1–4 show only ambient Corruption signatures (geometry too organized, too precise — nothing named).

The Episode 5 first-appearance is the COVETOUS reveal. This is significant for your color spec: the COVETOUS state is doing double duty as **debut expression**. The audience meets Glitch for the first time in an interior state. They are let inside immediately — they see the want before they see the performance. This is the design choice Jayden Torres responded to (C16 critique).

---

## Staging — Is Glitch Alone, or Is There a Character Visible?

**Glitch is not alone.** The COVETOUS beat explicitly includes:
- **Luma** — visible in frame, Glitch oriented toward her (lean +12°)
- **Byte** — positions between them immediately after the COVETOUS shift

The color spec should account for this triangulation: Glitch's COVETOUS lean is directed at Luma (or Luma + CRT screen as combined target). Byte is in the intervening space. The spatial arrangement is: Glitch at distance, Byte as barrier, Luma as subject of want.

One additional note from the appearance guide: Glitch does NOT pursue in this beat. The COVETOUS state is **watching and wanting**, not acting. The action comes later. This gives the color frame a held, suspended quality — tension without release.
